MARKET OUTLOOK

The single biggest equity overhang — Iran — has been removed, and the calendar is sparse between now and CQ2 earnings season (first busy day: July 14), giving bulls a clean runway. The Iran MOU opens the door to an oil supply glut scenario the IEA is already flagging for 2027, which is structurally disinflationary and supportive for risk assets. Sidelined cash watching the SPX rally is going to get increasingly uncomfortable, and that FOMO dynamic is real. That said, bears have a point: inflation is still above target globally, the ECB is explicitly warning the MOU isn't enough to stop tightening, and the AI spending cycle is showing early signs of demand-side stress at the enterprise level. Net-net, momentum favors the bulls through month-end, but position sizing matters.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US-Iran MOU signing ceremony is set for Friday in Switzerland — largely a formality, with Bloomberg publishing a 14-point draft MOU consistent with weeks of press reporting. Iran will be permitted to immediately sell oil and could gain access to a $300B development fund (Bloomberg).

- The IEA's June monthly report warns of a potential oil supply glut in 2027, with supply projected to surge ~8M BPD vs. demand rising only 2M BPD (IEA). Brent was ~$93 before Trump's Iran signals last Thursday; it's now ~$79.20.

- UAE is moving to reduce its Strait of Hormuz dependency to zero regardless of whether the waterway fully reopens (Bloomberg).

- European nations are working toward a grand bargain with Washington: EU support for the Iran deal in exchange for increased US pressure on Putin over Ukraine (Politico).

- DeepSeek, CXMT, and 100+ Chinese firms are being held off the US blacklist as the White House avoids escalating tensions with Beijing (Reuters).

- Sweden's Riksbank held rates steady but flagged rising odds of a hike later this year, citing supply-driven inflationary pressures (Riksbank). ECB officials similarly warned the Iran MOU alone won't prevent further tightening (Bloomberg).

- UK May CPI came in at +2.8%, flat vs. April and below the Street's +3.0% forecast (Reuters).

COMPANY NEWS

INTC announced its 18A-P process — the first performance enhancement in the Intel 18A family — has entered risk production on schedule.
