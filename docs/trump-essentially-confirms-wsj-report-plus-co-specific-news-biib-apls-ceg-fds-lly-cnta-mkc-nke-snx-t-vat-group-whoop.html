COMPANY NEWS

BIIB will acquire APLS for $41 per share cash plus a CVR worth up to $2 per share (total ~$5.6B), representing a massive premium to APLS's Monday close of $17.09. The deal is expected to be increasingly accretive to Biogen's non-GAAP EPS starting in 2027 and meaningfully boost the company's EPS CAGR through decade-end.

LLY is buying CNTA for $38 per share cash (~$6.3B) plus a CVR worth up to $9 per share for a total potential value of $47, well above CNTA's Monday close of $27.58. Centessa is developing treatments for excessive daytime sleepiness and other neurological conditions.

MKC reported solid Q1 results with EPS of 66¢ versus Street expectations of 59¢, driven by revenue of $1.87B (vs. $1.79B expected). While organic growth of 1.2% fell slightly short of the 1.4% estimate due to Flavor Solutions pressure, gross margins expanded 100 basis points. The lack of a guidance bump despite the beat might disappoint some, though multiple reports suggest a Unilever deal announcement is imminent.

NKE insists it won't sell Converse, but Authentic Brands has expressed interest in buying the struggling business (Bloomberg). Given Converse's brutal performance (consensus models -25% revenue decline in F26 to $1.26B following -19% in F25), investors would welcome a divestiture even if it doesn't bring significant proceeds.

CEG is targeting base EPS CAGR of 20%+ from 2026-2029 and increased its buyback authorization to $5B. For 2026, the company sees EPS of $11-12 with a midpoint of $11.50, slightly below consensus of $11.72.

FDS delivered modest Q2 EPS upside at $4.46 versus $4.37 expected, driven by 6.8% organic revenue growth and robust 6.7% organic ASV growth. Operating margins fell 230 basis points year-over-year to 35%, missing the 35.5% estimate, but management raised full-year EPS guidance to $17.25-17.75 from the prior $16.90-17.60 range.

SNX crushed Q1 expectations with EPS of $4.73 versus $3.29 expected, powered by sales of $17.16B (vs. $15.55B expected) and margin expansion. Q2 guidance midpoints are well ahead of Street estimates with EPS of $4 versus $3.45 expected.

T reiterated all 2026 and multi-year financial guidance plus capital return plans from its Q4 2025 earnings report.

VAT Group, the Swiss semiconductor supplier, issued mixed guidance with Q1 revenue tracking at CHF215MM versus prior guidance of CHF240-260MM due to Middle East supply chain disruptions, though underlying demand remains strong with Q1 book-to-bill at a robust 1.6x.

Whoop raised $575M at a $10.1B valuation, up dramatically from its $3.6B valuation in 2021, as the wearables company heads toward an IPO (Bloomberg).
