Vital Mid-Day Market Update for Tuesday March 31, 2026

MARKETS

SPX +1.15%, Nasdaq +1.55%, Dow +345 points/0.77%, R2K +33 points/1.37%. Brent up ~30 basis points to ~$107.75, gold advances ~200 basis points, silver climbs 450 basis points. Bitcoin about flat. DXY dipping 40 basis points.

RATES & FED

Treasury yields are flat to down 4 basis points on the day. The market is pricing in 7 basis points worth of Fed rate cuts for the remainder of this year, reflecting ongoing dovish sentiment despite geopolitical uncertainties.

MARKET OUTLOOK

Stocks are seeing decent gains largely because of a WSJ report (which Trump effectively confirmed via social media) signaling the administration could wind down the Iran war relatively soon without securing Hormuz. This suggests lower odds of a sustained ground campaign, which had been a major market fear—not so much the campaign itself, but Iran's likely retaliation by escalating attacks on critical regional economic infrastructure. Of course, nothing is guaranteed and the Iran situation remains extremely fluid. The equal-weight SPX is lagging the cap-weighted index, suggesting this rally is concentrated in mega-caps rather than broad-based.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Trump tells aides he's willing to walk away from the Iran war without reopening Hormuz, as seizing control would mean an extended military campaign beyond the administration's 4-6 week timeline (WSJ)

• Iran's remaining government is struggling to make decisions following ongoing war and assassination of senior leaders, though security services and military continue functioning while hardline IRGC elements assert themselves (NYT)

• Gulf allies are pressing the White House to continue fighting until Iran is decisively defeated (AP)

• Iran is pushing Houthis to resume Red Sea shipping attacks should the US escalate further (Bloomberg)
