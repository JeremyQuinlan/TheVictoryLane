MARKET OUTLOOK

The dominant theme heading into Thursday is a pro-chips/components vs. anti-hyperscaler rotation triggered by Google's raised capex guide — that trade is immediate and real. Non-tech earnings were broadly encouraging (CSX, KNX, LUV, URI, WCN all delivered), which keeps the cyclical recovery narrative intact. Legacy software remains the ugliest corner of the market; NOW's solid beat buys some breathing room but doesn't lift the cloud hanging over the space after IBM and PEGA. Tesla trades on faith, not fundamentals, and Musk talking down Optimus on the call is the kind of sentiment shock that lingers.

COMPANY NEWS

GOOGL reported Q2 revenue of $119.7B (+24%), with Cloud a blowout at $24.7B vs. the Street's $22.5B and Cloud operating income of $8.8B vs. $6.9B expected. Search was inline at $63.2B and YouTube ads slightly beat at $11.05B vs. $10.8B. Operating income was roughly inline at $40.77B but op. margins of 34% missed the Street's 34.75% estimate. Free cash flow was a jarring -$6B vs. the Street's +$2.4B on softer operating cash flow. The full-year capex guide was raised to $195-205B from $180-190B, with management calling 2027 "significant." Gemini now processes 22B API tokens per minute with 950M monthly active users.

NOW delivered a clean beat across every major KPI — 23% FXN subscription software growth (vs. Street 21.85%), 21.5% cRPO growth (vs. Street 19.5%), and 29.5% op. margins (vs. Street 27%). Full-year sub revenue guide nudged to +21% from +20.5-21%. Agentic deployments of ServiceNow AI increased ninefold in nine months. Relief, not euphoria — legacy software stays under scrutiny for several more quarters.

TSLA reported Q2 revenue of $28.23B (+26%, vs. Street $26.3B), but margin compression was severe: GAAP gross margins -31 basis points, operating margins -269 basis points, EBITDA margins -353 basis points, driving a large EPS miss at 33c vs. the Street's 51c. Free cash flow was -$1.09B (better than the feared -$3.6B). Capex spiked 142% Y/Y to $5.79B. On the call, Musk tempered Optimus expectations, calling the ramp "very difficult." FSD active subs jumped 56% Y/Y but remain a small line item.

TXN reported Q2 EPS of $2.09 ex-items vs. Street $1.93, on revenue of $5.46B (+23%, vs. Street $5.24B) with broad growth across industrial, data center, and auto. Q3 guidance mid-points are ahead of consensus: EPS $2.40 vs. Street $2.17, revenue $5.9B vs. Street $5.61B. The stock's pain is purely an expectations problem.

IBM cut its full-year revenue guide to +4-5% FXN from >5% following its earlier preannouncement, but reiterated FCF growth of ~$1B Y/Y. The stock stays in the penalty box.

LUV delivered a massive Q2 EPS beat at 94c vs. Street 52c, with full-year EPS guidance mid-point of $3.75 vs. Street $3.12, despite higher fuel costs. Record revenue and margin expansion signal turnaround traction.

LVS missed on both revenue ($3.15B vs. Street $3.3B) and adjusted property EBITDA ($1.11B vs. Street $1.3B), hurt by unusually low hold in Macau rolling play. The company repurchased $787M in stock and boosted its buyback authorization to $6B (~20% of market cap).

URI reported Q2 revenue of $4.41B vs. Street $4.21B and EBITDA of $2.056B vs. Street $1.91B. Full-year EBITDA guide raised to $7.975-8.125B from $7.625-7.875B.

CSX posted Q2 EPS of 54c (+23%, vs. Street 52c) on revenue of $3.94B (+10%). Operating margins expanded 240 basis points to 38.3%. Full-year revenue guide raised to +5-9% from +5%, with op. margin expansion now seen at >350 basis points vs. prior 275-300 basis points.

KNX beat Q2 EPS at 63c vs. Street 51c, with adjusted operating ratio improving 240 basis points. Q3 EPS guide of 71-77c brackets the Street at 71c. Truckload market tightening is accelerating.

AVB posted Q2 FFO of $2.86 vs. Street $2.81 and nudged full-year NOI guidance higher.

EQR reported Q2 FFO of $1.02 vs. Street $1.01 and raised full-year guidance, driven by San Francisco momentum and improving bad debt.

SLG reported Q2 FFO of $1.43 vs. Street $1.21 and dramatically raised its full-year FFO guide to $5.60-5.90 from $4.40-4.70. Occupancy ticked up 30 basis points Q/Q to 94.7%.

RJF reported Q2 EPS of $3.14 vs. Street $2.93, with revenue roughly inline; a lower tax rate provided the tailwind.

MOH beat on adjusted EPS and raised full-year guidance, but Marketplace MCR came in worse than anticipated, offsetting Medicare improvement.

MEDP reported Q2 revenue of $707.3M vs. Street $689.2M and EBITDA of $153.4M vs. Street $147M. Full-year EBITDA guide raised to $618-642M from $605-635M.

WCN beat Q2 EBITDA at $840M vs. Street $830M and raised full-year guidance.

CCI reported AFFO per share of $1.13 vs. Street $1.05; full-year guidance essentially unchanged.

SON posted Q2 EPS of $1.51 vs. Street $1.47; full-year guidance reaffirmed.

ROL missed Q2 EPS at 32c vs. Street 34c on weaker residential pest control volumes tied to declining digital/inbound lead generation.

PAG received a go-private bid at $210/share cash from Penske Corp. and Mitsui, who already own ~72.6% of the company. PAG closed Wednesday at $214.57.

LESL is exploring options including a potential Chapter 11 bankruptcy filing to address its debt load (Bloomberg).
