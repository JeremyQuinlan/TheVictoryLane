RATES & FED

Treasuries sold off with yields rising 2–5 basis points across the curve as Brent broke $100 on Middle East escalation. Fed expectations shifted hawkish — markets now pricing roughly 44 basis points of H2 rate hikes, with ~40% odds of a move at the July 29 meeting. Falling jobless claims alongside spiking oil are giving the hawks plenty of ammunition.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The equal-weight S&P holding up far better than cap-weighted tells you exactly what the tape is saying: this was a tech-driven selloff, not a macro panic — yet. The bearish case on mega-cap tech is getting harder to dismiss: Google and Tesla both printed negative free cash flow, capex guidance keeps ratcheting higher, and Google essentially told the market it views third-party compute as a bridge, not a long-term commitment — a direct threat to neocloud valuations. Oil at $100 with both Hormuz and the Red Sea under pressure is a genuine inflation shock, and the Fed meeting next week is now a live wildcard. Bulls have cover in industrials, defense, and rails, but until tech stabilizes, the path of least resistance is lower.

COMPANY NEWS

GOOGL reported Q2 revenue of $119.7B (+24%), beating estimates of $117B, but op. margins of 34% missed the Street's 34.75% target, and the full-year capex guide was raised again to $195–205B from $180–190B. Free cash flow was negative. Management flagged that third-party compute is only a "bridging strategy," spooking neocloud investors.

TSLA posted Q2 revenue of $28.23B (+26%, beat vs. $26.3B expected) but margins collapsed — GAAP gross margins -31 basis points, op. margins -269 basis points — driving a large EPS miss of 33c vs. 51c expected. Musk talked down Optimus expectations on the call. Free cash flow was negative.

LMT crushed Q2 estimates with EPS of $7.94 vs. $7.13 expected and revenue of $20.1B vs. $19.3B. Free cash flow was ~$2.9B, roughly double consensus, book-to-bill came in at ~3.2x, and full-year EPS guidance was raised to $29.95–30.65.

RTX reported Q2 EPS of $1.89 (+21%) vs. $1.66 expected, with organic sales +16% to $24.7B across all three units. Backlog grew to $289B from $271B in Q1, and full-year EPS guidance was raised to $7.10–7.25.

NOW delivered 23% FXN subscription software growth vs. 21.85% expected and cRPO growth of 21.5% vs. 19.5%, offering a much-needed counterpoint to the gloom around legacy software.

CSX beat Q2 EPS by 2 cents (54c vs. 52c) on +10% revenue to $3.94B, with operating margins expanding 240 basis points to 38.3%. Full-year guide raised.

NSC posted Q2 EPS of $3.52 vs. $3.29 expected on revenue of $3.46B, with op. ratio improving ~60 basis points to 65.5%.

BX reported distributable EPS of $1.52 vs. $1.33 expected, with inflows of $68.3B vs. $54B expected. Total AUM reached $1.34T.

TMO beat Q2 EPS ($6.03 vs. $5.72) and sales ($11.99B vs. $11.7B) with 5% organic growth, ~200 basis points above consensus.

ACI badly missed FQ1 EPS (42c vs. 54c expected) with comps of -0.8%, and slashed full-year guidance.

CMCSA posted Q2 pro forma revenue of $29.56B vs. $29.3B expected and free cash flow of $4.6B, roughly $1B above plan, though the company flagged softening theme park attendance since June.

MSFT is replacing OpenAI models with its own in PowerPoint and Bing (Bloomberg).

Nestle missed H2 margin expectations and sold a 50% stake in its Waters & Beverages unit to Platinum Equity for €3.4B (WSJ).
