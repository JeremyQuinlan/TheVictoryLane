COMPANY NEWS

MKC (McCormick) formally announced its combination with Unilever's food business in a deal valued at approximately $44.8 billion enterprise value, representing 13.8x fiscal year 2025 EBITDA. Unilever shareholders will receive 65% of the equity in the new unit (55.1% for Unilever holders, 9.9% for Unilever itself) plus $15.7 billion in cash. The combined entity targets 3-5% growth in year three, expects $600 million in annual run-rate cost synergies, and anticipates operating margins of 23-25% by the third year post-closing. The transaction will be structured as a Reverse Morris Trust, avoiding U.S. federal income tax implications for Unilever and its shareholders. This deal brings week-to-date M&A activity to approximately $90 billion (including recent deals from SYY-Jetro, BIIB-APLS, and LLY-CNTA). The food-focused staples space remains unloved by investors given both cyclical and secular headwinds, and the industry's track record with large-scale M&A remains questionable.
