RATES & FED

Treasuries sold off with yields rising 2–5 basis points across the curve. Fed expectations shifted hawkish, with markets now pricing 43 basis points of hikes in H2 and roughly 33% odds of a move at the July 29 meeting.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The market is stuck in a tug-of-war between an AI capex boom that shows no signs of slowing and a geopolitical backdrop that keeps getting worse — and right now the bears have the stronger argument on momentum. Brent pushing toward $100, a hawkish Fed reprice, and circular AI financing deals are not a cocktail that supports multiple expansion. Q2 earnings are coming in solid, but investors loaded up on optimism heading in, so the bar for upside surprise is meaningfully higher than Q1. The software complex is flashing a real warning sign after PEGA echoed IBM's AI-disruption thesis — legacy software could face a prolonged demand air pocket. Watch Wednesday night's GOOGL, TSLA, and NOW prints closely; they'll set the tone for the back half of earnings season.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Secretary Rubio said Iran "is not serious about talks," with no diplomatic off-ramp visible (CNBC). Iran's interior minister visited Pakistan but the meeting produced no de-escalation signals (Bloomberg).

- Iran's ballistic missile capabilities are proving far more formidable than previously assumed, with US and Israeli airstrikes failing to neutralize them (WSJ).

- Houthis have positioned missiles and drones near the Bab el-Mandeb strait and are prepared to attack Red Sea shipping — a potential naval blockade of Saudi Arabia would dramatically tighten energy corridors (Bloomberg).

- QatarEnergy is preparing to extend its LNG force majeure into October, further squeezing natural gas markets; European gas prices are approaching post-Iran-war highs (Bloomberg, FT).

- The White House approved a sweeping civilian nuclear deal with Saudi Arabia, including a potential path to uranium enrichment (WSJ).

- Trump announced 100% tariffs on generic drugs starting August 2028, rising to 200% in 2029; branded/patented drug policy is unchanged (Bloomberg).

- Trump appointed Adam Candeub, a known Big Tech critic, as DOJ antitrust chief (FT).

- The BOJ signaled openness to a faster rate-hike pace as yen weakness fuels inflation risk (Bloomberg).

COMPANY NEWS

AMD signed a 2GW AI chip supply deal with Anthropic but is injecting $5B into the company and may backstop Anthropic's data center leases — the circular financing concern is real and the market noticed (WSJ).

SMCI preannounced FQ4 revenue near the low end of $11–12.5B guidance, but gross margins are tracking ~16% (roughly double consensus), and orders were a staggering >$60B, implying a book-to-bill of ~5.5x.

GEV posted Q2 EBITDA of $1.25B (vs. Street $1.29B) but revenue of $11.1B beat ($10.8B est.), free cash flow crushed at $5.1B vs. $1.3B expected, orders surged +88% organic to $24.2B, and the full-year sales guide was raised to $45.5–46.5B. The stock still underperformed — expectations are that elevated.

PEGA missed FQ2 EPS (35c vs. 44c est.) and warned that "unprecedented AI changes" are causing CTOs to delay legacy software purchases — a direct echo of IBM's message last week, and a red flag for the entire software complex.

CME reported Q2 sales of $1.7B (vs. $1.67B est.) and EPS of $2.99 (vs. $2.90 est.).

MCO delivered Q2 EPS of $4.68 (vs. $4.28 est.) on revenue of $2.185B (vs. $2.086B est.), with MIS revenue +25%, operating margins expanding 440 basis points to 55.3%, and full-year EPS guidance nudged to $16.50–17 with buyback raised to $3B.

T beat on Q2 EBITDA ($12.3B vs. $12.1B est.) and free cash flow ($4.7B vs. $4.4B est.), posted postpaid phone net adds of +432K (vs. +325K est.) and fiber adds of +367K (vs. +303K est.), and is accelerating buybacks.

WAB beat Q2 EPS ($2.76 vs. $2.61 est.) on revenue +17.5% to $3.18B, raised full-year guidance ~$110MM on revenue and ~30c on EPS, though the 12-month backlog slipped slightly Q/Q.

PHM beat Q2 EPS ($2.48 vs. $2.36 est.) with housing GMs of 25% and solid orders +6% to 7,536 units; management flagged early signs of market stabilization.

PM posted Q2 EPS of $2.20 (vs. $2.04 est.) with smoke-free organic revenue +11.8%, though only reiterating full-year EPS guidance of $8.11–8.26 disappointed some.

CB beat Q2 EPS ($7.26 vs. $6.75 est.) on a favorable combined ratio, but net premiums written missed as management deliberately walked away from unfavorably priced business.

COF reported Q2 EPS of $5.81 (vs. $4.68 est.), with the beat driven largely by provisions ~$1B below expectations.

OTIS matched Q2 EPS at $1.01 but missed on margins (op. margins 15.2% vs. 16% expected) and cut full-year EPS guidance to $4.01–4.05 from $4.20–4.24.

IBKR beat Q2 EPS (69c vs. 63c est.) on revenue of $1.88B vs. $1.78B est., but the real headline is a 67% spike in margin debt balances to $108.5B — a noteworthy risk indicator.
