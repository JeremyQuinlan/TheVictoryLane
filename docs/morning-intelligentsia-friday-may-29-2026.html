MARKET OUTLOOK

The market is treading water as multiple crosscurrents balance out — Iran deal optimism (already largely priced in) versus lingering geopolitical risks, solid earnings momentum (particularly DELL's blowout) against emerging AI cost concerns, and cooling inflation data offset by persistent structural price pressures. While tech continues to drive the narrative, we're seeing early warning signs around "tokenmaxxing" pushback and circular financing deals that suggest the AI boom may be entering a more mature phase. The fundamental backdrop remains supportive, but investors should prepare for increased selectivity ahead.

COMPANY NEWS

DELL delivered perhaps the strongest earnings beat of the season with Q1 EPS of $4.86 versus Street expectations of $2.99, driven by massive 88% sales growth to $43.4B and operating margins hitting 20-year lows at 8.4%. The company raised full-year guidance substantially, though management acknowledged some upside came from demand pull-forward ahead of component price hikes. Shares are surging 40% in premarket trading.

AEO posted mixed results with aggregate comps falling short due to weakness in the AE brand (-2% vs. Street +2.7%), though Aerie performed well with +25% comps. Management cited "continued consumer and macroeconomic uncertainty" while only reiterating full-year guidance.

GAP reported inline EPS of 38¢ but disappointed on comps at +2% (100 basis points light), with weakness across Banana Republic, Old Navy, and Athleta. The company trimmed full-year sales guidance to up 1-2% from prior 2-3%, though the core Gap brand showed strength with +10% comps.

COST delivered essentially inline results with membership fees ahead at $1.373B versus Street $1.358B and EPS of $4.93 matching expectations at $4.91.

MDB reported solid Q1 upside with 25% revenue growth to $687.6MM and raised full-year EPS guidance as Atlas continues showing strength.
