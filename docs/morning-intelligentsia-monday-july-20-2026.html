RATES & FED

Treasuries are flat-to-up 1 basis point across the curve, doing essentially nothing this morning. Markets are pricing roughly 32 basis points of rate hikes this year — down from 40-45 basis points pre-CPI last week, but bouncing off the post-CPI trough of ~25 basis points. A coming methodology change in PCE compilation is expected to nudge inflation modestly lower when the new format debuts with the August reading on September 30, which could slightly soften the case for further hikes (WSJ).

MARKET OUTLOOK

The tape is holding up better than it probably should given a US-Iran conflict that is genuinely on a knife's edge, and that resilience tells you something — markets still believe a negotiated exit is the most likely outcome, and so do we. Wednesday night is the make-or-break moment of the week: Google will tell us whether hyperscaler capex conviction is cracking, and ServiceNow will either validate or dismiss IBM's warning that CTO budgets are pivoting hard to AI at the expense of legacy software. The "picks and shovels" logic for chips remains sound, but don't lose sight of the fact that OpenAI and Anthropic are the gravitational center of this entire AI capex supercycle — if their growth narrative cracks, the shockwaves hit everything. Moonshot's Kimi is a real conversation, but compute demand still looks to be rising regardless of which model wins.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US-Iran conflict escalated sharply over the weekend after at least two American servicemembers were killed in an Iranian strike on a base in Jordan and another in Iraq — the first US fatalities from Iranian fire since March. Iran has demonstrated it can evade US air defense systems and still possesses ample missile stockpiles (NYT). The Pentagon responded, risking further escalation, but the White House has shown no appetite for the troop deployments that would actually shift the battlefield.

- Secretary of State Rubio confirmed the US remains open to diplomacy despite the fighting (NYT), while Energy Secretary Wright noted Hormuz flows are running at roughly two-thirds of pre-war levels (Politico). Iran's supreme leader has rejected an MOU that was heavily skewed in its favor, and hardliners are increasingly dominating Tehran's posture, labeling negotiators as traitors (Reuters, CNN).

- The "YOLO Presidency" thesis — Trump focused on legacy rather than near-term consequences, untethered from electoral constraints — remains a tail risk worth monitoring (The Atlantic, "Regime Change" by Haberman/Swan).

- Oil markets are retightening via Hormuz disruption, removal of Iran sanctions waivers, Ukraine's strikes on Russian energy infrastructure, Red Sea risk, and a potential end to China's inventory drawdown. Countries are rushing to build alternative supply routes around Hormuz (Bloomberg). US gas prices crossed $4/gallon (Reuters).

- China is exporting ~20% fewer rare-earth magnets to the US despite the nominal trade truce (Bloomberg). Xi's state visit is still planned and the White House is not probing election-interference allegations against Beijing (CNN, Politico).

- European countries are pushing back on fresh Russia sanctions (FT). The ECB is expected to hold this week but keep a September hike on the table given oil pressure (Bloomberg).

- Trump threatened Canada with incremental tariffs over wildfire smoke blanketing US cities (FT).
