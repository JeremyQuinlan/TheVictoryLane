MARKET OUTLOOK

It's a slow, range-bound tape today, but don't mistake the calm for complacency — next week is loaded with catalysts that could reprice risk in either direction. Delta's stock shrug despite a strong Q2 print is a yellow flag: expectations coming into this earnings season are simply very high, perhaps dangerously so. The Iran tape bomb from Trump around 10:30am ET caused reflexive volatility but ultimately conveyed little incremental information — the market correctly faded it. A negotiated settlement in the Middle East remains the only credible exit, and technical talks are still alive (Bloomberg). The bigger medium-term risk is whether earnings can actually clear an already elevated bar — that answer starts arriving in earnest Tuesday morning.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Trump posted that the ceasefire is "over" but that talks are continuing — the market initially wobbled, then recovered, as the post contained little new information (the situation on the ground is unchanged). Qatar is actively working to get the US-Iran peace process back on track (NYT).

- A US official confirmed Washington remains committed to a resolution with Iran and that technical talks are ongoing (Bloomberg). Israel separately shared intelligence with the White House about a fresh Iranian plot to assassinate Trump — this comes just after Trump switched back to the old Air Force One returning from the NATO summit in Turkey as a "security precaution" (WSJ, NYT).

- Global oil supply rebounded sharply by 4.1 million barrels per day to 98.8 million in June as Hormuz flows improved, though output remains 9.4 million barrels per day below pre-war levels. Markets are expected to swing back to surplus later this year, but Middle East fighting remains a live risk (IEA).

- Russia is banning diesel exports due to domestic shortages caused by Ukrainian drone strikes on energy infrastructure — a significant supply crunch is building in the global diesel market (FT). Ukraine continues pounding Russian energy infrastructure with wave after wave of drone strikes, putting Putin in arguably his most difficult position of the war.

- China announced a temporary ban on helium exports (SCMP) and approved Shein's IPO in Hong Kong (Reuters).

- Japan's finance minister called on domestic pension funds to shift more assets into Japanese markets, boosting equities and the yen — though GPIF is likely to resist the political pressure (FT, Bloomberg).
