RATES & FED

Treasuries finished flat to down with yields rising roughly 2 basis points across the curve, a notably hawkish signal given the day's favorable inflation prints and oil weakness. The market is now pricing approximately 36 basis points of Fed hikes for the year. The fact yields couldn't rally despite softer oil and cooler Eurozone CPI suggests the bond market is sniffing out a more persistent inflation problem ahead.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The hyperscaler-vs.-chips rotation is the defining trade right now, and Wednesday's Meta news could accelerate it violently if the capex/revenue ratio conversation gains traction during Q2 earnings season. Legacy software and services names were the surprise winners of the session — money rotating out of overcrowded pick-and-shovel names and into the laggards is a pattern worth respecting. The macro backdrop is net constructive (cooler Eurozone CPI, decent ISM, falling layoffs), but sticky inflation expectations and tariff re-escalation via the USMCA walkout are real headwinds. Warsh's tone at Sintra was modestly encouraging — his acknowledgment of falling inflation expectations and bullish AI framing suggests the Fed isn't about to panic-hike. Keep eyes on Thursday's June jobs print; a soft number removes incremental pressure on the Fed without derailing the growth narrative.

COMPANY NEWS

META surged after Bloomberg reported the company is exploring selling excess computing capacity to external customers, following SpaceX's pivot toward a hyperscaler/neocloud model. Bullish for Meta's monetization story, but raises serious questions about industry-wide overbuilding of data center infrastructure.

FDS (FactSet) beat FQ3 EPS at $4.53 vs. Street $4.43, with organic revenue growth of +7% vs. Street +5.4%. Operating margins disappointed, falling 268 basis points Y/Y to 24% vs. Street's 34.6%, weighed by compensation and tech costs. Full-year guidance was merely reiterated despite the beat.

GIS (General Mills) delivered solid FQ4 EPS upside at $0.95 vs. Street $0.80, with gross and operating margins up 150 and 160 basis points respectively. Organic revenue was roughly inline, F27 guidance midpoints are near consensus, and the company announced a $3B cost-cut plan by F30.

MSM (MSC Industrial) posted strong FQ3 EPS of $1.43 vs. Street $1.27, with operating margins spiking 160 basis points Y/Y to 10.6%. Sales of $1.047B edged past the $1.032B estimate, and FQ4 guidance was described as bullish.

STZ (Constellation Brands) beat FQ1 EPS at $3.43 comparable vs. Street $3.18, with sales of $2.43B vs. Street $2.39B. Full-year EPS guidance was reiterated despite a "value-conscious consumer environment."

NKE (Nike) reported FQ4 results mostly inline ex-IAEA tariff benefit, but management struck a gloomy macro tone on the call, noting negative demand linearity during the quarter. Prior revenue guidance was trimmed, though EPS outlook held steady.

KR (Kroger) agreed to acquire Giant Eagle for $1.65B, comprising $1.25B in cash and assumption of ~$400MM in liabilities.

FDX (FedEx) will sell its FedEx Supply Chain division to CMA CGM Group for $1.4B enterprise value.

SMCI (Supermicro) saw Taiwanese officials detain two employees following a raid of local offices amid a probe into alleged smuggling of Nvidia chips to China (Bloomberg).

GM (General Motors) reported Q2 US unit sales down 4.2% Y/Y to ~715K, citing softer EV demand, discontinued vehicles, and inventory constraints. Separately, GM signed a Strategic Customer Agreement with MU (Micron) for memory supply.

AA (Alcoa) announced an acquisition of South32's bauxite, alumina, and aluminum operations for up to $4.85B ($3.1B cash + ~$1B stock + $750MM CVR).

MSFT (Microsoft) is preparing to cut thousands of jobs, representing less than 2.5% of its workforce, as part of ongoing cost discipline (Business Insider).

ALGN (Align Technology) faces a new EU antitrust probe into whether its Invisalign sales strategy violated competition rules (WSJ).

PRGS (Progress Software) beat FQ2 EPS at $1.62 vs. Street $1.49, on sales of $253MM vs. Street $243MM. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $6.09–$6.21 from $5.91–$6.03, though ARR growth remains muted.

BE (Bloom Energy) and Brookfield expanded their strategic partnership from $5B to $25B to meet surging hyperscaler and AI infrastructure power demand.

AAPL is in talks to source memory chips from China's CXMT and YMT for China-market devices, aiming to protect margins amid surging DRAM/NAND costs (Bloomberg).
