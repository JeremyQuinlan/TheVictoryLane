COMPANY NEWS

AAPL surged Thursday on two concurrent catalysts: the company is aggressively lobbying the White House for permission to use Chinese memory chips in devices sold in China — a margin-friendly move — and a Nikkei report indicating Apple is preparing a sweeping iPhone product offensive over coming quarters to exploit component market dislocations, which could drive a durable expansion of iOS market share.

ARM CEO Rene Haas told Tae Kim that CPU demand is "off the charts," driven by the rise of agentic AI, with both the number of CPUs and the core count per chip climbing simultaneously — a doubly bullish dynamic for ARM's royalty model (Tae Kim/Substack).

BX — Blackstone's QTS data center unit has abandoned plans for an approximately 800-acre facility in Virginia following local and regulatory opposition, a notable retreat in the hyperscale buildout (Bloomberg).

META is moving on multiple fronts: superintelligence chief Alexandr Wang said the upcoming "Watermelon" model is on par with OpenAI's GPT-5.5 and uses substantially more compute than Meta's existing frontier model "Avocado" (Business Insider). Separately, SemiAnalysis published a bullish read on Meta's potential neocloud ambitions, concluding that 2027 capex will be "shockingly high," the company remains committed to frontier model leadership, and any third-party capacity sales would be differentiated from vanilla IaaS — and highly accretive to earnings and cash flow (SemiAnalysis). Meta is also in talks to manufacture its next-gen MTIA AI chips at Samsung rather than TSMC, in a deal potentially worth $6.5 billion (Seeking Alpha).

NVDA — Nvidia's new initiative offering GPU access to startups in exchange for a share of their future profits is drawing scrutiny over circular financing risks within the AI ecosystem (CNBC).

Samsung Electronics is in parallel talks to produce AI chips for both Meta and Anthropic, positioning its foundry business as a credible TSMC alternative for hyperscaler custom silicon (Seeking Alpha).

Crusoe is in talks to raise $3 billion at a roughly $30 billion valuation — triple its $10 billion mark from October 2025 — underscoring relentless investor appetite for AI infrastructure (Bloomberg).
