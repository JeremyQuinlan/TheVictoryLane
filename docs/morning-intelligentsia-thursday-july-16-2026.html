RATES & FED

Treasuries are giving back modest ground after two days of gains following cooler CPI and PPI prints, with yields up roughly 2 basis points across the curve. The market is now pricing approximately 27 basis points of Fed hikes for the full year, down sharply from 40–45 basis points pre-CPI. The Fed is broadly expected to hold at the July 29th meeting, with NY Fed's Williams noting inflation has "peaked" and policy is "well positioned."

MARKET OUTLOOK

Two consecutive inflation undershoots — CPI then PPI — have meaningfully reduced pressure on the Fed, and the bond market is telling you the hiking cycle is nearly done. The TSMC report is the fly in the ointment today: AI demand remains ferocious, but margin softness and another capex budget increase raise the classic cyclical question — at what point does massive capacity buildout become a reason to fade semis rather than chase them? Iran remains the wildcard; the bull case (negotiated settlement) is more credible than the bear case (full escalation), but the uncertainty premium on oil isn't going away soon. With retail sales and jobless claims hitting at 8:30 this morning and NFLX after the close, today will be a live test of whether the soft-landing narrative holds.

COMPANY NEWS

TSM (TSMC) delivered mixed Q2 results — revenue guidance was strong but margins came in softer than hoped, capex was revised higher again, and management flagged weakness in non-AI consumer end markets. ADRs are down ~3% pre-market. The perennial question: are semis still cyclical? Historically, massive capex ramp-ups are signals to fade, not chase.

UBER announced a deal to acquire Delivery Hero at EU41.50/share, implying an equity value of $14.8B ($13.7B net of Uber's existing stake). Management expects 7–9% EPS accretion by year three (Uber IR).

UAL (United Airlines) beat Q2 EPS at $1.99 vs. Street $1.85, but fuel expense surged 84% Y/Y, dragging on Q3 guidance (EPS midpoint $3.00 vs. Street $3.62). Management expects to recover 80–90% of fuel costs in Q3 and 100% in Q4. Underlying demand remains healthy; yields were up 12% Y/Y.

JBHT (JB Hunt) posted Q2 EPS of $1.91 (+45%), well ahead of the Street's $1.74, on revenue of $3.49B vs. $3.25B expected. Intermodal — the business to watch — delivered revenue/operating income of $1.75B/$151MM vs. Street $1.6B/$127MM, with volumes up 10%.
