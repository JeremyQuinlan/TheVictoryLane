MARKET OUTLOOK

The calendar is deliberately light this week, which keeps the tape in a bit of a holding pattern before the action picks up sharply the week of 7/13 — US CPI, Warsh's congressional testimony, and a full broadside of major bank earnings will be the real stress test. Airlines look like the cleanest long setup near-term: demand is healthy, fuel costs are a tailwind, and carriers aren't rushing to give back pricing even as oil has softened (a margin gift they'll pocket, not pass on). Consumer staples remain a minefield — secular headwinds from private label, MAHA-driven consumption shifts, and minimal pricing power make the risk/reward unattractive. Watch whether PEP can hold the line on North America Beverages, which is carrying the rest of the portfolio.

GEOPOLITICAL

- China June inflation data drops Wednesday night 7/8, with China's trade data (imports/exports) and Q2 GDP following the week of 7/13 — the sequential read on the Chinese consumer and industrial economy will matter for global reflation narratives.

COMPANY NEWS

LEVI (Levi Strauss) reports FQ2 Wednesday 7/8 after the close. Street consensus is coalescing around a beat-and-raise scenario, supported by brand-specific momentum and management's credible execution track record. Estimates call for organic revenue growth of +3.7%, operating margins of 8.9%, and EPS of $0.24.

PEP (PepsiCo) reports FQ2 Thursday 7/9 before the open. Sentiment is cautious across consumer staples broadly — demand pressure, margin compression, private label encroachment, and the MAHA-driven pivot away from processed food and sugary drinks are all live headwinds. The beverage unit is PEP's relative edge here. Consensus looks for organic revenue +2.6% overall (North America Foods +0.85%, North America Beverages +2.2%), EPS of $2.21, and operating margins of 17.3%.

DAL (Delta Air Lines) reports FQ2 Friday 7/10 before the open. Delta is the consensus favorite in the group — premium customer focus, the American Express partnership as a durable revenue stream, and a management team with a strong operational track record differentiate it from peers. Street is modeling total revenue +14.3% to $17.73B and EPS of $1.47, which would represent a -30% year-over-year decline.
