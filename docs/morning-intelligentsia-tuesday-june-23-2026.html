RATES & FED

Treasuries catching a modest bid with yields down 1–4 basis points across the curve as risk-off flows find a home in duration. Markets are now pricing roughly 38 basis points of Fed hikes in H2, down from ~41 basis points at Monday's close. Fed Chair Warsh's next public appearance is expected at the ECB's Sintra conference on July 1, though given his stated preference for reducing forward guidance, don't expect fireworks.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

This selloff isn't one headline — it's an accumulation of legitimate grievances that have been building for weeks: extreme AI infrastructure costs scaling with revenue, open-source and Chinese models (Z.ai's GLM-5.2, potential DeepSeek via Microsoft) eating token share, government restrictions on frontier models, and a post-IPO SPCX that signals the compute supply may be larger than the market appreciated. The rotation into software/services names in Europe today is telling — that trade is likely coming to the US, though as Accenture demonstrated last week, legacy tech isn't a clean safe haven either. The next make-or-break moments are CBRS tonight and MU Wednesday; if those disappoint, the damage could deepen meaningfully before CQ2 earnings kick off July 14.

GEOPOLITICAL

- US-Iran: Trump continues to accommodate Tehran (the recent MOU terms reflect that clearly), and bulls are pricing in higher OPEC+ quotas, Iranian sanctions removal, UAE departing OPEC+, and higher Venezuelan output as a deflationary oil catalyst. Bears counter that Hormuz remains Iran's primary leverage point, traffic stays below pre-war levels, and the Israel-Hezbollah conflict in Lebanon is nowhere near resolution (internal view).

- Middle East diplomacy: Secretary Rubio embarks Tuesday on a Gulf tour covering UAE, Kuwait, and Bahrain (internal).

- US defense production: Trump will convene defense industry executives and senior Pentagon officials Wednesday to address concerns over depleted American missile stockpiles (WSJ).

- EU gas supply risk: The US and Qatar warned the Eurozone that new methane emission rules risk triggering a gas supply crunch (FT).

- South Korea market shock: Regulators cautioned on the popularity of leveraged ETF products, sparking a KOSPI plunge of nearly 10%. Retail investors have been rotating out of AI-driven equity gains (SK Hynix, Samsung) into real estate (Reuters, FT).

- UK/Brexit: A NYT analysis concludes the UK economy is 4–6% smaller than it would have been inside the EU, with Brexit blamed for years of political instability and economic damage (NYT).

COMPANY NEWS

SPCX (SpaceX) struck a third cloud computing deal Monday, this time with Reflection AI, following earlier pacts with Anthropic and Google. While revenue-accretive near-term, the hyperscaler pivot raises two uncomfortable questions: it implies a narrower TAM for Grok/Cursor ambitions and suggests overall compute supply may be larger than appreciated.

QCOM (Qualcomm) is in advanced talks to acquire AI chip startup Modular for ~$4B (Bloomberg), and holds an analyst meeting Wednesday at 2:15pmET.

ORCL (Oracle) filed its 10-K after Monday's close revealing 141K full-time employees at end of F26 — down ~20K from the 162K reported in the FQ3 10-Q.

APO (Apollo) capped withdrawals at 5% of shares outstanding in its largest non-traded private credit retail fund after redemption requests hit nearly 17% (Bloomberg).

PRIM (Primoris Services) issued a negative preannouncement: 2026 revenue and gross profit will miss prior guidance due to cost overruns in Renewables; EPS now guided to $2.05–$2.60 vs. prior $4.80–$5.00. COO also stepping down.

DPZ (Domino's) named COO Joe Jordan as incoming CEO effective October 1; Russell Weiner transitions to Executive Chairman Designate through the April 2027 shareholder meeting.

EPC (Edgewell Personal Care) rejected a $30/share unsolicited bid from Yellow Wood Partners as inadequate (Bloomberg).

TSLA (Tesla) posted Eurozone registrations of 28.61K vehicles in May, more than double year-over-year (WSJ) — but separately faces an NHTSA investigation after a fatal Model 3 automated driving crash into a Texas home (WSJ).

BBY (Best Buy) CFO Matt Bilunas is departing end of July; CEO Corie Barry, a former CFO, will provide interim financial oversight.

CBOE/CME/ICE/NDAQ have all sold off amid concerns over market share losses to newly CFTC-approved perpetual futures products (WSJ).

Couche-Tard reported FQ4 EPS of 73c vs. Street at 54c; revenue ~$19.5B vs. $18.73B estimate, with US fuel margins jumping 9.17 cents per gallon to 52.44 cents.
