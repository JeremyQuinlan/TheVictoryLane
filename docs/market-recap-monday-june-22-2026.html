RATES & FED

Treasuries sold off despite falling oil prices, with yields rising 5–6 basis points across the curve — a signal the market views recent inflation acceleration as more durable than hoped. The market is now pricing in 41 basis points of Fed rate hikes, with growing chatter that Warsh could be forced to move 25–50 basis points in H2. Warsh's opaque communication style is itself a risk factor, with his multiple internal task forces potentially ushering in structural changes at the Fed before year-end.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The rotation Monday told the real story: money moved out of hyperscalers and legacy software into banks, utilities, industrials, and healthcare — classic late-cycle defensiveness. The AI trade is not broken, but it's cracking at the edges, with CTO pushback on token costs, no free cash flow in sight at any major neocloud, and Chinese open-source models (GLM-5.2 from Z.ai, DeepSeek) making the case for premium frontier pricing harder by the day. The Treasury market's refusal to rally on a 2.85% drop in Brent is the single most important signal from Monday — if oil can't buy bonds a bid, inflation expectations are becoming unanchored. Watch PCE Thursday and the June jobs report on 7/2 closely; if both run hot, the rate-hike narrative accelerates fast.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US and Iran declared "encouraging progress" after weekend talks in Switzerland, agreeing to a 60-day roadmap toward a final deal; Washington formally removed sanctions on Tehran's energy industry (CNBC). The oil market read this as net positive — Brent fell sharply — but the hard issues remain unresolved.

- The shipping industry is increasingly worried Iran could impose transit fees on vessels through the Strait of Hormuz once the 60-day window closes (FT). Trump floated a US military "Guardian Angel" toll for Hormuz security (NBC News).

- US intelligence agencies warn the White House that Netanyahu is likely to take steps in Lebanon that could undermine the Iran détente (Washington Post, NYT). The Lebanon conflict remains a major wildcard for the entire framework.

- US-Qatar initiative is in progress to give Iran access to frozen funds for humanitarian purchases (WSJ).

- Ukraine's drone campaign targeting Russian energy and military infrastructure is shifting battlefield momentum toward Kyiv; Crimea has halted civilian gasoline sales following strikes on Russian energy infrastructure (Politico, NBC News).

- China responded to US export restrictions by placing trade curbs on dozens of American firms, though analysts don't expect the move to materially derail bilateral relations (SCMP).

- UK PM Starmer formally resigned Monday; Andy Burnham is expected to become the UK's 7th leader in a decade, with a successor in place by September (NBC News).

- Congress is growing alarmed that the White House plans to dismantle USMCA in favor of non-binding bilateral MOUs (NYT).

COMPANY NEWS

CRH is acquiring ACA (Arcosa) for $150/share in cash, implying an enterprise value of $8.5B at 11.5x 2026 post-synergy EBITDA.

ABBV (AbbVie) is acquiring APGE (Apogee) for $135.11/share, valuing the company at $10.9B in equity; AbbVie says the deal will be accretive to EPS beginning in 2032.

GOOGL (Google) slumped Monday as two high-profile AI researcher departures — Nobel laureate John Jumper to Anthropic and Noam Shazeer to OpenAI — reinforced the narrative that Google is losing its frontier model edge (Bloomberg). Separately, Google is leveraging its balance sheet aggressively to build a rival AI chip business to Nvidia (WSJ).

SPCX (SpaceX) signed a cloud computing deal with Reflection AI worth up to $6.3B ($150M/month), following similar pacts with Anthropic and Google (CNBC). The company is also seeking ~$20B via a debt offering (Bloomberg). The pivot toward infrastructure hosting raises questions about whether SpaceX is stepping back from frontier model ambitions — a potential TAM-shrinking development for its multiple.

MU (Micron) signed a multi-year memory supply deal with Anthropic and also participated in Anthropic's Series H funding round. MU reports earnings after the close Wednesday 6/24.

META is investing $900M into fintech startup Cred, with Cred founder Kunal Shah set to lead WhatsApp (FT). Separately, concerns are mounting about Meta's infrastructure overbuild relative to internal ad revenue needs and deteriorating employee morale (The Information).

MSFT (Microsoft) signed a 20-year natural gas supply deal with CVX (Chevron) for a Texas data center (Bloomberg) and is considering offering DeepSeek models to customers to meet demand for lower-cost AI (WSJ). CEO Nadella publicly warned that AI value cannot accrue exclusively to a handful of frontier firms (WSJ).

AMZN (Amazon) is in talks to sell its Trainium AI chips to external data center operators, intensifying competition with Nvidia and AMD (Bloomberg).

LCID (Lucid) is cutting headcount by 18% and eliminating the second production shift at its AMP-1 factory; the COO is also departing.

GETY (Getty Images) surged in pre-market after announcing a content licensing partnership with OpenAI, giving ChatGPT access to Getty's licensed image libraries.

RGNX (Regenxbio) — the FDA will reverse its prior rejection of the company's brain disease gene therapy (WSJ).

MS (Morgan Stanley) is weighing a $1.3B operational hub in Dallas (WSJ).

TSLA (Tesla) faces an NHTSA investigation after a Model 3 using automated driver assistance crashed into a Texas home, killing one person (WSJ).

DIS (Disney) — "Toy Story 5" delivered the biggest box office debut of 2026, contributing to what's shaping up as one of Hollywood's best summers in years (Barron's).

NKE (Nike) CEO Elliott Hill told the FT his turnaround is working and will eventually show up in the financials (FT).

MSFT/AMZN/CRWV/ORCL/SPCX — the hyperscaler/neocloud complex broadly underperformed Monday. ACN extended its post-earnings losses; ADBE, CRM, NOW, PLTR, and WDAY also declined.
