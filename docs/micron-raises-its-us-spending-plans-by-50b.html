MARKET OUTLOOK

The Micron news is telling in what it reveals about the broader AI infrastructure trade — memory supply is clearly starting to respond to the demand surge, but the HBM market in particular looks capacity-constrained for the foreseeable future, which keeps the fundamental bull case intact for the leading players. The competitive landscape is getting more crowded (SK Hynix US ADRs debuting Friday, CXMT eyeing a China IPO), so selectivity matters more than it did a year ago. Don't read the Micron headline at face value — this is as much a political gesture toward Washington as it is a capex commitment.

GEOPOLITICAL

- SK Hynix's US ADRs are set to begin trading on Friday, adding a new publicly traded vehicle for investors seeking HBM exposure. Chinese memory player CXMT is also reportedly moving toward a public listing in China, signaling Beijing's push to build domestic memory capacity. (Vital Knowledge intraday note)

- Samsung and SK Hynix have recently made fresh expansion announcements out of South Korea, reinforcing the broader theme of global memory supply build-out in response to AI-driven demand. (Vital Knowledge intraday note)

---

COMPANY NEWS

MU (Micron Technology) announced it now plans to invest more than $250B in US manufacturing and R&D through 2035, up from a prior target of approximately $200B — a $50B increase on paper. However, the author's read is skeptical: spread across nearly a decade, that's roughly $5B per year in incremental US spending, a relatively modest figure. Critically, it's unclear whether Micron is genuinely increasing its total global capex or simply reshuffling spend from overseas to domestic facilities. The press release reads more like a message to Washington than a signal to Wall Street, and the market should treat it accordingly.
