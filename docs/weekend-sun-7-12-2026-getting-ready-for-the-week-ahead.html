MARKET OUTLOOK

The weekend's Iran escalation will almost certainly trigger a knee-jerk move lower in equities Monday, but the author's view is that fallout stays contained — a military solution doesn't exist, and some form of negotiated settlement remains far more likely than not. Oil retightening is the real risk, and a Brent rally back into the $80s (from ~$70) could paradoxically make the White House more eager for offramps. Banks are well-positioned heading into earnings — healthy loan growth, contained credit costs, booming capital markets — but sentiment, valuation, and positioning are all crowded bullish, capping near-term upside. AI remains the dominant market force; a single hyperscaler capex cut headline would dwarf anything out of Tehran or Moscow in terms of market impact.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Iran appeared headed toward a Hormuz deescalation Saturday — expressing contrition for last week's commercial vessel strikes and blaming "rogue IRGC elements" — before firing on another commercial ship Saturday evening, forcing crew abandonment, and declaring Hormuz closed; the US launched its third round of strikes in a week, and Iran subsequently attacked targets across the region including Jordan (Vital Knowledge, NYT).

- Oman negotiations were moving toward a dual-route Hormuz framework (one Iranian-controlled lane, one Omani-coast lane), with ship fees potentially mirroring Malacca Strait voluntary payment arrangements — that framework may still be the eventual resolution (Reuters, WSJ).

- Iran's economy is already devastated, and conditions worsen now that oil-sales sanction waivers have been rescinded; over time, Hormuz control obsession will accelerate regional alternative infrastructure buildout (Reuters, WSJ).

- Ukraine has significant battlefield momentum; Trump granted Patriot manufacturing rights and long-range strike approval at NATO, and senators reached a White House-backed deal to tighten Russia sanctions Friday (Reuters). Russia's gasoline output covers only 65% of domestic demand after Ukrainian drone strikes, and China has again warned Moscow against nuclear weapons use (Reuters, Bloomberg).

- Senator Lindsey Graham (R-SC) passed away Sunday at 71 following a "brief and sudden" illness (NYT).

- Senate Republicans are growing nervous about a government shutdown fight just before November midterms — current spending authorization expires September 30 (Axios).

- US population growth could halt next decade and reverse by the 2050s, with net immigration collapsing under Trump, creating severe long-term fiscal consequences (NYT).
