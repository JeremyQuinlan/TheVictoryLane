COMPANY NEWS

AMC (AMC Entertainment) is selling 95.25M shares of common stock at an implied price of ~$2.10/share — a steep ~24% discount to Monday's close of $2.76 — raising approximately $200MM. Classic dilutive capital raise; not a confidence-inspiring price.

COUR (Coursera) issued a business update post-Udemy deal closure, targeting 2026 revenue of $1.21–$1.24B with gross margins of 61.5% and EBITDA margins of 13%, implying ~$160MM in EBITDA. The company has executed ~$70MM of its $500MM buyback authorization and held ~$1.15B in cash with zero debt as of Q1-end — meaning the implied enterprise value of ~$154MM is less than 1x EBITDA. Synergies from the Udemy merger are tracking ahead of schedule.

FMC (FMC Corp) announced a $114MM sale-leaseback of its Newark, Delaware property — a straightforward balance sheet optimization move.

KDP (Keurig Dr Pepper) reaffirmed its 2026 guidance, citing "strong momentum," alongside leadership changes. The planned separation of its coffee business remains on track for early 2027.

Maersk CEO says container shipment demand is likely to remain solid in H2 2026 (Bloomberg) — a constructive data point for global trade and freight-exposed names.

MGX, the Abu Dhabi-based AI firm, has raised ~$50B from investors to accelerate AI infrastructure and technology spending (Bloomberg). The scale of this raise underscores that sovereign-backed AI capital deployment is still in full sprint mode.

QDEL (QuidelOrtho) named Micah Young as CFO, succeeding Joseph M. Busky who had previously announced his retirement. Routine transition.

SHO (Sunstone Hotel Investors) is selling its Hyatt Regency San Francisco to Blackstone for $279MM — roughly $340K per key, 21.4x EBITDA, and a 3.5% cap rate. A clean read on where institutional buyers are pricing trophy hospitality assets right now.

SMCI (Supermicro)-backed Argentum announced $7.8B in AI cloud deals for its data center in Poland (Barron's). Another data point in the accelerating buildout of European AI infrastructure using Nvidia chips.

SPCX / SpaceX: SoftBank's Masayoshi Son publicly dismissed the concept of orbital data centers as impractical and not an AI race winner (Bloomberg) — a pointed jab at Musk's space-based compute ambitions.

SUNB (Sunbelt Rentals) reported a modest FQ4 miss — EPS of 74c vs. Street 76c, EBITDA of $1.067B vs. Street $1.07B — as EBITDA margins of 38.7% came in roughly 120 basis points below consensus, offsetting a revenue beat of $2.75B vs. Street $2.633B. The F2027 guide tells the same story: sales growth of 6% (ahead of Street's +4.4%) but EBITDA margins of 41.9% vs. Street's 43.44%. Top-line strength, margin pressure — a theme worth watching broadly.

Vuori plans to double its retail footprint both domestically and internationally, with China a key target market (WSJ). The athleisure brand is pressing its growth window hard.

WEN (Wendy's) appointed Steve Cirulis as CFO and Chief Strategy Officer, effective June 23. Cirulis comes from Potbelly — the same shop that produced Wendy's new CEO — signaling a deliberate leadership continuity play at the top.
