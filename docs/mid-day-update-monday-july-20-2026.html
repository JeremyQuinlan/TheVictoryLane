RATES & FED

Treasuries are under selling pressure with yields up roughly 3 basis points across the curve. Markets are now pricing approximately 34 basis points of rate hikes in H2 — a hawkish tilt that reflects lingering inflation concerns. A statistical overhaul to how PCE is compiled could nudge inflation modestly lower when the new format debuts with the August number on September 30, which may slightly soften the case for tightening (WSJ).

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The SPX's equal-weight version dropping while cap-weighted holds up tells you everything — this is a narrow, tech-driven tape, not a broad bull move. The rotation back into pick-and-shovel names (chips, infrastructure) is logical given Moonshot's demand-overwhelmed subscription pause, which underscores the compute deficit thesis remains very much intact. That said, open-source Chinese models continuing to eat OpenAI and Anthropic's lunch is a real near-term risk to the capex mania narrative if it pressures those firms' revenue. Geopolitical tail risk from the US-Iran conflict and a Houthi naval blockade threat around Saudi Arabia keeps energy in play and consumer confidence fragile. Don't chase the headline index — the internals are quietly deteriorating.

- China is shipping roughly 20% fewer rare-earth magnets to the US versus pre-trade-war levels, despite the nominal truce (Bloomberg).

- Xi is still planning a lavish US state visit in the coming months; the White House is not pursuing a probe of alleged Chinese election interference (CNN, Politico).
