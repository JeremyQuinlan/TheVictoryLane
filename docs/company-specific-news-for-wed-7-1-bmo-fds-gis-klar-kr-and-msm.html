COMPANY NEWS

FDS (FactSet) posted a mixed FQ3 beat-and-reiterate. EPS came in at $4.53 versus the Street's $4.43, with organic revenue growth of +7% handily clearing the consensus of +5.4%. The problem was margins — operating margins fell 268 basis points year-over-year to 24%, well below the Street's 34.6%, with the drag coming from higher compensation and tech-related expenses. Organic ASV growth, the closely watched metric, was inline at +7.1%, and full-year guidance was merely reiterated despite the beat. The bottom line here is classic AI trade-off: spend to stay competitive and bleed margins, or protect profitability and risk share loss.

GIS (General Mills) delivered a genuinely impressive FQ4 margin print despite soft top-line dynamics. EPS of 95 cents crushed the Street's 80-cent estimate, with gross and operating margins up 150 and 160 basis points respectively. Organic revenue was flat year-over-year — inline overall, though Pet was the one segment that missed. Volumes disappointed at -2% versus the Street's -0.7%. Management unveiled a new cost-cutting program targeting $3 billion in cumulative savings through F2030, including $750 million in F2027. F2027 EPS guidance midpoint of $3.10 is roughly inline with the Street's $3.13, while the organic sales outlook of -0.5% is modestly below the +0.1% consensus. Not a great report on the absolute, but the margin story is enough to keep investors reasonably happy.

KLAR (Klarna) saw a Swedish court rule that Google must pay approximately $1.47 billion related to the PriceRunner price comparison case. That figure equals roughly 20% of Klarna's market cap. Google will almost certainly appeal.

KR (Kroger) announced the acquisition of family-owned Giant Eagle for $1.65 billion — $1.25 billion in cash plus assumption of approximately $400 million in liabilities. Giant Eagle operates 197 supermarkets and 11 standalone pharmacies across Ohio, Pennsylvania, West Virginia, Maryland, and Indiana, with roughly $9 billion in annual sales (about 6% of Kroger's total). The deal is expected to be accretive to adjusted EPS in the second full year post-close and will not affect KR's existing capital return program. After watching the ACI deal get killed by regulators, Kroger is clearly sticking to small, digestible bolt-ons.

MSM (MSC Industrial) delivered a standout FQ3 with EPS of $1.43 versus the Street's $1.27. Operating margins spiked 160 basis points year-over-year to 10.6%, beating consensus by roughly 60 basis points. Sales of $1.047 billion edged out the $1.032 billion estimate, with management noting that average daily sales exceeded the high end of their own outlook on the back of pricing benefits and a return to volume growth. FQ4 guidance is bullish — ADS growth of ~7.5% versus the Street's 7%, with operating margins of 10.4% versus consensus of 10%. Nothing to complain about here.
