MARKET OUTLOOK

The earnings tape this morning is telling a nuanced story — beats are plentiful but the bar was high, and guidance quality is separating winners from laggards fast. GEV's order explosion and WAB's margin strength confirm industrial demand is real and durable, while OTIS's guide cut and CALM's egg-price collapse are reminders that not every sector is firing. The broader market should digest this mixed-but-leaning-positive batch reasonably well, though richly valued names like GEV leave little room for execution slips.

COMPANY NEWS

CALM (Cal-Maine) posted a brutal FQ4 miss — EPS of -76 cents versus the Street's +8 cents, and sales of $552MM against estimates of $595MM. Management pinned the damage on industry oversupply driving wholesale shell egg prices to historically low inflation-adjusted levels, calling it "supply-driven rather than demand-driven" with long-term demand fundamentals still intact.

PM (Philip Morris) delivered solid Q2 EPS of $2.20 versus the Street's $2.04, with smoke-free organic revenue up 11.8%. The disappointment is that FX-neutral EPS guidance was merely reiterated at $8.11–$8.26 rather than raised — the market wanted a hike.

CME (CME Group) beat modestly in Q2 — sales of $1.7B versus $1.67B expected, EPS of $2.99 versus $2.90. Average daily volume hit 29.8 million contracts, the second-highest second quarter on record, with all-time high ADVs in both May (+15% to 33.2M) and June (+19% to 30.6M).

MCO (Moody's) posted strong Q2 EPS of $4.68 versus $4.28 expected, on sales of $2.185B versus $2.086B. MIS revenue spiked 25% and crushed estimates; MA grew only 4% and missed. Operating margins expanded 440 basis points to 55.3%. Full-year EPS guidance nudged to $16.50–$17.00 from $16.40–$17.00, and the buyback was increased to $3B from $2.5B.

PHM (PulteGroup) beat Q2 EPS at $2.48 versus $2.36, driven by housing gross margins of 25% against the Street's 24.3%. Orders rose 6% to 7,536 units. Management struck a cautiously optimistic tone, citing "early signs that conditions may be stabilizing in select geographies."

BLFS / RGEN — Repligen is acquiring BioLife Solutions in a deal valued at $1.5B enterprise value. BLFS holders receive $31 per share ($11.25 cash plus 0.1442 RGEN shares).

GEV (GE Vernova) missed Q2 EBITDA ($1.25B vs. $1.29B expected) but revenue came in at $11.1B versus $10.8B, free cash flow was a massive $5.1B against estimates of $1.3B, and organic orders surged 88% to $24.2B. Full-year sales guidance raised to $45.5–$46.5B; cash flow guidance nearly doubled to $11.5–$12.5B. Power orders +135%, Electrification +93%, Wind -39%. Bottom line: the Power/Electrification order surge and FCF outperformance are the real story — secular demand is intact — but the valuation is extremely rich and Wind remains a drag.

OTIS (Otis Worldwide) matched Q2 EPS at $1.01, with organic top-line growth of 6% versus the Street's +2.8%, but operating margins fell 180 basis points to 15.2% versus 16% expected. Full-year EPS guidance cut to $4.01–$4.05 from $4.20–$4.24. New equipment orders fell 5% FX-neutral, driven by a greater than 20% decline in Asia Pacific and high-teens decline in China. The margin miss and guide cut will dominate the reaction.

RPM (RPM International) posted FQ4 EPS of $1.89 versus $1.85 expected. F27 guidance mid-points are modestly ahead — sales +5% versus the Street's +4.5%, EBITDA +7.5% versus +6.5%. Added $700MM to the buyback; analyst day set for November 9.

WAB (Wabtec) delivered Q2 EPS of $2.76 versus $2.61, with sales up 17.5% to $3.18B versus $3.07B and operating margins expanding 80 basis points to 21.9%. Full-year revenue and EPS mid-points raised ~$110MM and ~30 cents respectively. The 12-month backlog dipped slightly quarter-over-quarter to $9.14B from $9.247B.

MNDY (Monday.com) announced a ~20% headcount reduction to support a "leaner, more focused operating model" centered on AI. The company expects full-year 2026 results in-line with or above prior guidance.

T (AT&T) beat Q2 EBITDA at $12.3B versus $12.1B, with free cash flow of $4.7B versus $4.4B. Postpaid phone net adds of 432K crushed the 325K estimate; fiber net adds of 367K beat 303K. Buyback pace accelerating in 2026. Execution is solid — but Starlink competitive anxiety remains the structural overhang.

TEL (TE Connectivity) posted FQ3 EPS of $2.94 versus $2.84, on sales of $5.16B versus $5.0B. FQ4 guidance of $5.25B sales and $3.05 EPS both top consensus. TEL also announced the $1.4B acquisition of Astrodyne TDI. Investors have come to expect modest beats from TEL — this clears the bar but doesn't exceed it.
