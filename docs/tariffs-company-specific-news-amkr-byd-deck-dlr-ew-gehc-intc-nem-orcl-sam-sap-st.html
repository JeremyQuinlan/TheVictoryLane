MARKET OUTLOOK

The tariff regime landing largely in line with expectations is a relief — markets had priced in worse — and the 10-12% universal baseline (replacing the expiring 10% global temp) gives corporate planning desks something to work with. Earnings quality tonight is genuinely strong across healthcare and TMT, with Intel in particular delivering a blowout that should lift semis broadly. The Oracle overhang (OpenAI execution risk, aggressive capex) is the notable wet blanket. Net-net, the tape has more reasons to grind higher than to roll over here.

---

GEOPOLITICAL

- The White House formally unveiled a 10-12% tariff regime under Section 301 of the Trade Act of 1974, applied across more than 80 countries — timed to replace the temporary global 10% tariff expiring tomorrow. Exemptions cover oil and gas, USMCA-covered goods, and national security tariffs already in place on autos and steel (Bloomberg, CNBC, NYT). The move was expected and holds levies roughly consistent with current levels, which the White House had flagged as a goal since pledging to return average rates to pre-SCOTUS levels by summer's end.

COMPANY NEWS

INTC delivered the headline beat of the cycle — Q2 EPS of 42c crushed the Street's 21c, with revenue of $16.1B vs. expectations of $14.4B. Client, Data Center, and Foundry all came in above plan; gross margins hit 41.8% vs. the Street's 39.2%. Q3 guidance is equally bullish: EPS of 38c vs. 27c expected, revenue midpoint of $16.3B vs. $15.1B. CEO Lip-Bu Tan cited "AI driving unprecedented demand for compute" and confirmed Intel plans to "meaningfully increase" capex — potentially ~$20B this year. On foundry, Tan told Bloomberg there are "multiple engagements" but stayed quiet on names.

AMKR announced a multi-year strategic partnership with Nvidia to develop advanced semiconductor packaging and test technologies. Nvidia will provide a prepayment to support expansion of Amkor's U.S. advanced packaging capacity.

DLR posted strong Q2 FFO upside and raised its full-year FFO guide to $8.10–8.15 (FXN) from $7.95–8.05. Bookings dipped to $307MM from $707MM in Q1, but management noted two hyperscale leases signed in July totaling $410MM — so the air-pocket looks temporary.

ORCL secured a 10-year Pentagon software contract worth up to $7B, but the market's real focus remains on its massive capex commitments and whether OpenAI can deliver on its obligations — a cloud over the stock that a defense win doesn't dissolve.

SAP beat on cloud metrics in Q2 — current cloud backlog +26% FXN vs. Street +23.8%, cloud revenue +24% vs. +23.2% — but margin pressure drove an earnings miss. The company trimmed its full-year operating profit outlook due to acquisition dilution, while flagging revenue acceleration in 2027.

EW turned in a clean Q2 beat: sales +12.5% vs. Street +10.3%, EPS 78c vs. 74c, and the full-year sales guide was nudged up to +10-11% from +9-11%.

GEHC preannounced Q2 EPS above expectations with organic revenue growth of 3.5% (inline) and reiterated its full-year outlook. CFO departure is the one cautionary note.

THC beat on Q2 sales and EBITDA and raised full-year guidance — a meaningful positive given the recent negative read from peer HCA. The company also announced a $2B buyback increase.

NEM posted Q2 EPS of $2.10 vs. Street $2.02, with better free cash flow, though EBITDA fell slightly short. Full-year production guidance reiterated, with costs tracking "well below" plan.

DECK reported FQ1 EPS of 94c vs. Street 89c, driven by gross margin strength; sales were inline and SG&A ran slightly hot. Full-year EPS guide raised modestly, though the lift is tax-rate-driven rather than operational.

SAM posted Q2 EPS of $4.96 vs. Street $4.84, with depletions of -6% beating the feared -9%. Full-year guidance maintained.

BYD (Boyd Gaming) beat on EBITDA ($350.5MM vs. Street $339MM) with inline sales. Las Vegas destination softness offset by strength in Midwest/South and Online segments.

Stripe is in talks to acquire AI startup OpenRouter in a deal that could value the target at ~$10B (WSJ).
