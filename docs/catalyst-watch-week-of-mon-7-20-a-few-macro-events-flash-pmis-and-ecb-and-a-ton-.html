MARKET OUTLOOK

The week of July 20 is essentially an earnings gauntlet, and Wednesday night is the one session that matters most — GOOGL and NOW will either validate or shatter the current AI spending narrative. IBM's pre-announcement on July 14 (blaming a mainframe cycle downturn and a CTO pivot away from legacy software toward AI infrastructure) has already rattled software sentiment, and ServiceNow is now in the hot seat to disprove that thesis. If Google confirms hyperscaler capex conviction and ServiceNow posts clean RPO growth, the market gets its "all clear" on AI. If both disappoint, the software complex — already cheap but unloved — takes another leg down. Defense names look complicated: global budgets are surging, but the legacy primes face drone disruption, margin pressure, and the risk that hot conflicts (Iran, Ukraine) are past peak intensity.

---

GEOPOLITICAL

- Defense spending is surging globally, but legacy contractors (LMT, NOC, RTX) face structural headwinds from the pivot toward cheap drones over expensive manned platforms, fiscal uncertainty, and the prospect of cooling conflicts — the Iran war is described as past its peak, and Ukraine may be at an inflection point.

- SpaceX/Starlink is an emerging competitive threat to the Big 3 carriers (T, VZ, TMUS), though for now satellite is characterized as more complement than competitor to consumer mobile. The medium-term risk is real and weighing on valuations.

- The UNP-NSC merger will face heavy regulatory scrutiny, but the base case is ultimate approval — a deal that would meaningfully concentrate pricing power across the remaining rails.

COMPANY NEWS

GOOGL reports Wednesday night. Street is modeling total revenue (ex-TAC) +23.4% to ~$100.9B, including Search +16.6% to $63.2B, Cloud +64.2% to $22.37B, and YouTube +10.3% to $10.8B. Op. income +29% to $40.36B. Full-year 2026 capex guidance stands at $180–190B. Gemini has lost momentum since its November launch, which is a watch item.

IBM pre-announced on July 14 with revenue of $17.2B and EPS of $2.93, blaming mainframe cycle weakness post-z17 launch and a CTO spending shift toward data center hardware and security software. The formal Wednesday night report will focus on H2 guidance and whether management raises or cuts the full-year outlook.

NOW reports Wednesday night. Street expects FXN cRPO growth of ~19.5%, FXN subscription revenue growth of ~21.9%, and op. margins of 27.8%. IBM's warning makes this one of the highest-stakes prints of the season for software broadly.

TSLA reports Wednesday night. Street models revenue of $25.5B, aggregate GMs of 19.25%, and EPS of 47c. The author is blunt: with the vast majority of market cap tied to Optimus, FSD, Robotaxi, and a potential SpaceX merger, Q2 numbers are almost beside the point.

INTC reports Thursday night. Street expects revenue +11.9% to $14.38B — Datacenter/AI +39% to $5.45B, Foundry +24% to $5.48B — with GMs of ~39% and EPS of 22c. Sentiment is very bullish on a CPU-over-GPU shift in agentic AI and Apple foundry reports, though execution risk on foundry is high.
