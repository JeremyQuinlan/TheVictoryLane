MARKET OUTLOOK

The earnings season is off to a strong start, with financials and healthcare both delivering meaningful upside — that's a constructive signal for the broader market. BlackRock's AUM crossing $15.3T and margin expansion to near five-year highs suggests the asset management industry is in genuinely good shape, not just riding beta. Elevance's MCR improvement points to stabilizing medical cost trends in managed care, which had been a persistent source of anxiety. If this tone holds through the rest of July reporting, the bull case gets harder to argue against.

COMPANY NEWS

BASF issued an upside preannouncement for Q2, with EBITDA expected at €2.4B versus the Street's €2.1B, driven by higher prices (+11%) and higher volumes (+7%). Full-year guidance was raised to €6.9–7.7B from the prior €6.2–7.0B range — a meaningful step up that signals genuine demand recovery in European chemicals.

BLK (BlackRock) delivered a strong Q2 beat, with adjusted EPS of $13.91 (+15%) versus the Street's $12.66, on revenue of $7.08B (+31%) versus consensus of $6.82B. Operating margins expanded roughly 260 basis points to 45.9% — about 120 basis points above consensus and the highest level in nearly five years. Total long-term inflows of $199B came in approximately $30B ahead of expectations, and AUM finished the quarter at $15.344T. The company is accelerating buybacks, raising its 2026 repurchase target to $2B. Management noted quarterly operating income grew approximately 40% year-over-year. The only mild drag: a slightly elevated tax rate that capped what would have been an even larger EPS beat.

ELV (Elevance) posted Q2 EPS of $7.45 versus the Street's $6.18, with revenue of $49.8B slightly ahead of the $48.7B consensus, supported by higher premium yields and CarelonRx product revenue despite anticipated membership declines. The medical cost ratio (MCR) of 89.7% came in roughly 45 basis points better than expected. Worth noting: approximately $0.80 of the beat came from below-the-line items — strip that out and EPS lands around $6.65, still solidly above consensus. Full-year EPS guidance was nudged up to "at least $27" from "at least $26.75" — the modest raise relative to the Q2 beat is the one thing worth watching, but overall execution here was disciplined.
