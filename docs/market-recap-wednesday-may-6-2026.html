MARKETS

The SPX surged +1.46% while the Nasdaq jumped +2.02%. The Dow climbed +1.24% and R2K spiked +1.47%. Brent oil plummeted -7.75% to ~$101.40. Gold rose +3.00% while silver jumped +6.40%. Bitcoin ticked down -0.20%. The DXY fell -0.40%.

RATES & FED

Treasuries saw solid gains with yields falling 5-8 basis points across the curve, driven by the oil price collapse. The market is pricing in essentially zero movement in the Fed Funds Rate over the next 12+ months.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The rally is impressive but increasingly stretched — we've seen a nearly uninterrupted 16% ramp since late March that has largely priced in both Iran optimism and robust earnings. While Trump appears more likely to push for a deal rather than escalate militarily, a vague MOU won't return conditions to pre-Epic Fury levels. Semiconductors look particularly extended, and positioning alone usually doesn't make for a durable thesis. The news flow is good, but it's now reflected in prices.

COMPANY NEWS

AMD delivered solid Q1 upside with EPS of $1.37 vs Street $1.28, driven by higher sales ($10.25B vs $9.88B) including Data Center at $5.77B vs $5.6B expected. Q2 guidance came in bullish at $11.2B vs Street $10.5B.

CVS posted strong Q1 beat with EPS $2.57 vs Street $2.18 on higher revenue (+6.2% to $100.4B vs $95B expected) and improved medical cost ratio. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $7.40 midpoint from prior $7.10.

DIS reported solid FQ2 upside at $1.57 vs Street $1.51, with revenue up 7% to $25.2B vs $24.9B expected. Segment operating margins of 18.3% beat consensus by 70 basis points. Guidance bullish for FQ3 and F26.

UBER delivered modest EBITDA upside at $2.48B vs Street $2.43B, driven by strong bookings (+25% to $53.7B vs $52.9B expected). Q2 guidance robust with $57B bookings vs Street $56.2B.
