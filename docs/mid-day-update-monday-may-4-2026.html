May 04, 2026 · 11:06 AM ET

MARKETS

The SPX gained +0.06% while the Nasdaq climbed +0.22%. The R2K jumped +0.32% but the Dow fell -0.42%. Brent crude surged ~300 basis points to $111.50, while the DXY ticked up 17 basis points. Gold slid -110 basis points and silver dropped -185 basis points. Bitcoin jumped +185 basis points to ~$80.35K.

RATES & FED

Treasuries saw pressure with yields rising 4-5 basis points across the curve amid Iran-related oil volatility. Fed rhetoric continues shifting hawkishly as officials lay groundwork for potential rate hikes if the Iran conflict persists (WSJ).

MARKET OUTLOOK

Markets remain caught between geopolitical volatility and solid earnings execution. The brief Iran missile scare this morning showed how jumpy investors remain, though the quick rebound after CENTCOM's refutation suggests underlying resilience. Key AI earnings this week (AMD, ANET, LITE after Tuesday close) will test whether the AI narrative can weather macro headwinds. The equal-weight SPX outperforming cap-weighted suggests broadening participation beneath the surface volatility.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Early Monday missile strike reports on US Navy ship proved erroneous, refuted by CENTCOM, but highlighted hair-trigger market sensitivity (newsletter observation)

• US will return seized Iranian ship and crew as "confidence-building measure" under Project Freedom initiative (CNN)

• Airlines globally slashed ~2M seats from May schedules responding to Iran-linked oil spike (FT)

• Exxon CEO warns oil markets haven't reflected full Iran war fallout as March/April were protected by tankers already en route (CNBC)

• Putin grows increasingly isolated amid rising assassination fears (FT)

COMPANY NEWS

BRK/B posted solid Q1 numbers though the massive cash hoard (now larger than market caps of all but 24 S&P 500 companies) continues weighing on sentiment. Most investors consider the cash pile too large to deploy absent a market crash, making Berkshire the market's largest anti-AI hedge.

TSN beat FQ2 EPS expectations at 87¢ vs. Street 79¢, driven by higher operating margins (3.64% vs. 3.4% expected) and sales of $13.653B vs. $13.577B estimate. Full-year operating income guidance raised to $2.2-2.4B from prior $2.1-2.3B.

NCLH reported Q1 EBITDA upside at $533MM vs. Street $502MM but slashed full-year guidance, citing not just fuel costs but softer demand as consumers reevaluate European travel plans amid Middle East disruptions.

GBTG will be acquired by Long Lake Management for $9.50/share cash in a $6.3B deal representing ~60% premium to Friday close.

AMZN launched Amazon Supply Chain Services extending its freight, distribution and fulfillment solutions to businesses of all sizes, pressuring logistics stocks.
