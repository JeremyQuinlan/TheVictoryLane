RATES & FED

Treasuries saw modest selling pressure Thursday, with yields rising 1 to 2 basis points across the curve. The market is pricing in roughly one Fed rate hike at some point in H2, with policy expected on hold at the July 29 meeting. Dallas Fed's Logan and Kansas City Fed's Schmid both leaned hawkish, warning inflation doesn't appear headed back to 2% and rates may need to move modestly higher.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

Thursday's headline number is misleading — the real story is a healthy rotation out of tech and into value and cyclicals, with regional banks, REITs, healthcare, transports, and staples all outperforming on the equal-weight measure. The AI narrative is deteriorating in a meaningful way: free cash flow is absent for hyperscalers, capex is swamping operating cash flow, open-source models are taking share, and data center overcapacity fears are mounting. The fact that GE couldn't rally on a genuine beat-and-raise tells you how high the bar is right now. Iran is noise for equities; AI monetization is the actual swing factor, and the next few weeks of earnings will be decisive.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The Iran standoff remains stalemated — Trump won't escalate militarily to the degree needed, and JD Vance confirmed on the Rogan podcast that the White House isn't seeking an Iraq-style occupation or a failed Iranian state, signaling a negotiated outcome remains the base case (Fox News). Iran's lead negotiator signaled negotiations aren't off the table, though Tehran contradicted reports of releasing a US citizen it had held for 1.5 years — Trump had called it a "gesture of goodwill" (WSJ).

- Oil market risks are stacking up: Hormuz flow disruption, removal of Iran sanctions waivers, Ukraine's continued strikes on Russian energy infrastructure, and Tehran reportedly ordering Houthis to shut the Red Sea if the US hits Iranian power infrastructure (Reuters). Black Sea grain shipping is also at risk as Russia targets Ukrainian vessels (FT).

- Trump announced a 25% tariff on certain Brazilian goods citing "unfair trade practices," though major categories — oil/gas, beef, coffee, and aircraft parts — are exempt (NYT).

- South Korea's central bank hiked rates 25 basis points, its first increase since 2023, and warned of further tightening ahead; regulators also moved to curb risks from single-stock leveraged ETFs (WSJ).

- A senior Chinese official (Ma Zhaoxu) will travel to the US the week of July 20 to lay groundwork for an upcoming Xi visit to America (SCMP).

- Chevron is joining a consortium to build a pipeline bypassing the Strait of Hormuz for Iraqi oil (WSJ).

COMPANY NEWS

UNH (UnitedHealth) delivered strong Q2 EPS of $6.38 vs. the Street's $4.89, and raised full-year guidance to $19.75 from "greater than $18.25" — passing through the full beat, unlike ELV which raised by less than its Q2 upside.

STT (State Street) posted Q2 EPS of $3.65 vs. $3.33 expected, with revenue up 17% to $4B, fees up 17%, NII up 18%, and pre-tax margins expanding 470 basis points year-over-year. Full-year fee revenue guidance raised to +12-13% from +7-9%.

GE (GE Aerospace) beat on Q2 EPS ($2.02 vs. $1.86) and revenue ($12.6B adjusted, +24%, vs. $11.8B expected), raised full-year guidance — and the stock still sank, underscoring how punishing the current earnings bar is for large-cap industrials.

ABT (Abbott) posted Q2 EPS of $1.31 vs. $1.28 and nudged full-year guidance to $5.45–$5.60 from $5.38–$5.58. Stock spiked.

CFG (Citizens Financial) beat Q2 EPS at $1.30 vs. $1.25 on slightly better NII and noninterest income, with guidance moving higher.

USB (US Bancorp) earned $1.35 in Q2 (+21.6%) vs. $1.28 expected, with efficiency ratio improving more than 200 basis points year-over-year and provisions ~$60MM below forecast. Full-year guidance raised.

MAN (Manpower) beat Q2 EPS (99c vs. 94c) and revenue ($4.86B vs. $4.71B) on solid execution and FX tailwinds. Q3 guide roughly inline.

JBHT (JB Hunt) reported Q2 EPS of $1.91 (+45%) vs. $1.74, with sales of $3.49B vs. $3.25B. Stock spiked.

TSM (TSMC) gave mixed guidance — revenue strong but margin guidance slightly disappointing, capex again moving higher, and some non-AI end market softness flagged. Stock underperformed despite the AI segment remaining robust.

GOOGL (Google) is behind schedule on its Gemini 3.5 Pro flagship model after it underperformed on internal tests including code generation, raising competitive risk vs. Anthropic and OpenAI (Bloomberg).

LLY (Eli Lilly) announced an acquisition of ATAI (AtaiBeckley) for $6.75/share cash plus a CVR worth up to $2.50.

UBER announced a deal to acquire Delivery Hero at EU41.50/share ($14.8B equity value, or $13.7B net of Uber's existing stake), with 7–9% EPS accretion expected by year three.

EBAY — GME's Ryan Cohen reiterated his intent to pursue a deal for eBay "one way or another" (Bloomberg).

UAL (United Airlines) beat Q2 EPS but elevated energy costs are weighing on forward guidance.

Anthropic is scheduling investor meetings ahead of a planned IPO (CNBC). SPCX (SpaceX)'s rocky public market debut is being watched as a sentiment read on AI-linked IPO appetite, with lockup expirations beginning in early August (Reuters).
