MARKET OUTLOOK

This is a tech-driven selloff, full stop — don't let the Iran headlines distract you. The SOX peaked June 22 and has been grinding lower ever since; Micron is down more than 30% from its post-earnings peak in pre-market alone. The structural concerns are real and compounding: hyperscalers burning cash faster than they generate it, a flood of debt/equity supply overwhelming the market, open-source models (hello, Moonshot) eating into frontier lab economics, and now whispers of data center overcapacity as Meta eyes selling compute externally. Corporate insiders selling at near-record pace (Bloomberg) is the kind of signal you don't ignore. The bull case rests on a negotiated Iran détente and an AI narrative that stabilizes — neither is guaranteed in the near term.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US is striking IRGC infrastructure in Iran — bridges and supply routes used to arm attacks on Hormuz shipping (WSJ, FT). Iran has continued retaliating, and Tehran reportedly ordered the Houthis to shut down the Red Sea if the US hits Iranian power infrastructure (Reuters).

- Energy firms including BP and ConocoPhillips are backing major Iraq investment to route oil around Hormuz; Chevron is joining a consortium to build a bypass pipeline (CNBC, WSJ). The Hormuz disruption is accelerating permanent infrastructure alternatives.

- Bulls note JD Vance's Rogan appearance made clear the White House has no appetite for an Iraq II-style conflict. Bears counter that Trump's incoherent posture and Iran's divided leadership make a negotiated settlement extremely difficult.

- Russia is hitting Ukrainian ships in the Black Sea, putting global grain markets at risk (FT). Brazil is preparing retaliatory measures after the White House threatened a 25% tariff (Reuters).

- China's Xi is positioning Beijing as the global AI leader via an open-source coalition challenging US dominance (Reuters). A senior Chinese official (Ma Zhaoxu) travels to the US the week of July 20 to lay groundwork for Xi's visit (SCMP). China sees no need for large-scale fiscal stimulus at present (Reuters).

- Trump's Thursday night speech was a rehash of election fraud grievances with zero incremental policy content — but the fact it happened raises legitimate concerns about midterm election integrity (NYT). The White House also issued new rules limiting student visa duration, another blow to US higher education (WSJ).
