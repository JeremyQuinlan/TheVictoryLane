MARKET OUTLOOK

The AI earnings gauntlet arrives this week and Wednesday night is the most consequential session — GOOGL will either validate or rattle hyperscaler capex conviction, while NOW will tell us whether IBM's warning about CTOs pivoting budgets away from legacy software toward AI is a real trend or one-off noise. The picks-and-shovels crowd remains firmly in the bull camp on chips, but the author makes a sharp point: OpenAI and Anthropic are the gravitational center of the entire AI ecosystem, and if their growth narratives crack, the shockwave hits semiconductors, hyperscalers, and hardware providers simultaneously. Oil retightening toward $90 Brent adds a macro tax on top of all of this, and the geopolitical backdrop is not getting cleaner anytime soon.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US-Iran conflict escalated materially over the weekend after two American servicemembers were killed in an Iranian strike on a base in Jordan — the first American fatalities from Iranian fire since March. Iran has demonstrated it still holds ample missile stockpiles and is growing "more adept at evading US air defense systems" (NYT). The Pentagon responded swiftly, risking further escalation, while Iran's supreme leader rejected an MOU heavily skewed in its favor and hardliners are increasingly shaping the regime's posture, with the negotiating team being labeled traitors internally (Reuters, CNN).

- Despite the fighting, the author views a negotiated deal as still the most probable outcome — Trump appears to have no appetite for the troop deployments that would actually alter the conflict's trajectory, and the memory of Iraq II weighs heavily. That said, the "YOLO Presidency" thesis (The Atlantic, Haberman/Swan's "Regime Change") posits that a legacy-obsessed Trump, untethered from electoral constraints, could opt for more dramatic action.

- Russia launched one of the largest ballistic missile attacks of the war on Kyiv over the weekend.

- Oil markets are retightening due to reduced Hormuz flows, removal of Iran sanctions waivers, Ukraine's attacks on Russian energy infrastructure, Red Sea disruptions, and the potential end of China's inventory drawdown. Brent is pressing back toward ~$90. Countries are rushing to build alternative supply chains around Hormuz (Bloomberg).

- Xi is still planning a lavish US state visit and the White House is not probing Trump's election-interference allegations against Beijing (CNN, Politico). China's Q2 GDP missed its target range, and Beijing is contemplating stimulus including accelerated bond issuance (FT).

- Trump threatened Canada with incremental tariffs over wildfire smoke blanketing several US cities (FT).

- The ECB is expected to hold this week but may hike again in September given renewed oil pressure (Bloomberg). Singapore is considering cutting hedge fund taxes to compete with Hong Kong for talent (FT).
