RATES & FED

Treasuries are getting hit hard, with yields up 4 to 6 basis points across the curve as the surge in oil drives hawkish repricing. Markets are now pricing roughly 46 basis points of H2 rate hikes, with odds of a move at next week's Fed meeting sitting around 40%.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The headline damage is real but don't overread it — the equal-weight S&P is only down about 22 basis points, which tells you this selloff is concentrated in mega-cap tech and oil-sensitive consumer names, not a broad market breakdown. GOOGL and TSLA are doing the heavy lifting on the downside, while industrials, rails, and defense names are putting up genuinely strong numbers. On Iran, the base case remains a negotiated settlement — Trump has shown little appetite for a full military escalation, and that's the right read. If oil stabilizes around $100 and doesn't push materially higher, the Fed repricing should fade and the market's decent underlying tone reasserts itself heading into a monster earnings week.

COMPANY NEWS

TSLA reported Q2 revenue of $28.23B (+26%, well ahead of the Street's $26.3B), but margins were the problem — GAAP gross margins fell 31 basis points, operating margins dropped 269 basis points, and EBITDA margins collapsed 353 basis points, producing an EPS miss of 33c vs. 51c expected. Musk also talked down Optimus expectations on the call.

GOOGL delivered Q2 revenue of $119.7B (+24%), with operating income of $40.77B roughly inline with the $40.5B consensus, but margins disappointed at 34% vs. the Street's 34.75% expectation. Capex guidance was raised again to $195–205B from $180–190B.

LMT was a standout, with Q2 EPS of $7.94 vs. $7.13 expected, revenue of $20.1B vs. $19.3B, and free cash flow of $2.9B — roughly double consensus. Book-to-bill was approximately 3.2x, and full-year EPS guidance was raised to $29.95–30.65.

RTX reported Q2 EPS of $1.89 (+21% vs. $1.66 expected) and organic sales growth of +16% to $24.7B, with all three segments beating. Backlog grew to $289B from $271B; full-year EPS guide raised to $7.10–7.25.

NOW delivered 23% FXN subscription software growth (vs. 21.85% expected), 21.5% cRPO growth, and 29.5% operating margins vs. 27% expected — a strong rebuttal to legacy software gloom post-IBM.

TXN beat on Q2 EPS ($2.09 ex-items vs. $1.93) with sales of $5.46B (+23%), and Q3 guidance midpoints are ahead — $2.40 EPS vs. $2.17 and $5.9B revenue vs. $5.61B expected.

NSC posted Q2 EPS of $3.52 vs. $3.29, with revenue of $3.46B and operating ratio of 65.5%, about 60 basis points better than expected. Canadian National separately dropped its opposition to the UNP-NSC merger in exchange for expanded network access (WSJ).

URI reported Q2 sales of $4.41B vs. $4.21B and EBITDA of $2.056B vs. $1.91B; full-year EBITDA guide raised to $7.975–8.125B.

ACI (Albertsons) badly missed FQ1, with EPS of 42c vs. 54c expected and comps of -0.8% vs. -0.55% forecast. Full-year guidance was slashed.

BX reported distributable EPS of $1.52 vs. $1.33, inflows of $68.3B vs. $54B expected, and total AUM of $1.34T — though performance revenue (+68% to $793MM) did much of the heavy lifting.

TMO beat Q2 with EPS of $6.03 vs. $5.72 and sales of $11.99B vs. $11.7B, with organic growth of 5% about 200 basis points above consensus.

NDAQ delivered EPS of $1.07 (+25% vs. 99c expected) and revenue of $1.5B (+15% vs. $1.46B).
