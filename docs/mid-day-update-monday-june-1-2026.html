Mid-Day Update · Monday June 1, 2026

Jun 01, 2026 · 11:15 AM ET

MARKETS

The SPX and Nasdaq were essentially flat to slightly down, while the Dow dropped 143 points and the R2K sank 29 points. Brent crude spiked 700 basis points to ~$97.60. Gold fell 170 basis points, silver dropped 100 basis points, and Bitcoin tumbled around 300 basis points.

RATES & FED

Treasuries faced selling pressure with yields climbing 5-8 basis points across the curve as geopolitical tensions flared. Powell warned that political attacks on Fed independence risk undermining monetary credibility, while Warsh continues advocating for "trimmed mean" inflation gauges which currently signal a more benign price backdrop (WSJ, FT).

MARKET OUTLOOK

The modest stock pressure stems largely from Middle East setbacks, but Washington and Tehran still appear keen to avoid escalation — making détente much more likely than not. While geopolitical headlines aren't great and oil is spiking in response, the underlying dynamic suggests cooler heads will prevail. Manufacturing ISM showed decent performance on growth-linked numbers with prices cooling sequentially, though the price index remains very elevated.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Iran suspended talks with the US over Israel's latest Lebanon actions, with the two countries exchanging fresh fire over the weekend — Pentagon conducted "self-defensive strikes" on Iranian military targets, Tehran retaliated by hitting a Kuwaiti military base (NYT)

• Netanyahu ordered IDF to launch deeper incursions into Lebanon as part of the ongoing Hezbollah campaign (Reuters)

• Trump reportedly seeking last-minute changes to the Iranian MOU, though the White House still wants a deal and feels one is close (Axios)

COMPANY NEWS

MGM is rallying after Barry Diller's People Inc. offered $48.30 per share for the 73.9% it doesn't already own, valuing the casino giant's equity at ~$12.3 billion. SAIC spiked on strong Q1 results with EPS of $3.23 versus Street expectations of $2.31, driven largely by EBITDA margins of 11.6% (nearly 200 basis points ahead of consensus) and revenue of $1.91 billion versus estimates of $1.822 billion. NVDA unveiled a new "RTX Spark" superchip for AI-powered Windows PCs, putting it in more direct competition with Intel and AMD, while Commerce closed a loophole allowing Chinese firms to secure advanced Nvidia chips outside mainland China (FT, Reuters). BRK announced it's acquiring TMHC for $72.50 per share in cash. MSI will buy counter-drone leader D-Fend Solutions for $1.5 billion (D-Fend expects $185 million in 2026 sales with >50% growth over three years).
