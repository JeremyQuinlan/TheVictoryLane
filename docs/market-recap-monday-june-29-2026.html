---

MARKET OUTLOOK

Tech's rebound Monday was real but the underlying tensions haven't cleared. The hyperscaler free cash flow problem — capex dramatically outrunning operating cash flow — remains the market's single biggest structural overhang, and any hint from Amazon, Google, or Meta of moderating spend could trigger a savage drawdown in memory and components. The bull case hinges on a durable US-Iran détente translating into lower oil, softer inflation, and eventually a less hawkish Fed — that's still the base case, but it's not a straight line. CQ2 earnings in mid-July will be the real test: if hyperscalers guide capex/revenue ratios lower, expect a violent rotation. Until then, the path of least resistance is modestly higher, though margin debt and leveraged ETF positioning (WSJ) is adding fragility beneath the surface.

- Fed Chair Warsh profiled in depth (NYT); he's reducing forward guidance while making the Fed's reaction function less transparent — a potentially disruptive combination (WSJ). Task force details expected in coming weeks.

- German automakers launching the largest restructuring wave in the industry's history as BYD and Chinese OEMs capture growing Eurozone share (FT).

RKLB will acquire IRDM in an ~$8B deal; IRDM holders receive $27/share cash plus stock, creating a SpaceX-like vertically integrated satellite and launch firm. RKLB says the deal is "significantly accretive to cash flow" (Bloomberg).

MLM is buying Lhoist North America for $13.5B ($7B cash, $6.5B stock), representing 15x EBITDA including synergies.

FBIN spiked after appointing Jesse Singh (ex-AZEK CEO) as CEO effective June 29.

KFY is acquiring AMS for GBP 850MM, expected to be immediately accretive to EPS in year one.

UBER has ended its Waymo robotaxi partnership in Phoenix and will announce a new autonomous partner (Bloomberg).
