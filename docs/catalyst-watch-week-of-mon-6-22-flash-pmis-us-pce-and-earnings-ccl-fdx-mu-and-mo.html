MARKET OUTLOOK

Memory and AI infrastructure remain the most consensus-bullish corners of the market right now, and MU's print Wednesday night will either validate or test that conviction hard. The cruise sector has a cleaner setup now that Iran-related energy fears have receded, though management credibility on guidance will matter. Staples remain broadly unloved — elevated leverage, input cost pressure, and private label encroachment make it hard to own the space even on cheap valuations. Employment data staying firm keeps payroll processors like PAYX from being a disaster, but AI displacement risk is a real long-term overhang that the market hasn't fully priced.

---

GEOPOLITICAL

- The Iran/US deal has been struck, which has brought Brent prices down meaningfully — a direct tailwind for energy-intensive sectors like cruise lines, though uncertainty around deal durability remains (Vital Catalyst Watch).

- Tim Cook recently told the WSJ that Apple would be open to providing capital to memory vendors to increase output, and separately suggested American OEMs should be able to source memory chips from Chinese vendors including CXMT — comments that carry significant implications for both supply dynamics and geopolitical trade positioning in semiconductors (WSJ).

COMPANY NEWS

CCL (Carnival) reports FQ2 Tuesday 6/23 pre-market. Street expects revenue +5.6% to $6.684B, EPS of $0.34, and EBITDA of $1.485B. Management guided for FXN yield growth of ~2%. The drop in Brent is a potential upside driver, but don't expect full guidance flow-through given deal uncertainty on Iran.

CBRS (Cerebras) reports FQ1 Tuesday 6/23 post-close. The stock is well off its post-IPO high but sentiment stays bullish on AI infrastructure demand. The number itself matters less than guidance — Street is modeling a steep ramp: $825MM in '26, $2.8B in '27, $7.2B in '28.

FDX (FedEx) reports FQ4 Tuesday 6/23 post-close. Street penciling in EPS of $5.95, total revenue +8.3%, and operating margins of ~8.6%. Aggressive cost cuts, the FedEx Freight spin, and capital return are the bull levers here.

PAYX (Paychex) reports FQ4 Wednesday 6/24 pre-market. Street expects EPS of $1.31 on revenue of $1.6B. The Paycor deal synergies and mix shift away from payroll are positives; AI displacement and macro employment softness are the bear flags, though the latest jobs report took some edge off the latter.

MU (Micron) reports FQ3 Wednesday 6/24 post-close. This is the week's marquee print. Street models sales +276% to $35B, gross margins of 81.6%, operating margins of 77.6%, and EPS +950% to ~$20, with capex of ~$7.4B and free cash flow of ~$11.8B. Peak EPS estimates sit around $115–120 in F27/F28. Capex guidance is the critical variable — any sharp supply ramp feeds the cyclicality bear case and pressures memory pricing, but paradoxically helps hyperscaler FCF. CXMT capacity expansion from China is the background risk worth watching.

DRI (Darden) reports FQ4 Thursday 6/25 pre-market. Street expects EPS +22.5% to $3.65, revenue +14% to $3.72B, restaurant margins of ~23.4%, and aggregate comps +4.3% (Olive Garden +3.75%). Execution has been strong enough to offset macro headwinds.

MKC (McCormick) reports FQ2 Thursday 6/25 pre-market. Street looks for organic revenue growth of +1.7%, operating margins of 15.8%, and EPS of $0.69. The Unilever deal adds synergy potential but also elevated leverage, and broader staples sentiment is deeply negative.
