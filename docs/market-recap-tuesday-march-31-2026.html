Market Recap · Tuesday March 31, 2026

Mar 31, 2026 · 04:04 PM ET

MARKETS

SPX climbed +2.91% Tuesday with solid gains across the board. Nasdaq surged +3.83%, Dow advanced +2.49%, and R2K jumped +3.41%. DXY slumped 60 basis points while Brent fell 270 basis points to $104.50. Precious metals rallied hard with gold up 385 basis points and silver advancing 735 basis points. Bitcoin ramped 175 basis points to around $67.75K.

RATES & FED

Treasuries saw gains with yields dropping 3-7 basis points across the curve. The market is pricing in roughly 7 basis points of Fed Funds cuts this year, reflecting expectations for extended policy accommodation.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The Iran deescalation narrative is driving serious momentum, and we're buying it. Trump's confirmation that he can exit the conflict without militarily securing Hormuz dramatically reduces ground troop deployment risk (WSJ). Even with stagflationary pressures building globally, the prospect of reduced geopolitical tension trumps everything else right now. The $90 billion in M&A activity this week signals corporate confidence despite macro volatility. Bulls are in control until proven otherwise.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Iran acknowledged that diplomatic messages are being exchanged with the US, with the country's president stating readiness to end the war

• Trump confirmed WSJ reporting about exiting the Middle East conflict without militarily reopening Hormuz, significantly reducing odds of substantial ground troop deployment

• Iran continues targeting critical Gulf infrastructure, striking a Kuwaiti oil tanker despite diplomatic overtures

• USS George HW Bush is replacing the USS Gerald Ford in the Middle East as the US maintains military presence during negotiations

COMPANY NEWS

MKC reported Q1 adjusted EPS of 66 cents versus Street expectations of 59 cents, with revenue beating at $1.87 billion versus $1.79 billion expected. The company formally announced plans to combine with Unilever's food business, contributing to this week's $90 billion M&A tally.

BIIB will acquire APLS for $41 per share cash at closing (approximately $5.6 billion) plus a contingent value right worth up to $2 per share. Apellis closed Monday at $17.09.

LLY announced acquisition of CNTA for $38 per share cash (roughly $6.3 billion) plus a CVR worth up to $9 per share ($1.5 billion) for total potential value of $47. Centessa closed Monday at $27.58.

CEG shares underperformed after providing 2026 EPS guidance slightly below expectations, with the implied 2029 forecast also disappointing at their analyst meeting.

FDS delivered Q2 EPS of $4.46 versus Street $4.37, driven by 6.8% organic revenue growth versus 6% expected, though operating margins fell 230 basis points year-over-year to 35% versus Street 35.5%.
