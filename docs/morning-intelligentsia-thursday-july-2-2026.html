MARKET OUTLOOK

The chip selloff is real but the rotation story is more important — money isn't leaving equities, it's moving from pick-and-shovel AI hardware names into software, hyperscalers, and non-tech sectors, which is exactly why European indices are green while Nasdaq futures lag. The catalyst cocktail (Meta neocloud report, Apple sourcing Chinese memory, Samsung/SK Hynix capacity expansions, and GLM-5.2's DeepSeek-style shock) is hitting a positioning structure that was dangerously crowded. The complacency among chip bulls — many still calling this a buyable dip — suggests the drawdown has further to run. Meanwhile, software and hyperscalers could continue to squeeze higher. The June jobs print (+113K consensus vs. +172K prior) and Tesla Q2 deliveries (~400K consensus) are the last meaningful catalysts this week.

COMPANY NEWS

AAPL is planning five new iPhone models for H2:26 and H1:27 — including higher volumes of foldables — to exploit the component supply crunch and take market share, per Nikkei. Separately, Apple is in talks to source DRAM and NAND from China's CXMT and YMT for devices sold in China to protect margins amid surging memory costs (Bloomberg).

GOOGL lost its appeal at Europe's top court, which upheld the EU's €4.1B Android antitrust fine imposed by the European Commission (CNBC).

KO is moving forward with an IPO of its Indian bottling unit, targeting roughly $1B in proceeds (Bloomberg). Carlsberg separately filed confidentially for a ~$700M India unit IPO (Bloomberg).

NVDA will offer GPU access to startups in exchange for a cut of future cloud revenues, extending its balance sheet to drive sales growth (The Information).

SMCI pushed back on reports about a Taiwan chip-smuggling investigation, asserting it is cooperating with officials and that individual employees — not the company — are the targets (Digitimes).

META has shifted toward a hyperscaler/neocloud model, a move that raised fresh questions about data center capacity constraints across the industry.

SAP is restricting hiring and travel to fund what it calls a "significant" AI product push (Bloomberg).

SPCX (SpaceX): the working assumption among those close to Musk is that SpaceX will acquire a US mobile carrier — T-Mobile cited as a possibility — to expand in communications (Sources). WSJ separately reported SpaceX showed investors a prototype AI device.

OpenAI has discussed offering the US government a 5% equity stake to ease White House tensions (FT). SoftBank is renewing its push for a $10B margin loan secured by its OpenAI stake, with additional concessions offered to lenders (Reuters).

CBOE is seeking to list prediction market-style binary contracts tied to corporate earnings metrics (Bloomberg). UBS is exploring offering retail banking services in the US to capture wealthy American clients (FT).

Takeda struck an AI drug-discovery deal with InSilico worth up to $600M (WSJ). Bayer consolidated its US glyphosate business into a new entity called Ruveon LLC, widely seen as a liability-capping move (Bloomberg). KNDS, the European tank maker, has delayed its IPO citing unfavorable market conditions (FT).

AEO announced a CFO transition and reiterated Q2 and full-year guidance.
