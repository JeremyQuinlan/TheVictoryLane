MARKETS

The SPX fell -0.41% while the Nasdaq sank -0.89%. The Dow dipped -0.37% and the R2K ticked down -0.37%. Treasuries saw modest pressure. The DXY rallied 0.25%. Brent jumped ~3.80% to ~$105.80. Gold slumped -0.85% while silver dropped -2.65%. Bitcoin fell -0.85% to ~$77.8K.

RATES & FED

Yields rose 1-2 basis points across the curve. The market is pricing in just 6 basis points worth of Fed cuts this year, reflecting increasingly hawkish expectations as policy shifts toward reduced monthly purchase pace.

MARKET OUTLOOK

Our view on the SPX right here is (at best) neutral. While we're hopeful about a US-Iran deal, this is largely anticipated by investors already — meaning consummation won't trigger meaningful further gains. There's still escalation risk (stocks will fall much more on "bombing resumes" headlines than they'll rally on "deal reached" ones). Even the best-case outcome seems like a vague, non-binding understanding that fails to address core underlying sources of contention. The stagflationary fallout from what's already happened will persist for months, with Hormuz never returning to its previous state as markets permanently price in waterway risk premiums.

GEOPOLITICAL

Stocks took a sharp mid-day dive on Middle East noise — chatter about a lead "moderate" departing Iran's negotiating team and speculation about resumed Israeli strikes — before rebounding when the situation appeared relatively "status quo" by close. Trump ordered the US Navy to fire on any Iranian boat laying mines in Hormuz, while Axios reported Iran laid more mines this week. Israel's Defense Minister said the country was ready "to return Iran to the dark ages" (Axios, various). The White House accused Beijing of engaging in a "deliberate, industrial-scale" campaign to steal American AI IP, threatening to undermine the relatively stable US-China relationship (FT). It could take up to 6 months to fully clear Hormuz of mines once the conflict formally ends (Washington Post).

COMPANY NEWS

TSLA reported solid Q1 EPS upside at 41¢ vs. Street 38¢, driven by strong margin expansion (GMs rose 478 basis points, EBITDA margins climbed 183 basis points) and healthy free cash flow, though sales fell slightly short due to energy generation/storage pressure. AXP delivered solid EPS beat ($4.28 vs. Street $4.03) on higher revenue ($18.9B vs. $18.6B) with Card Member spending growing 9% FX-adjusted — the highest quarterly growth in three years. CMCSA posted strong upside across key metrics including EPS (79¢ vs. 72¢) and sales ($31.45B vs. $30.3B), with broadband losses better than feared (-65K vs. -160K) and Peacock subs rising 12% to 46M. NOW disappointed with modest shortfall in subscription revenue growth (+19% vs. Street 20.4%), hurt by 75 basis points headwind from delayed Middle East deal closures. META formally announced plans to slash headcount by ~10% or ~8K people while not filling ~6K open roles (Bloomberg). MSFT is offering voluntary buyouts to certain employees for the first time in company history (CNBC). TXN and STM both surged on earnings, leading semiconductor strength.
