MARKET OUTLOOK

The earnings tsunami ahead looks like a proper litmus test for market resilience, with heavyweight names from Berkshire to Disney reporting in rapid succession. The semiconductor rally (fueled by those robust TXN/STM/NXPI prints) has investors betting on broad-based industrial and auto chip recovery — a thesis that gets stress-tested with ON Semi, AMD, ANET, and Microchip all reporting. COMPANY NEWS

BRK reports Saturday with the stock mired in significant underperformance versus the SPX. Key focus areas include Abel's buyback activation during Q1, capital deployment challenges with the massive cash hoard, BNSF performance following strong railroad earnings across the sector, and insurance profitability amid cooling prices and rising competition.

ON delivers Q1 results Monday after close with sentiment turning bullish on accelerating revenue growth and margin expansion expectations. Street looking for sales +4.8% to $6.283B with EPS +25.7% to $2.95, gross margins of 39.6%, and operating margins of 21.1%.

PLTR reports Monday night with revenue growth and margin expansion driving continued bullish sentiment despite the pullback from October highs. Street expects revenue +74% to $1.53B with operating margins of 57%.

PFE faces Tuesday morning with the most negative sentiment among large-cap pharma peers due to an unappealing pipeline and COVID exposure, though aggressive cost cutting and elevated dividend provide downside support. Street looking for EPS of 72c with revenue +0.8% to $13.829B.

PYPL has nearly recovered from its devastating February earnings collapse thanks to activist/M&A speculation and aggressive buybacks, though fundamental sentiment remains negative on branded checkout share loss concerns. Street expects revenue +3.5% to $8.06B with TPV of ~$447.2B and EPS of $1.27.

DIS reports Wednesday morning with neutral-to-positive sentiment driven by Experiences and Streaming strength, though CEO succession uncertainty and linear TV's secular decline remain overhangs. Street looking for EPS of $1.50 with total revenue of $24.83B.

UBER faces Wednesday morning earnings with autonomous driving displacement risk as a secular overhang, though management is positioning these vehicles as network complements rather than disruptors. Street expects revenue +15.4% to $13.31B with bookings +23.6% to $52.9B.

MCD reports Thursday morning with fairly positive sentiment as management flexes pricing power through strategic discounts. Street looking for total comps +4% with operating margins of 43.3% and EPS of $2.75.

ABNB delivers Thursday night results with healthy travel demand indicators supporting positive sentiment. Street expects revenue +15.3% to $2.6B with EBITDA +16% to $484MM and bookings +13.5% to $27.81B.
