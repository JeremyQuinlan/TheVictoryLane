RATES & FED

Treasuries are under pressure with yields up 1–4 basis points across the curve, and Fed expectations are tilting hawkish — the market is now pricing in 38 basis points of H2 hikes. The catalyst is the oil spike, with Brent surging past $91 on Middle East escalation fears.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The cap-weight S&P is masking a more fragile tape — the equal-weight index is essentially flat while chips and tech infrastructure carry the load, which is not a particularly healthy breadth picture. That said, the beat-and-raise cadence from this earnings season is holding up, and Wednesday's after-close slate (GOOGL, TSLA, TXN, NOW, IBM) is the real verdict on whether the AI-capex narrative stays intact. Jamie Dimon's warning that markets are underpricing geopolitical and fiscal risks (CNBC) deserves respect — he said he'd be a buyer of neither stocks nor long-dated Treasuries. Oil above $91 with no clear Iran off-ramp is a genuine macro headwind that the equity market is currently choosing to ignore.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US-Iran situation is at a critical inflection: a 10-day ceasefire is being floated, but the alternative is dramatic escalation with the US acting alongside Israel (Axios). Current US airstrikes are unlikely to change Iranian behavior per US intelligence assessments (Washington Post).

- Israel believes Iran has relocated significant covert nuclear infrastructure to Pickaxe Mountain; Trump has said he's prepared to bomb it (WSJ).

- Strait of Hormuz traffic has slumped materially since the US reimposed its blockade on Iran, feeding the Brent spike (CNBC).

- China is cracking down on AI exports — model weights and chips — while simultaneously launching a coordinated market rescue with record inflows into tech ETFs after recent equity weakness (FT, Bloomberg). China also vowed to hit its full-year growth target despite a Q2 shortfall (Bloomberg).

- The White House is weighing options to block or significantly restrict American companies' use of open-source Chinese AI models, a move that would benefit Anthropic and OpenAI (Axios, WSJ).

- Trump is set to impose a 50% tariff on Canadian hockey sticks and wine.

COMPANY NEWS

GM crushed Q2 estimates with EPS of $3.57 vs. the Street's $3.19 and revenue of $48B vs. $46.6B expected. Full-year EPS guidance midpoint moves to $13 from $12.50, driven by strong execution, lower EV losses, growing software/services revenue, and pricing power.

MMM delivered Q2 EPS of $2.40 vs. $2.25 expected on 5.4% organic growth vs. 3.2% expected, with operating margins at 24.9%. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $8.80–$8.95 from $8.50–$8.70.

HAS posted Q2 EPS of $1.28 vs. $1.13, with sales of $1.13B beating the $1.066B consensus across both physical toys and gaming. Full-year guidance raised.

CCK beat Q2 EPS by 15%+ at $2.49 vs. $2.16, raising full-year guidance to $8.30–$8.50 from $7.90–$8.30 on solid can demand.

DHR beat Q2 EPS ($1.94 vs. $1.84) on better revenue ($6.26B vs. $6.1B) but trimmed its full-year sales guide to +3–4% from +3–6% due to softness in respiratory testing, and diagnostics operating margins fell 160 basis points year-over-year.

DHI beat FQ3 EPS ($3.20 vs. $2.98) but cut full-year revenue guidance to $32.5–$33B from $33.5–$34.5B amid persistent housing headwinds and light orders.

NOC beat Q2 EPS ($7.68 vs. $6.83) largely on a 6.3% tax rate, but segment operating margins fell 120 basis points to 10.6%.

SYF beat Q2 EPS significantly ($2.59 vs. $2.12) driven by lower provisions, with full-year EPS guidance raised to $9.25–$9.50.

MSCI missed Q2 EPS and revenue ($4.94/$867MM vs. $4.99/$870MM) while raising full-year expense guidance — stock sliding.

ORCL credit default swap costs hit near 18-year highs amid balance sheet concerns and massive capex commitments; the company may also be forced to post significant collateral for its Wisconsin data center project (Bloomberg, FT).

TSM plans to raise chipmaking prices up to 10% in 2027, with advanced nodes potentially higher, to offset rising costs (Nikkei).

KHC struck a supply and licensing deal with Disney to serve Kraft products at North American Disney properties and use Disney characters on packaging (WSJ).

UTZ agreed to be acquired by Intersnack for $14.25/share in cash — roughly a 90% premium to Monday's close.

IEP is selling Pep Boys to Mavis Tire Express for ~$700MM in cash (WSJ).

MSFT expanded its partnership with Mistral, integrating Mistral's models across Azure, Copilot Studio, and Microsoft Foundry.

SCHW beat Q2 EPS ($1.62 vs. $1.55) with $120B in core net new assets and 1.4M new brokerage account openings.

STLD posted Q2 EBITDA of $921MM vs. $884MM expected on $6.1B in sales vs. $5.58B, benefiting from healthy steel fundamentals.

ZION headline Q2 EPS of $3.05 included $1.31 in one-time gains; adjusted EPS of $1.74 beat the $1.63 consensus but quality was low — provisions drove the beat while NII/NIM missed.
