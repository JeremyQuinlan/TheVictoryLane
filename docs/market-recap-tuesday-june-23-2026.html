RATES & FED

Treasuries posted modest gains Tuesday, with yields dipping 1–3 basis points across the curve. The market is currently pricing roughly 38 basis points of Fed hikes in H2 — a hawkish tilt that's being reinforced by the view that the recent inflation acceleration runs deeper than just energy prices. If that read is correct, the Fed could be forced into 1–3 hikes rather than the more benign 0–2 scenario bulls are hoping for.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The damage Tuesday was real but concentrated — tech and chips bore the brunt while equal-weight S&P outperformed Nasdaq by a wide margin, which tells you this isn't a broad market breakdown (yet). The AI narrative is the single most important variable in markets right now, more so than Iran or the Fed, and it's moving in the wrong direction: frontier model economics are deteriorating, Chinese open-source models are eating token share, and the entire data center buildout is dangerously dependent on capital markets generosity. SpaceX's post-IPO slump is a warning shot — if appetite for fresh AI equity and debt issuance cools even marginally, infrastructure capex guidance will follow. The bull case rests on a durable Middle East détente driving oil lower, which removes pressure on inflation and yields, and Corporate America has earned the benefit of the doubt given how strong Q1 earnings were. But until AI finds its footing, expect more sector rotation into defensives and rate-sensitives.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US-Iran MOU continues to point toward sustained détente, with oil supplies potentially expanding beyond pre-war levels via higher OPEC+ quotas, Iranian sanction removal, UAE's OPEC exit, and increased Venezuelan and US production (newsletter).

- Russia is under severe geopolitical pressure: Ukraine strikes are disrupting domestic energy supply, cities are rationing fuel, and Trump is reportedly "hugely impressed" by Ukraine's battlefield advances and "warming" to Kyiv (FT). A separate report flagged Putin potentially drawing Belarus deeper into the war (WSJ).

- The ECB's chief economist Philip Lane warned Tuesday that inflation risks remain elevated post-Iran MOU, with energy price shocks feeding through to broader prices and CPI likely to stay above 2% "for quite some time" (ECB).

- The US and Qatar warned the EU that new methane emission rules risk triggering a European gas supply crunch (FT).

- South Korean regulators flagged concern over leveraged ETF popularity amid a record domestic equity rally driven almost entirely by SK Hynix and Samsung AI demand (Reuters/FT).

COMPANY NEWS

FY guidance was mixed: FXN yield guide trimmed, but EPS/EBITDA largely unchanged. Stock underperformed on the nuanced print.
