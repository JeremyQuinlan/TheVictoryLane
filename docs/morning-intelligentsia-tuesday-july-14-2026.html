MARKET OUTLOOK

Today is the calm before the storm — major bank earnings, June CPI, and Warsh's House testimony all hit simultaneously, kicking off what will be a multi-week gauntlet of market-moving events running into August. Bank sentiment is bullish on healthy fundamentals, strong capital markets, and a permissive regulatory backdrop, but valuations are rich and positioning is crowded, which likely caps near-term upside even on solid prints. Chips and semis remain the most fragile pocket of the market — psychology is still rattled after Monday's Korea memory selloff, and ASML and TSMC earnings (Wednesday and Thursday, respectively) are must-watch events. Brent approaching $90 puts the White House in an uncomfortable spot, and any Trump "we'll probably make a deal" comments today could generate volatility across energy and risk assets. Bottom line: it's a week where execution on positioning matters more than adding new exposure.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US launched fresh airstrikes on Iran overnight; Iran retaliated by targeting American military facilities in the region and ships in the Strait of Hormuz (Politico). Trump formally notified Congress of a new war authorization on July 7, restarting the 60-day clock.

- The situation is stalemated and increasingly absurd — Iran rejected an MOU heavily skewed in its favor, Tehran appears deeply divided internally, and Trump's "Guardian of the Hormuz Strait" proposal with a 20% surtax (~$30M/vessel) is being widely dismissed (WSJ). Neither side has a coherent exit strategy.

- Bulls note that some type of negotiated settlement remains inevitable; there is talk of two Hormuz lanes (one Oman-controlled, one Iranian) as a potential off-ramp, and Dubai is reportedly planning a new port to reduce Hormuz dependence (FT).

- Fighting has also erupted between Saudi Arabia and the Houthis in Yemen, raising fresh concerns about Red Sea shipping lanes.

- China's strategic inventory drawdown — which helped suppress Brent through the spring — may be reversing; if Beijing begins refilling stockpiles, oil markets could tighten sharply (NYT).

- Iranian nationalism is rising domestically, further complicating diplomat efforts to reach a Washington deal (WSJ).

- Israeli polls show Gadi Eisenkot overtaking Netanyahu ahead of the October 27 election (FT).

- The Senate may pass a Russia sanctions bill in bipartisan fashion (Axios).
