RATES & FED

Treasuries have a modest bid with yields down 1–2 basis points across the curve. Markets are pricing in 38 basis points of Fed hikes in H2 2026, roughly unchanged from Tuesday. Notably, the failure of yields to drop meaningfully despite sharply lower oil prices signals growing concern that the inflation impulse is broader than just energy.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The two catalysts this week — Micron earnings tonight and May PCE Thursday morning — will do more to set the tape's near-term direction than anything else on the calendar. The bull case rests on a sustained Iran détente compressing oil and eventually yields, but the bear case is getting louder: Australia's trimmed mean CPI surprised to the upside, BOJ is hawkish, and the Fed looks increasingly likely to hike 1–3 times in H2 rather than standing pat. FedEx margins disappointed and Cerebras, while beating on operating income, couldn't sustain elevated expectations — net-net, Tuesday night earnings were negative. The next major inflection point after PCE is the June jobs report (July 2) and CPI (July 14), and between now and then the macro data will either validate or undercut the soft-landing narrative.

---

GEOPOLITICAL

- Iran/Middle East: Bulls point to growing evidence of sustained US-Iran détente — higher OPEC+ quotas, removal of Iranian energy sanctions, UAE departing OPEC, and rising Venezuelan/US output all point to structurally lower oil. Qatar's PM confirmed the country will resume normal LNG production within weeks (FT). Bears counter that negotiations remain fraught, Washington and Tehran still offer conflicting accounts of what was agreed, and the Senate voted 50–48 to direct Trump to withdraw forces — a mostly symbolic move that could complicate White House diplomacy (WSJ).

- Russia/Ukraine: Russia is geopolitically cornered — fuel shortages are spreading domestically, Ukrainian strikes are disrupting energy infrastructure, and Trump is reportedly "hugely impressed" by Kyiv's battlefield advances (FT). Putin's desperation is real, and a WSJ report flagged Russia pressuring Belarus to open a new front in the war (WSJ).

- Chinese robots: Commerce Secretary Lutnick warned CEOs that the White House could soon act to block imports of Chinese robots (Politico).

- China/yuan: China is successfully internationalizing the yuan, meaningfully complicating Washington's ability to impose financial sanctions (WSJ).

- Germany defense: Rheinmetall tumbled in European trading after the German government scrapped plans for a multibillion-euro warship program (FT).

COMPANY NEWS

FDX (FedEx) posted FQ4 EPS of $6.31, beating estimates, with revenue of $25B — nearly $1B above the $24.06B consensus. The problem: operating margin came in at 8.4%, about 25 basis points below consensus. For C2026, the company guides to 11% revenue growth, ~$17.50 adjusted EPS at the midpoint, and $1B in opportunistic buybacks (fiscal year shifting to calendar year, post-Freight spinoff adds noise).

CBRS (Cerebras) reported FQ1 operating loss of just -$3.5M vs. the Street's -$31.5M forecast — a substantial beat — with core revenue of $191.3M (+92% Y/Y, +12% Q/Q) vs. consensus $181.4M. Q2 guide of $194M in core sales beats the Street's $173M; full-year guide of ~$860M tops the $825M consensus. That said, the stock's elevated expectations made the beat feel narrower than the numbers suggest.

KBH (KB Home) missed FQ2 EPS by a tick — 43 cents vs. 45 cents expected — though KPIs were broadly inline and the FY guide holds up: 10.5–11K home deliveries with gross margins of 16.1–16.5%, above the Street's ~15.8% estimate. "Could have been worse" sums it up.

NKE (Nike) announced CFO David Denton (ex-Pfizer) takes over from Matthew Friend in August. Upcoming FQ4 (June 30 AMC) will benefit from a tariff refund not in prior guidance; ex that item, results are "generally inline." The CFO transition adds anxiety ahead of F27 guidance, where the Street models EPS jumping to ~$1.84 vs. ~$1.50 in F26.

MU (Micron) reports tonight after the close — the week's single biggest equity catalyst.

WMT (Walmart) is paying $1.4B for French ad-tech firm Vibe.co, its biggest deal in two years, as it builds out its digital advertising business (WSJ).

GME (GameStop) dropped CEO Ryan Cohen's performance award to refocus attention on the eBay acquisition bid.

QCOM (Qualcomm) is in talks to provide custom chip-design services to ByteDance as it seeks to diversify beyond smartphones (Reuters). Analyst day is at 2:15pm ET today.

NVDA (Nvidia) black-market chip prices in China have spiked amid tighter export restrictions and surging domestic demand (FT).

GOOGL (Google) replaces Verizon in the Dow Jones Industrial Average effective June 29 — a mixed honor historically; Dow inclusion has often been a contrary indicator for tech names.

HONA (Honeywell Aerospace) joins the S&P 500 on June 29, replacing CAG (Conagra), which is down over 20% YTD and carries a 10.4% dividend yield that many expect will eventually be cut.

PLD (Prologis) was rebuffed in a ~$16.6B takeover approach for UK REIT Segro, whose shares spiked on the news (WSJ).

SPSC (SPS Commerce) surged after Reuters reported the company is exploring a sale amid activist pressure.

VRNS (Varonis) spiked on Bloomberg reports of takeout interest from Blackstone, Thoma Bravo, and Vista Equity.

MS (Morgan Stanley) private credit fund capped redemptions at 5% — consistent with previously disclosed terms; the shareholder letter was actually relatively sanguine on redemption trends.

ICLR (ICON) posted FQ1 results with a book-to-bill of 1.42x — the standout metric — and reiterated F26 guidance.
