MARKETS

No price data provided in this edition. This issue is a forward-looking catalyst watch for the week of July 13.

---

RATES & FED

No Treasury or Fed funds data was included in this edition. The key rates event this week is Fed Chair nominee Kevin Warsh's congressional testimony — House on Tuesday 7/14, Senate on Wednesday 7/15. Don't expect fireworks; Warsh's default mode is opaque and noncommittal, though he'll have June CPI and PPI in hand when he speaks.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The macro and earnings calendar this week is genuinely loaded, and the market's reaction to each print will be amplified by elevated sentiment and rich valuations across cyclicals and financials. Banks are the most crowded long in the room right now — fundamentals are fine, but when positioning is this stretched, even a solid quarter can disappoint. The CPI print Tuesday morning is the week's linchpin: headline is expected to cool 40 basis points month-over-month to +3.8% with core ticking down 10 basis points to +2.8% — anything hotter resets rate cut expectations hard. China's Q2 GDP (seen slowing to +4.5% from +5.0% in Q1) adds another variable for global growth bulls to navigate.

---

GEOPOLITICAL

- China trade and growth data dominate the international macro slate: June imports/exports land Monday night, followed by Q2 GDP, June retail sales, and industrial production Tuesday night. The Street sees GDP cooling meaningfully, retail sales staying muted, and IP holding near 4.5% — a soft but not alarming picture (Bloomberg consensus).

TSM reports Thursday 7/16 early morning. GE reports Thursday 7/16 pre-market. Consensus revenue +16.3% to $11.8B, operating margins ~22%, EPS ~$1.86. Secular tailwinds intact; the only risk is the bar is impossibly high and valuation is stretched.

UNH reports Thursday 7/16 pre-market. Consensus EPS +18.5% to $4.84, revenue roughly flat at ~$110.7B, medical cost ratio ~88.5% (a 100 basis point year-over-year improvement). Hemsley's turnaround is gaining credibility.

NFLX reports Thursday 7/16 after the close. Consensus revenue +13.5% to $12.57B, EPS $0.79, operating income $4.12B, free cash flow ~$2.65B. Competitive pressure from Disney, Amazon, Warner-Paramount, and YouTube is the key sentiment overhang.

TRV reports Friday 7/17 pre-market. Consensus EPS ~$5.05, combined ratio 96.1%, underlying combined ratio 85.2%, NPWs -1.1% to $11.41B.
