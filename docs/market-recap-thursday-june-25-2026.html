RATES & FED

Treasuries were mixed — 2-year yields dipped 2 basis points while 10-year yields held flat and 30-year yields nudged up 2 basis points. The Fed pricing held steady, with markets still embedding roughly 34 basis points of hikes in H2. Inflation running well above target, and yields refusing to rally despite lower oil, tells you the market isn't buying the "transitory" story this time.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The Micron blowout roared back AI sentiment, but the zero-sum read is becoming the dominant framework — what's great for memory is increasingly a headwind for hyperscalers and OEMs, as evidenced by Apple and Microsoft hiking hardware prices within 24 hours of Micron's print. Software and services remain the most despised corner of the market, and that's unlikely to change until free cash flow from hyperscalers actually materializes rather than being entirely dependent on capital markets and circular financing. Inflation may have peaked on a year-over-year basis in May, but it's tracking far above the Fed's target in absolute terms and AI is now a structural inflationary force — not just an energy or Middle East story. The market is pricing in 1-2 hikes in H2, and with yields unable to fall despite cheaper oil, the path of least resistance for rates is sideways-to-higher. Expect tape bombs out of the Middle East to continue; the détente trajectory is real, but the journey is decidedly nonlinear.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Iran attacked a cargo ship in the Strait of Hormuz, testing the framework of Trump's deal to reopen the waterway (WSJ). Tehran is simultaneously angling to impose a toll on Hormuz transit that could generate ~$40 billion annually, with plans to share proceeds with Gulf states to build regional support (WSJ).

- Brent hit an intra-day low near $72 before rebounding to $75.40 on the Hormuz attack headlines. Notably, Hormuz shipping insurance costs had already plunged from ~5% to ~2% of ship value in recent days before the incident (FT).

- Israel and Lebanon are in active talks over a US-backed proposal for Israel to cede portions of its southern buffer zone back to Lebanon, a move that would help defuse regional tensions (Reuters).

- Iraq is weighing an exit from OPEC+ unless its production quota is significantly raised, which would add to a growing list of potential supply increases including higher OPEC quotas, the UAE's departure, and full removal of Iranian energy sanctions (Reuters).

COMPANY NEWS

MU (Micron) posted a blowout FQ3 with FQ4 guidance well above consensus. The standout detail was incremental color on strategic customer agreements (SCAs), signaling the EPS boom has structural legs rather than being a one-quarter phenomenon — a key PE multiple re-rating catalyst per the bull case.

QCOM (Qualcomm) raised its F29 non-handset revenue target to ~$40 billion (roughly 2x the prior goal), including more than $15 billion from AI data center infrastructure, with F29 EPS guidance of >$18 versus the Street at ~$14.75.

AAPL (Apple) raised Mac prices 15–20% and iPad prices 15–25%, with iPhone hikes potentially coming later this year, explicitly citing rising memory costs (WSJ).

MSFT (Microsoft) announced Xbox console price increases, also citing soaring memory expenses (WSJ).

JPM (JPMorgan) named Doug Petno and Troy Rohrbaugh as Co-Presidents effective immediately; Marianne Lake is retiring. Rohrbaugh is considered the frontrunner to eventually succeed Jamie Dimon, who plans to remain CEO roughly three more years before transitioning to executive chairman (WSJ).

TECH (Bio-Techne) agreed to be acquired by Merck KGaA for $73/share cash, a deal valued at $11.3 billion enterprise value versus TECH's $59 close the prior day.

BB (BlackBerry) delivered strong FQ1 upside — EBITDA of $36.3MM vs. the Street at $21MM on revenue of $152.9MM vs. $137MM expected — and raised full-year EBITDA guidance to $119–139MM from $110–130MM.

AYI (Acuity) beat FQ2 estimates with EPS of $5.31 vs. $5.18 expected on revenue of $1.198B vs. $1.177B, with operating margins at 18.7% vs. 18.5% consensus.

SNX (TD SYNNEX) posted a major FQ2 beat — EPS $4.85 vs. $4.14 expected — on revenue that jumped 31% to $19.575B vs. the $16.77B consensus, with FQ3 guidance midpoints ahead of Street.

DRI (Darden) eked out a slight FQ4 EPS beat ($3.66 vs. $3.63) with overall comps of +4.6% ahead of plan, but Olive Garden missed by ~100 basis points (+2.4% vs. +3.4% expected) and F27 EPS guidance midpoint of $11.10–11.35 came in light vs. the $11.41 consensus.

MKC (McCormick) beat FQ2 EPS handily at 80c vs. 69c expected, driven by margin expansion and a 160 basis point year-over-year tax rate reduction to 22.5%, with organic revenue +1.7% roughly inline and full-year guidance reaffirmed.
