MARKET OUTLOOK

The mega-cap earnings wave delivered largely strong results, with Google leading the pack thanks to explosive Cloud growth (+63%) and resilient Search business. While Meta's user decline and elevated capex spending ($125-144B vs prior $115-135B) raised eyebrows, the overall tech landscape remains robust. The real story is the emerging custom silicon opportunity at Qualcomm, which could unlock AVGO/MRVL-style growth despite soft June guidance—exactly the kind of pivot that creates new market leaders.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Meta reported quarterly user decline driven by internet disruptions in Iran and WhatsApp restrictions in Russia, marking the first geopolitical impact on major platform metrics this cycle.

COMPANY NEWS

GOOGL posted stellar Q1 results with operating income of $39.7B vs Street $36.1B, driven by 200 basis point margin expansion to 36.1%. Cloud revenue surged 63% to $20B (vs Street $18.4B) while Search delivered $60.4B vs $59B expected. The standout: Cloud backlog nearly doubled quarter-over-quarter to over $460B. Full-year capex guidance nudged up to $185B from $180B.

AMZN beat Q1 operating income expectations at $23.9B vs Street $20.7B, with total revenue up 17% to $181.5B. AWS accelerated 400 basis points sequentially to 28% growth with margins strengthening to 37.7%. The chips business hit a $20B revenue run rate (triple-digit growth). However, Q2 operating income guidance of $20-24B came in slightly soft vs Street $22.85B.

META delivered operating income of $22.8B vs Street $19.5B on revenue up 33% to $56.3B, but negatives emerged: quarterly user decline (attributed to Iran disruptions and Russian WhatsApp restrictions) and 2026 capex budget increase to $125-144B from prior $115-135B guidance.

MSFT reported mixed FQ3 results with operating income of $38.4B vs Street $37B, though commercial bookings growth cooled sharply to -6%. The positive: FQ4 Azure growth forecast of 39-40% vs Street 36.75%, with management guiding double-digit sales and operating income growth for F27.

QCOM provided soft FQ3 guidance (EPS $2.20 vs Street $2.43), but management's data center commentary sparked excitement. A leading hyperscaler custom silicon engagement targets initial shipments later this year, potentially opening AVGO/MRVL-like opportunities. Added $20B buyback authorization.

F reported adjusted EBIT of $3.5B vs consensus $1.26B, boosted by a one-time $1.3B IEEPA tariff benefit. Full-year guidance mid-point rose to $9.5B from $9B, though the increase was less than both the tariff benefit and Q1 beat magnitude.

CMG delivered relief with comps of +0.5% vs Street -1% expectation, though margins softened to 12.9% vs Street 13%. EPS came in-line while full-year guidance remained unchanged.

ORLY posted strong comps of +8.1% vs Street +5.4% with EPS beating by 2 cents at 72 cents. However, guidance increases were modest: EPS range moved just 5 cents higher while comp forecasts stayed unchanged despite Q1 outperformance.
