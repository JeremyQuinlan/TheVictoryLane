RATES & FED

Treasuries are rebounding after Wednesday's post-FOMC slump, with yields down 1-5 basis points across the curve. Markets are now pricing roughly 36 basis points of Fed hikes in 2026, down from a Wednesday afternoon peak of ~40 basis points but well above the ~20 basis points priced before the FOMC statement. The real story isn't just the hawkish pivot — it's the deep uncertainty of the Warsh era, with five new task forces underway and far less forward guidance than markets have grown accustomed to.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The three biggest June catalysts — the Iran MOU, SpaceX's IPO, and the first Warsh FOMC — are now in the rearview mirror, and markets are stabilizing with tech leading the bounce. That said, the Warsh uncertainty premium isn't going anywhere; expect volatility every time one of his task forces surfaces a finding. The bull case rests on oil continuing lower (Brent sub-$78 and falling further as Iranian supply returns), which should cool inflation and eventually take pressure off central banks. Bears will point out the Iran MOU is vague, non-binding, and requires 60 more days of negotiation — it's hardly a solved problem. Micron on June 24th is the next real catalyst; everything else until CQ2 earnings kick off July 14th is noise.
