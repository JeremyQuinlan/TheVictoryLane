MARKET OUTLOOK

The S&P futures selloff triggered by Trump's Iran comments isn't necessarily a screaming buy, but it's also not the end of the world — the White House remains extremely reluctant to fully resume hostilities, and a negotiated settlement is still far more likely than not. The bigger and more lasting risk heading into earnings season is elevated expectations: Samsung's ugly preliminary Q2 reaction is a flashing red light for what could happen even if results are technically strong. Add to that the "green premium" now embedded in options markets — heavy call skew, minimal downside hedging — and any disappointment could get amplified fast (Barron's). The Asia tech rotation (memory down, hyperscaler-adjacent names up) is consistent with the US dynamic and worth watching closely.

GEOPOLITICAL
