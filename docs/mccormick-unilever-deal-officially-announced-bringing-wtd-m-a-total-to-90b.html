COMPANY NEWS

MKC (McCormick) officially announced its combination with Unilever's food business, with deal terms largely consistent with recent media reports. Under the structure, 65% of the new entity's equity goes to Unilever and its shareholders (55.1% to Unilever holders, 9.9% to Unilever itself), while Unilever receives $15.7B in cash. This implies an Enterprise Value for Unilever Foods of approximately $44.8B, or roughly 13.8x fiscal 2025 EBITDA. The combined company targets 3-5% growth in year three, expects $600MM in annual cost synergies, and aims for 23-25% operating margins post-closing. The deal will be structured as a Reverse Morris Trust transaction to avoid U.S. federal income tax implications. Food-focused staples remain hated by investors given cyclical and secular headwinds, and the industry has a poor track record with large-scale M&A (though MKC reported decent results this morning).
