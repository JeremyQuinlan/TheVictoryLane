RATES & FED

Treasuries are selling off with yields rising roughly 3 basis points across the curve, driven by a jump in Brent on Hormuz tensions. The market is pricing approximately 30 basis points of Fed hikes in H2. Yields have failed to drop meaningfully despite soft oil prices and a weak June jobs report, reflecting persistent investor anxiety over underlying inflation and fiscal imbalances.

MARKET OUTLOOK

Samsung's preliminary Q2 beat-but-didn't-wow moment is a preview of the central tension for this earnings season — the numbers will be good, but expectations are now very elevated with the SPX roughly 1,000 points above where it sat heading into Q1 reports. The bar is high, and the market will punish anything short of exceptional. Tech faces a double headwind: Samsung-driven semiconductor weakness across Asia and Europe, plus a Bloomberg report showing Chinese firms actively shifting away from Nvidia toward domestic silicon. Next week is the real stress test — CPI/PPI, six major bank earnings, ASML, NFLX, TSM, and Warsh's House testimony all land in the same five days. Stay nimble.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Iran fired ballistic missiles at two commercial ships near the Strait of Hormuz early Tuesday, striking a Qatari LNG vessel — raising fresh fears of escalation just as US-Iran talks were supposedly progressing (WSJ, Bloomberg). The author's view: sustained détente remains the base case, but it won't be linear, and markets are dangerously complacent about Middle East risk.

- Kuehne + Nagel pushed back on the idea that land routes can substitute for Hormuz — the strait remains indispensable to global shipping (FT).

- China is accelerating oil purchases from Middle East producers, stoking fears of inventory restocking that could put a floor under Brent prices (FT).

- Belarus's Lukashenko confirmed he will not join Russia's war in Ukraine, removing a significant tail risk; Trump separately said a Ukraine resolution is "getting much closer than people realize" (FT).

- Trump is expected to approve F-35 sales to Turkey ahead of the NATO Summit in Ankara, which kicks off today (Reuters).

- The NY Fed's Global Supply Chain Pressure Index fell to 1.25 in June from 1.81 in May; Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd are shifting the AE15 service back through Suez.

COMPANY NEWS

NVDA (Nvidia) faces a split narrative: a SemiAnalysis report frames Nvidia as the "central bank of AI," providing revenue guarantees to neoclouds to backstop a debt market forecast to exceed $7 trillion by 2029 — potentially the second-largest ABS sector after mortgages. Simultaneously, Bloomberg reports Chinese firms are actively migrating to domestic silicon suppliers, a meaningful long-term demand headwind.

CRNX / VRTX — Vertex is acquiring Crinetics for $85/share cash, implying ~$10 billion equity value and $8.5 billion enterprise value, a roughly 100% premium to Monday's close. Vertex sees CRNX's pipeline delivering $5B+ in peak revenue; deal expected to be accretive to operating income in 2029 (dilutive initially).

FISV — WSJ reports JPM, BAC, WFC, and PNC held "preliminary and tentative talks" about acquiring a Fiserv payments network, though several banks have since stepped back. Even without a deal, the story highlights Fiserv's sum-of-the-parts value after a disastrous October earnings print and a surprise CEO change.

RIVN — Rivian announced a 75 million share secondary offering Monday night. Separately guided Q2 revenue to ~$1.6 billion, above the Street's ~$1.44 billion estimate.

META — Four states are seeking $1.4 trillion in penalties ahead of an August trial accusing Meta of deliberately engineering its platforms to addict minors (Reuters).

CRTO — Criteo shares spiked after Bloomberg reported Vista Equity and Quinti Capital are exploring a joint takeover bid at a significant premium.

WMT — Trump's social media post claiming Walmart will cut ground beef prices ~15% was followed by a Walmart press release on "thousands" of price reductions. This is almost certainly a continuation of existing rollback strategy (7,200+ price rollbacks in FQ1), dressed up for political optics ahead of midterms. Win-win noise, not a strategic shift.

Shell — Trading unit guided to significantly higher Q2 results vs. Q1, though gas output was hit by Middle East disruptions affecting Qatari volumes (WSJ).

SPCX (SpaceX) — Wall Street firms initiated coverage with bullish ratings ahead of Nasdaq-100 inclusion (Bloomberg, Reuters).

SNPS — Synopsys will discontinue certain manufacturing process control products to focus on higher-margin AI design software (Reuters).
