COMPANY NEWS

TXN reported solid Q1 upside with EPS of $1.68 versus Street's $1.37, driven by higher sales ($4.83B vs. $4.52B) and nearly 200 basis point gross margin beat. The company benefited from strength in industrial and data center markets.

TSLA beat Q1 EPS estimates at 41¢ versus 38¢ expected, with strong margin expansion (gross margins up 478 basis points, EBITDA margins up 183 basis points) and healthy free cash flow, though sales fell slightly short due to energy segment pressure.

CMCSA delivered across all metrics with EPS of 79¢ versus 72¢ expected and revenue of $31.45B versus $30.3B. Broadband losses were better than feared (-65K vs. -160K) while Peacock subscribers rose 12% to 46M.

NOW disappointed with subscription revenue growth of 19% versus Street's 20.4%, hurt by approximately 75 basis points headwind from delayed Middle East deal closures due to regional conflict.

URI raised full-year guidance after Q1 beats on both sales ($3.985B vs. $3.88B) and EBITDA ($1.759B vs. $1.68B).

FCX cut gold and copper production outlook while warning of higher production costs.
