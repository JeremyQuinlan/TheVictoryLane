Vital Market Recap for Tuesday March 31, 2026

Mar 31, 2026 · 04:04 PM ET

MARKETS

SPX gained +2.91%, equal-weight S&P +2.02%, Nasdaq surged +3.83%, Dow climbed +2.49%, and R2K advanced +3.41%. Brent oil fell ~2.70% to ~$104.50, gold climbed 3.85%, silver advanced 7.35%, Bitcoin ramped 1.75% to ~$67.75K, and DXY slumped 0.60%.

RATES & FED

Treasuries saw gains with yields dropping 3-7 basis points across the curve. The market is pricing in approximately 7 basis points of Fed Funds cuts this year as deescalation hopes in the Middle East reduce inflationary pressures.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The tea leaves continue pointing toward White House deescalation — Trump's social media confirmation of WSJ reporting about ending the war without fully reopening Hormuz significantly reduces odds of material ground troop deployment. While Iran publicly pushes back, reports suggest background communications are occurring, and even Tehran's president acknowledged readiness to end the conflict. The ~$90 billion in M&A announced this week (MKC-Unilever, BIIB-APLS, LLY-CNTA) signals Corporate America remains relatively confident despite macro volatility. Bears worry about stagflationary impulses and limited good options in the Middle East, but housing data continues providing disinflationary pressure domestically.

GEOPOLITICAL

Iran acknowledged that messages are being exchanged between both sides, with the country's president saying Iran is ready to end the war, though this stands in contrast to Tehran's public rhetoric (various sources). Trump effectively confirmed WSJ reporting about exiting the conflict without militarily opening Hormuz, reducing odds of US ground troop deployment. Iran continues demonstrating capacity to target critical Gulf infrastructure, evidenced by the strike against a Kuwaiti oil tanker.

COMPANY NEWS

BIIB will acquire APLS for $41 per share cash (~$5.6B) plus a CVR worth up to $2 per share, a significant premium to Monday's $17.09 close. LLY announced plans to buy CNTA for $38 per share cash (~$6.3B) plus a CVR worth up to $9 per share for total value up to $47 versus Monday's $27.58 close. MKC reported Q1 adjusted EPS of 66¢ versus Street estimate of 59¢ driven by higher revenue of $1.87B versus $1.79B expected, while formally announcing its combination with Unilever's food business. FDS delivered modest Q2 EPS upside at $4.46 versus Street $4.37, driven by 6.8% organic growth and robust organic ASV growth of 6.7%, though operating margins fell short at 35% versus 35.5% expected. CEG shares slumped after providing 2026 EPS guidance slightly below expectations during its analyst meeting.
