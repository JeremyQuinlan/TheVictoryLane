Market Recap · Monday May 4, 2026

May 04, 2026 · 04:04 PM ET

MARKETS

The SPX dropped -0.40% to 7200, while the Nasdaq fell a modest -0.19%. The equal-weight S&P declined -0.58%, the Dow plunged -1.13%, and the R2K sank -0.60%. Brent crude surged ~5.50% to $114.10, gold slumped -2.05%, silver fell -3.25%, Bitcoin advanced +1.35% to $79.97K, and the DXY rose 25 basis points.

RATES & FED

Treasuries slumped as oil prices spiked, with yields jumping 6-7 basis points across the curve. Fed expectations shifted hawkishly, with the market now pricing in 7 basis points worth of hikes this year as policymakers continue laying groundwork for potential rate increases if the Iran conflict persists (WSJ).

MARKET OUTLOOK

Despite Middle East violence flaring for the first time in weeks, stocks showed remarkable resilience—but this complacency is concerning. While Q1 earnings strength is appreciated, it's largely reflected in prices after the recent multi-week rally. Markets seem dangerously relaxed about Iran risks, given Trump's limited control over the theater and the high probability of unplanned escalation. Even with a deal, the Iran war will have profound inflationary effects for months, and Hormuz will never return to its previous state. Risk/reward on the SPX at these levels is neutral at best.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Iran fired drones and missiles at the UAE and US Navy ships for the first time in weeks, underscoring Tehran's grip on Hormuz (CNN)

• Trump told Fox that Iranian negotiators were being "far more malleable," echoing optimistic social media comments Sunday night

• The US will return a seized Iranian ship and crew as a "confidence-building measure" (CNN)

• South Korea's central bank deputy said it's time to consider rate hikes due to oil price pressures (Reuters)

COMPANY NEWS

TSN reported upside Q2 EPS of 87¢ vs. Street 79¢, driven by higher operating margins (3.64% vs. 3.4% expected) and sales of $13.653B vs. $13.577B expected. Full-year operating income guidance was raised to $2.2-2.4B from $2.1-2.3B prior.

NCLH beat on Q1 EBITDA at $533MM vs. Street $502MM but slashed full-year guidance, citing not just fuel costs but "signs of softer demand as consumers reevaluate travel plans, particularly to Europe."

GBTG will be acquired by Long Lake Management for $9.50/share cash in a $6.3B deal—a ~60% premium to Friday's close.

EBAY jumped after GameStop built a ~5% stake and offered to buy the e-commerce firm for $125/share, with TD Bank providing $20B in debt financing (WSJ). Few believe the bid is credible.
