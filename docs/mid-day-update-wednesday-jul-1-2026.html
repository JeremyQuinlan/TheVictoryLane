MARKET OUTLOOK

Today's session is a tale of two markets — software and hyperscalers are flying while the chip complex gets hit hard on fears of AI overbuilding, and that tension is the defining trade right now. The Meta cloud-compute story is a genuine regime shift in how AI infrastructure economics get priced; it's bullish for software/services and bearish for pick-and-shovel names in the near term. The ISM prices print gives the Fed some breathing room, and Warsh's bullish AI commentary at Sintra suggests the board isn't looking to choke off the cycle. Eyes on Thursday's jobs report — that's the next real catalyst, and a soft number could finally push the Fed off the sidelines.

COMPANY NEWS

META is surging after Bloomberg reported the company is shifting toward a hyperscaler/neocloud model, potentially selling spare compute capacity to external customers — bullish for Meta, but sparking a selloff across hardware/infrastructure names on overbuilding concerns.

FDS (FactSet) beat on FQ3 EPS ($4.53 vs. $4.43 Street) and organic revenue growth (+7% vs. +5.4% Street), but operating margins disappointed, falling 268 basis points year-over-year to 24% vs. the Street's 34.6%, and full-year guidance was merely reiterated.

GIS (General Mills) delivered strong FQ4 EPS upside (95c vs. 80c Street), with gross and operating margins up 150 and 160 basis points respectively, alongside a plan to cut ~$3B in costs by fiscal 2030.

MSM (MSC Industrial) posted FQ3 EPS of $1.43 vs. $1.27 Street, with operating margins spiking 160 basis points year-over-year to 10.6%, and issued a bullish FQ4 guide.

NKE (Nike) reported FQ4 results largely inline ex-tariff benefit; management struck a cautious macro tone on demand trends and trimmed prior revenue guidance, though the stock is rallying after EPS guidance was held unchanged.

STZ (Constellation Brands) beat on FQ1 EPS ($3.43 vs. $3.18 Street) and revenue ($2.43B vs. $2.39B Street), reiterating full-year guidance despite a "value-conscious consumer environment."

PRGS (Progress Software) beat FQ2 EPS at $1.62 vs. $1.49 Street on stronger sales (+6% FX-neutral to $253MM) and raised full-year EPS guidance to $6.09–$6.21, though ARR growth remains muted.

KR (Kroger) is acquiring Giant Eagle for $1.65B ($1.25B cash plus ~$400MM in assumed liabilities).

FDX (FedEx) is selling FedEx Supply Chain to CMA CGM Group for $1.4B enterprise value.

AA (Alcoa) is acquiring South32's bauxite, alumina, and aluminum assets for up to $4.85B ($3.1B cash + ~$1B stock upfront, plus a $750MM CVR).

BE (Bloom Energy) and Brookfield expanded their strategic partnership from $5B to $25B, citing surging hyperscaler and AI infrastructure demand for power.

MSFT (Microsoft) is preparing to cut thousands of jobs — under 2.5% of its workforce — as part of ongoing cost discipline (Business Insider).

SMCI (Supermicro) — Taiwanese officials detained two employees following a raid of the company's local offices amid a probe into alleged smuggling of Nvidia chips to China (Bloomberg).

ALGN (Align Technology) is facing an EU antitrust probe into whether its Invisalign sales strategy broke competition rules (WSJ).

FMC — Tessenderlo Group is making a ~$400MM strategic minority equity investment at $13.30/share, a notable premium to FMC's prior close of $11.50.
