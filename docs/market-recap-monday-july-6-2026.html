RATES & FED

Treasuries finished flat to slightly stronger, with yields down 0–3 basis points across the curve. The market is pricing roughly 30 basis points of Fed hikes remaining this year. The persistent inability of yields to rally meaningfully — despite softening oil and a weak June jobs report — reflects stubborn underlying inflation fears and sovereign fiscal concerns (Germany announced plans to dramatically accelerate borrowing for rearmament, per FT).

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The hyperscaler-vs.-chip debate will be the single most defining theme of Q2 earnings season — even a subtle hint of capex restraint from Amazon, Google, Meta, or Microsoft could trigger a violent rotation out of semiconductors. While Q2 results should be strong in absolute terms, the bar is now dramatically higher than it was heading into Q1, with the SPX sitting roughly 1,000 points above those April levels. Next week is the real test: CPI on 7/14, PPI on 7/15, major bank earnings, TSM, NFLX, ASML, and Fed Chair nominee Warsh testifying before the House. The risk/reward on Treasuries looks compelling here — inflation data could be the catalyst that finally gets yields moving lower.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Middle East/Iran: Markets remain complacent about genuine détente risks — Washington and Tehran hold competing interpretations of the MOU, kinetic exchanges continue, and Iran and Oman are seriously considering tolls on Hormuz traffic. Lebanon remains a wildcard. An oil glut is building as Hormuz traffic rebounds, OPEC+ hiked output again over the weekend, and Iranian sanctions are being removed (WSJ, Bloomberg).

- China/Oil: Beijing is accelerating oil purchases from Middle Eastern producers, raising fears of inventory restocking that could stall Brent's decline (FT).

- Russia/Ukraine: Ukraine is striking Russian energy infrastructure at an unprecedented pace, with nearly every Russian region now facing a fuel crisis (FT, CNN). Russia hit Kyiv with ballistic missiles and drones Monday morning ahead of the NATO summit in Turkey (CNN). Belarus's Lukashenko confirmed he will not join Russia's war against Ukraine (FT). Trump said a resolution is "getting much closer than people realize."

- Israel/Iran: U.S. officials grew worried this spring that Israel planned to assassinate Iran's chief cease-fire negotiators and sent warnings through regional intermediaries (NYT). Trump said Netanyahu "knows who the boss is" ahead of the Israeli PM's White House visit; administration officials have grown increasingly disillusioned with Netanyahu (Axios). Iran's new leadership is described as "younger, savvier, ruthless, and even more hardline" (Washington Post).

- NATO Summit: Kicks off Tuesday in Turkey; Trump will attend.

COMPANY NEWS

AVGO (Broadcom) expanded its chip collaboration with AAPL (Apple) through 2031, covering multiple generations of custom ASIC silicon products across Apple's product lineup.

MSFT (Microsoft) announced 4,800 job cuts (~2.1% of headcount), with management careful to note the eliminated roles are not being directly replaced by AI — though AI-driven workflow changes are clearly the backdrop.

NVDA (Nvidia): The next-gen Kyber NVL144 AI rack system has been delayed until 2028 due to manufacturing difficulties, with the NVL576 also likely to face delays — a potential opening for AMD and Google's TPU (CNBC/SemiAnalysis, Bloomberg).

META (Meta): Superintelligence chief Alexandr Wang said the upcoming "Watermelon" model is on par with OpenAI's GPT-5.5 and uses substantially more compute than existing frontier models (Business Insider). SemiAnalysis concludes Meta's 2027 capex will be "shockingly high" and any neocloud pivot would be highly accretive (SemiAnalysis). Meta is also in talks to manufacture next-gen AI chips at Samsung rather than TSMC (Seeking Alpha).

CRTO (Criteo) surged after Bloomberg reported Vista Equity and Quinti Capital are considering a joint takeover bid at a significant premium.

ESI (Element Solutions) agreed to be acquired by SOLS (Solstice) in a cash-and-stock deal valued at $14.5B — $10/share cash plus 0.5 SOLS shares, totaling ~$50.10/share.

LMT (Lockheed Martin) will pay $3.5B to acquire Ultra Maritime, a next-gen undersea warfare specialist, from Advent.

KLAR (Klarna) filed for a U.S. banking license, which would allow it to bring its existing banking operations in-house rather than relying on partner banks.

WULF (TeraWulf) signed a 20-year lease with Anthropic at its Kentucky data campus expected to generate ~$19B in contracted revenue, and will sell its 50.1% stake in the Abernathy Joint Venture to Fluidstack-led investors.

MSTR (Strategy) sold 1,363 bitcoin on 6/29–6/30 and 2,225 bitcoin on 7/1–7/5, and recorded an $8.32B loss on digital assets for Q2 (including $8.31B unrealized).

MU (Micron) announced a long-term Strategic Customer Agreement with Ford for memory supply — consistent with the wave of SCAs disclosed on its recent earnings call, which likely included last week's GM deal as well.

CLVT (Clarivate) agreed to divest its Life Sciences & Healthcare segment to Altaris for $600MM while reaffirming prior guidance.

CXW (CoreCivic) completed the sale of two detention facilities for ~$1.5B, expecting net proceeds of ~$1.1B.

ARM: CEO Rene Haas says CPU demand is "off the charts" driven by agentic AI — not just more CPUs, but more cores per CPU, doubly bullish for the model (Tae Kim/Substack).

GFL (GFL Environmental) is weighing a take-private after receiving buyout interest (Bloomberg).

TSLA (Tesla) is capping employee AI spending at $200/week to control surging token costs (The Information).
