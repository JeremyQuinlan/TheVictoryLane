MARKETS

SPX +0.48%, Nasdaq +1.00%, Dow +47 points, R2K +15.5 points. Brent +1.40% to ~$84.50 (off morning highs near $87.50). Gold +2.30%, Silver +3.20%, BTC +3.30%. Equal-weight S&P slightly negative.

---

RATES & FED

Treasuries are rallying hard, with yields down 3–11 basis points across the curve following the cooler-than-expected June CPI print. The market is now pricing roughly 31 basis points of Fed cuts in H2, down from ~43 basis points pre-CPI. Fed Chair Warsh's congressional testimony was largely a non-event — he reiterated the same hawkish inflation stance delivered at Sintra, no new signals.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The CPI relief is real but don't get complacent — the macro tape is full of cross-currents that make the bull case harder to run straight. Bank earnings are blowout-level strong, which matters, but IBM's profit warning is a serious gut-punch to the software/services complex and HCA's uninsured-mix warning introduces a genuine policy-driven headwind into healthcare. Oil remains a wild card as Iran stays stalemated and China's inventory posture could quickly flip the script. The sector rotation — into chips, banks, industrials, and materials, out of software and healthcare — tells you investors are repositioning rather than broadly pressing risk. Stay selective.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Trump sent a fresh war notice to Congress on July 7 restarting a 60-day clock on U.S. military action against Iran; his latest de-escalation proposal — vague "trade and investment deals" — replaces the prior 20% ship tax idea, both widely seen as unworkable (Politico).

- China's strategic oil inventory drawdowns over recent months have suppressed Brent prices, but if Beijing reverses course and begins restocking, the upside risk to crude could become severe (NYT).

- NY Governor Hochul signed an executive order banning large new data center construction in the state for up to a year, a meaningful headwind for AI infrastructure buildout (WSJ).

- The White House is discussing taking equity stakes in AI companies, though no formal plan exists yet (NYT).

- Japanese officials again floated the idea of pension funds shifting allocations toward domestic assets — a recurring theme with real long-term FX and JGB implications (Reuters).

COMPANY NEWS

BAC reported Q2 EPS of $1.21 vs. Street $1.13, on revenue of $31.5B vs. $30.8B expected. NII was roughly inline; the beat came from noninterest income strength, cost discipline, and provisions of $1.36B vs. $1.51B expected.

C delivered a strong Q2 with EPS of $3.15 vs. Street $2.74, revenue of $24.8B vs. $23.7B, and an efficiency ratio of 57.4% — improving roughly 530 basis points year-over-year.

GS posted a blowout Q2: EPS $20.98 vs. Street $14.49, revenue +39% year-over-year to $20.3B vs. $16.34B expected, with an efficiency ratio of 57.4% — several hundred basis points better than consensus.

JPM beat Q2 estimates across the board driven by robust noninterest income, expense controls, and lower provisions. Specific EPS figures not broken out separately in the release but the upside was described as solid.

WFC earned $2.00 in Q2 EPS vs. Street $1.71. Noninterest income rose 13% (including a ~$250M JV gain), efficiency ratio of 60% was several hundred basis points better than expected, and provisions came in at $914M vs. ~$1.2B forecast.

IBM pre-announced a significant revenue miss — Q2 revenue guided to $17.2B (up just 1% year-over-year) vs. Street ~$17.9B, with gross margins down 70 basis points and EPS of $2.93 vs. $3.02 expected. The stock is crashing and dragging software/services peers with it.

HCA cut full-year guidance, citing a payer mix shift driven by rising uninsured volumes — patients losing health exchange coverage. The warning is rippling through the broader healthcare sector.

NVDA H200 chip shipments to China have commenced but in very low volumes (Reuters). Separately, Nvidia has cut the number of authorized Asian buyers as part of a compliance crackdown to curb chip trafficking into China (FT).

UBER is in advanced talks to acquire Delivery Hero, with a deal potentially closing this week (Bloomberg).

TRI will sell a 51% stake in its Global Print business to KKR for ~$500M in gross proceeds, retaining a 49% stake.

LBRT/SLB formed a strategic alliance to provide infrastructure and power generation solutions for data centers.

BRK/B — Buffett stated his goal is to dispose of all his Berkshire shares within eight years.

TREX issued an upside Q2 preannouncement citing strong consumer sell-through and raised full-year guidance.

YUM — federal and state health officials are investigating whether Taco Bell's lettuce supply is linked to a multi-state cyclosporiasis outbreak (Washington Post).

BF/B CEO Lawson Whiting is stepping down; the company reaffirmed F27 guidance.
