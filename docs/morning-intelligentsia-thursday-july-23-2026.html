COMPANY NEWS

GOOGL reported Q2 operating income of $40.77B (vs. Street $40.5B) on revenue of $119.7B (+24%), but op. margins of 34% missed the Street's 34.75% estimate. The stock is under pressure primarily from a boosted full-year capex guide of $195–205B (up from $180–190B) and vague language on reclaiming AI frontier leadership with Gemini.

TSLA posted Q2 sales of $28.23B (+26%, vs. Street $26.3B) but missed badly on EPS at 33c vs. 51c expected, with GAAP gross margins -31 basis points, op. margins -269 basis points, and EBITDA margins -353 basis points. FCF was -$1.09B; capex surged 142% Y/Y to $5.79B. Musk talked down Optimus on the call — the stock trades on hope, not numbers.

NOW delivered a clean beat: 23% FXN subscription software growth (vs. Street 21.85%), 21.5% cRPO growth (vs. Street 19.5%), and 29.5% op. margins (vs. Street 27%). Full-year sub rev guide nudged to +21%.

CSX beat on EPS at 54c (+23%, vs. Street 52c) with revenue of $3.94B (+10%), op. margins expanding 240 basis points to 38.3%. Full-year guide raised.

LUV crushed expectations: EPS of 94c vs. Street 52c, with full-year EPS guidance midpoint of $3.75 vs. Street $3.12 — this despite a significant fuel cost increase.

TXN beat Q2 EPS at $2.09 ex-items (vs. Street $1.93) on sales of $5.46B (+23%), but Q3 guide, while above plan, wasn't enough to offset semi sentiment headwinds.

URI beat on Q2 sales ($4.41B vs. Street $4.21B) and EBITDA ($2.056B vs. Street $1.91B); full-year EBITDA guide raised to $7.975–8.125B.

LVS missed on Q2 sales ($3.15B vs. Street $3.3B) and EBITDA ($1.11B vs. Street $1.3B); unusually low hold in Macau rolling play was the culprit. Buyback authorization raised to $6B (~20% of market cap).

IBM pre-announced a Q2 miss; formal release lowered top-line guidance but reiterated FCF outlook (up ~$1B Y/Y).

KNX beat strongly: EPS 63c vs. Street 51c; Q3 guide of 71–77c vs. Street 71c.

NESTLE reported inline Q2 organic growth of +3.7% with Americas disappointing at +2.8% vs. Street +3.7%; H2 margin outlook cut to flat vs. H1. Separately, Nestle sold a 50% stake in its Waters and Beverages unit to Platinum Equity for €3.4B (WSJ).

PAG received a go-private bid at $210/share cash from Penske Corp. and Mitsui (who already own ~72.6%); PAG closed Wednesday at $214.57.

UNP/NSC merger cleared a key hurdle as Canadian National dropped its opposition in exchange for expanded network access (WSJ).

Nokia surged in Europe: FXN revenue +9% (vs. Street +7.85%), with AI & Cloud customer sales up 105%.

SOITEC surged after FQ1 sales grew +23% vs. guidance of +15%; Q2 guide calls for >30% growth on Photonics-SOI demand acceleration.

STM tumbled: Q3 revenue guidance of $3.7B fell short of Street's $3.78B, weighing on European semi sentiment broadly.
