RATES & FED

Treasuries caught a bid after cooler-than-expected PPI data and dovish remarks from NY Fed's Williams, who believes inflation has already peaked and that policy is well positioned — yields fell 2–5 basis points across the curve. The market is now pricing roughly 25 basis points of Fed hikes in H2, down meaningfully from the 40–45 basis points priced before the CPI print.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The bull case is holding together reasonably well — earnings are mostly constructive, inflation data keeps surprising to the soft side, and the Fed is sounding less hawkish by the day. The wildcard remains the Iran situation, which feels like it's entering a pause phase rather than a true de-escalation, and that ambiguity is keeping energy from running. The rotation within tech — chips getting sold while software catches a bid — reflects genuine anxiety about capacity overbuild in semiconductors, and ASML's guidance raise, paradoxically, is feeding that fear. Watch retail sales Thursday morning; if the consumer is holding up, the path of least resistance is still higher.

COMPANY NEWS

BLK (BlackRock) delivered a standout Q2, with EPS of $13.91 (+15% vs. the Street's $12.66), revenue of $7.08B (+31%, beating the $6.82B estimate), and operating margins expanding roughly 260 basis points to 45.9% — about 120 basis points above consensus.

BNY (Bank of New York Mellon) posted Q2 EPS of $2.45 (+27% vs. the Street's $2.23) on revenue of $5.7B vs. $5.4B expected, with operating leverage expanding 591 basis points year-over-year and margins rising more than 300 basis points to 39.9%; full-year guidance was raised across sales, expenses, and operating leverage.

MS (Morgan Stanley) beat Q2 estimates with EPS of $3.46 vs. $2.93 expected on revenue of $21.3B vs. $19.6B, with the efficiency ratio improving 600 basis points year-over-year to 65%.

PNC reported Q2 adjusted EPS of $4.85 vs. the Street's $4.53, driven by fee revenue outperformance; full-year sales guidance raised to +13% from +11%.

ELV (Elevance) beat Q2 EPS at $7.45 vs. $6.18 expected, aided by a favorable MCR of 89.7% and roughly 80 cents of below-the-line tailwinds; stripping those out still yields $6.65, comfortably above consensus. Full-year guide nudged to "at least $27" from "at least $26.75." Stock is underperforming on quality-of-beat concerns.

JNJ reported Q2 EPS of $2.90 vs. $2.87 on sales of $25.3B vs. $25B; Innovative Medicine grew 6.9% operationally while MedTech disappointed at +3.7% due to Cardio softness. Full-year adjusted EPS guide raised to $11.68 from $11.55.

PGR (Progressive) reported June EPS down 30% year-over-year, driven by a 340 basis point deterioration in the combined ratio — shares are getting hit hard.

PNR (Pentair) pre-announced negatively and cut full-year guidance, blaming inventory destocking in the pool business — the third notable negative preannouncement in quick succession.

CAG (Conagra) barely beat FQ4 EPS (47c vs. 46c) but disappointed on organic revenue (flat vs. +0.75% expected), cut its dividend in half to $0.175/quarter, and guided FY27 EPS to $1.45 vs. the Street's $1.54.
