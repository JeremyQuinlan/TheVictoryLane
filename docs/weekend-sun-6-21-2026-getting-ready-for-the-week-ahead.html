RATES & FED

The Warsh-led Fed's first decision moved policy in a hawkish direction, though the author argues the shift may already be going stale given the oil supply picture. Warsh is also instituting structural changes — task forces due by Dec 31 — that could overhaul how the central bank operates, creating opacity and uncertainty for several quarters. The core question Warsh faces: can AI drive a 1990s-style productivity boom that justifies staying dovish, or will inflation keep the Fed's hands tied (WSJ)?

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

The bull case is straightforward: oil supplies are about to expand materially — Iranian energy sanctions lifted, OPEC+ quotas higher, the UAE unshackled, Venezuelan output rising — and oil was driving the bulk of upward pressure on yields and inflation. If Brent keeps sliding, the hawkish Fed posture becomes less of a market headwind than it looks today. That said, the bear case has teeth: CPIs are still overshooting targets globally, Warsh's institutional changes introduce genuine policy uncertainty, tariffs haven't gone away (average rates could reset higher by summer's end), and USMCA may be on the chopping block (NYT). The calendar is relatively clean until CQ2 earnings kick off July 14, so near-term direction likely hinges on May PCE (June 25) and the June jobs report (July 2).
