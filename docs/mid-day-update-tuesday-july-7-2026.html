RATES & FED

Treasuries are under selling pressure with yields up 2–4 basis points, driven largely by the spike in oil following fresh Middle East hostilities. Markets continue to price in roughly 30 basis points of Fed hikes in H2 — a modest but real tightening bias that's keeping rate-sensitive sectors on edge.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

Don't mistake today's tape for broad deterioration — the equal-weight S&P is actually slightly positive, which tells you the rot is concentrated in mega-cap tech and data center-adjacent names. Samsung's Q2 beat the print consensus but failed to clear the stratospheric expectations the market had baked in, and that's spooking investors heading into a Q2 earnings season where the bar for pick-and-shovel AI names may simply be too high. Financials, insurance, healthcare, staples, energy, and utilities are all holding up well — classic defensive rotation that makes sense given geopolitical uncertainty and rate jitters. The Iran-Hormuz flare-up is worth watching but shouldn't derail the broader US-Iran détente thesis; the path just won't be linear. Stay selective, not defensive wholesale.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Iran's IRGC fired ballistic missiles at two commercial ships near the Strait of Hormuz early Tuesday — a Qatari LNG tanker was struck by a projectile — spiking oil and rattling energy markets (WSJ, Bloomberg).

- Despite the uptick in land-route alternatives, Kuehne + Nagel warns that trucking cannot substitute for Hormuz, which remains structurally critical to global shipping (FT).

- China is accelerating oil purchases from Middle East producers, raising the prospect of inventory restocking that could put a floor under Brent prices just as they were softening (FT).

- Belarus's Lukashenko stated he will not join Russia's war against Ukraine, defusing a scenario markets had been quietly worried about (FT).

COMPANY NEWS

FISV (Fiserv) is surging after the WSJ reported that a consortium of major banks — JPM, BAC, WFC, and PNC — held "preliminary and tentative talks" about acquiring a payments network owned by Fiserv. FIS is also catching a bid on sympathy flows.

VRTX (Vertex) is acquiring CRNX (Crinetics) for $85/share in cash, implying an equity value of ~$10B and enterprise value of $8.5B — a roughly 100% premium to Monday's close (deal announced Monday night).

NVDA (Nvidia) is in focus on two fronts: a SemiAnalysis report projects AI debt financing will exceed $7T by 2029, with Nvidia effectively acting as the sector's central bank by providing revenue guarantees to neoclouds. Separately, a Bloomberg survey finds Chinese firms are increasingly shifting away from Nvidia toward domestic silicon suppliers.

RIVN (Rivian) announced a 75M-share secondary offering Monday night but softened the blow with Q2 revenue guidance of ~$1.6B, comfortably above the Street's ~$1.44B estimate.

EFX (Equifax) is acquiring Círculo de Crédito, Mexico's fastest-growing credit bureau, for an enterprise value of $750M.

ON (ON Semiconductor) is divesting two manufacturing facilities, targeting ~$35M in annual cost savings beginning in 2027 with full realization in 2028.

META faces $1.4T in potential penalties as four states pursue claims that the company deliberately designed its apps to addict young users (Reuters).

SPCX (SpaceX) received bullish initiation coverage from multiple Wall Street firms ahead of its Nasdaq 100 inclusion (Bloomberg, Reuters).

Shell guided for significantly higher Q2 trading unit results versus Q1, though gas output was hurt by lost Qatari volumes tied to Middle East disruptions (WSJ).
