MARKET OUTLOOK

The earnings tape this morning is broadly constructive — beats outnumber misses, and several names are raising full-year guidance — but the quality of those beats is uneven. GM and MMM are the standouts; SYF's EPS beat was provision-driven, and NOC's headline number was flattered by a suspiciously low tax rate. The looming tariff reset is the real wildcard: if the White House follows through on reimposing levies at pre-SCOTUS levels, cost-of-living pressure could bite consumer names into year-end, right before midterms — which is exactly why some inside the administration are pumping the brakes. Stay selective, lean into execution stories with pricing power.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The White House is preparing a fresh wave of tariffs as the current 10% blanket levy expires at month-end, with the stated goal of restoring average tariff rates to pre-SCOTUS levels by fall. The objective is to hold rates steady rather than escalate, but internal factions are urging Trump to honor existing trade deals and stay mindful of consumer cost pressures ahead of the midterms (FT).

COMPANY NEWS

GM delivered a strong Q2 beat — EPS of $3.57 vs. the Street's $3.19, revenue $48B vs. $46.6B expected — and raised full-year EPS guidance to a $13 mid-point from $12.50. Warrant costs and EV losses are declining, software/services revenue is growing, and the vehicle lineup is helping hold pricing and capture share. The stock still trades below 6x PE, though the EBIT margin range for North America is unchanged at 8–10%, which limits the upside surprise on guidance.

HAS delivered a clean beat-and-raise: Q2 EPS $1.28 vs. $1.13 expected, revenue $1.13B vs. $1.066B, with both physical toys and gaming outperforming. Full-year revenue growth guidance raised to +5–7% from +3–5%, with operating margins guided to 25–26% vs. the prior 24–25%.

MMM posted Q2 EPS of $2.40 vs. $2.25 expected, with 5.4% organic growth crushing the 3.2% consensus estimate. Operating margins came in roughly 15 basis points better at 24.9%, and full-year EPS guidance moves to $8.80–$8.95 from $8.50–$8.70.

KHC struck a supply and licensing deal with Disney — Kraft products will be served at Disney's North American properties, and Disney characters can appear on select Kraft goods (WSJ).

DHI beat FQ3 EPS ($3.20 vs. $2.98) on slightly better deliveries and housing gross margins, but orders disappointed and full-year revenue guidance was trimmed to $32.5–$33B from $33.5–$34.5B. Management cited "affordability constraints and cautious consumer sentiment" with elevated sales incentives expected to persist in Q4.

DHR beat Q2 EPS ($1.94 vs. $1.84) and revenue ($6.26B vs. $6.1B) with better margins, and nudged full-year EPS guidance to $8.45–$8.60. However, the full-year sales guide was cut to +3–4% from +3–6% due to softness in respiratory testing, and that revenue guide trim is likely the dominant takeaway.

EFX posted a modest Q2 EPS beat ($2.25 vs. $2.20) and tightened full-year guidance, but Q3 EPS guidance midpoint of $2.20 came in light vs. the $2.25 consensus — call it inline overall.

KEY beat Q2 EPS by two cents (44c vs. 42c) on lower provisions and slightly better efficiency, but NII and NIM were both modestly below consensus. Full-year revenue guidance nudged to +7–8% from ~+7%, though the print looks underwhelming relative to broader bank sector results.

MRSH beat Q2 EPS ($2.96 vs. $2.90) driven by 5% organic growth — roughly 150 basis points above plan — with Consulting notably strong.

MSCI posted a rare miss: Q2 EPS $4.94 vs. $4.99 and revenue $867MM vs. $870MM expected, with full-year expense guidance moving higher.

SYF beat Q2 EPS handily ($2.59 vs. $2.12) and raised full-year EPS guidance to $9.25–$9.50 from $9.10–$9.50, with all-time high purchase volumes. The caveat: the beat was largely provision-driven, and NII/NIM both disappointed.

NOC beat Q2 EPS ($7.68 vs. $6.83), but the outperformance was largely a function of a 6.3% tax rate, while segment operating margins contracted 120 basis points to 10.6%. Backlog grew to $104.7B from $95.6B. Not as clean as the headline suggests.

HAL was essentially inline — adjusted EPS 55c vs. 54c expected — with higher revenue offset by weaker operating margins; adjusted operating income of $683MM came in just shy of the $689MM estimate.

GPC posted a modest Q2 beat (EPS $2.15 vs. $2.07; revenue $6.53B vs. $6.43B) and reiterated full-year guidance, with North America auto sales trimmed slightly and international auto bumped higher.
