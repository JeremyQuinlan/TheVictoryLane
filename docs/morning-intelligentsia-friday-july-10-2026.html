MARKET OUTLOOK

This is the calm before the storm — next week is a genuine gauntlet with June CPI (expected to ease to +3.9% from +4.2%), PPI, Warsh's testimony, and a wave of major bank earnings. The inflation print is the most important near-term catalyst; a soft number would meaningfully complicate the Fed's hawkish lean. Meta's AI week has been extraordinary — the SemiAnalysis endorsement placing it alongside OpenAI and Anthropic is the kind of institutional credibility that re-rates a stock. Meanwhile, the Anduril CEO calling valuations "irrational" is the sort of comment that should at least give bulls pause about froth in private markets spilling into public sentiment.

COMPANY NEWS

META has had arguably its best week in years on AI. The company launched Muse Spark 1.1 — a multimodal agentic reasoning model — and for the first time introduced a paid API tier, marking its first real attempt to monetize its AI infrastructure. Zuckerberg also flagged plans to sell compute capacity externally and confirmed targets of 7GW this year and 14GW in 2027. Meta's "Iris" chip (co-developed with Broadcom, built at TSMC) enters production in September. SemiAnalysis put Meta in the top-three frontier AI labs alongside OpenAI and Anthropic (Reuters, Bloomberg, SemiAnalysis).

BA is set to receive FAA approval for the 737 MAX 7 by month-end and will also regain permission to conduct final safety inspections on 737s — a meaningful regulatory unlock after years of delays (WSJ).

NFLX is reportedly seeing a dip in subscriber engagement amid rising competition; the company is exploring live TV channels and third-party bundling (e.g., Peacock) to address the trend (WSJ).

WDFC crushed FQ3 estimates: EPS of $2.33 ex-items vs. Street at $1.57, revenue $195.1M vs. $173M expected, with gross margins roughly 70 basis points ahead of plan. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $6.05–$6.35 from $5.75–$6.15.

MU raised its US investment target to over $250B through 2035, up from ~$200B previously.

ORCL was downgraded by S&P from BBB to BBB-, the last notch above junk, though the outlook was revised from negative to stable.

GOOGL and OpenAI are accused of selling advanced models to blacklisted Chinese tech firms, intensifying calls for tighter export controls (FT).

Vodafone received a major catalyst as French billionaire Xavier Niel announced plans to acquire a 16.2% stake from Emirates Telecoms for GBP4.4B, making him the largest shareholder (WSJ). EasyJet received a GBP7.15/share (GBP5.7B) takeover bid from Apollo, topping Castlelake's prior GBP6.90 offer (FT). Bayer is selling a minority stake in its contraceptive business to Apollo for €3B (WSJ). CCC Intelligent Solutions is exploring a sale via Morgan Stanley (Reuters). Carlyle is selling Copia Power to EQT for $2.6B, a fivefold return (FT). SK Hynix priced its US ADR at $149/share, raising ~$26.5B (FT).

FDX and UPS saw mild mid-day pressure Thursday after reports of Amazon aggressively undercutting both on logistics pricing (Supply Chain Dive). Volkswagen plans to cut its model lineup by 50% and shrink manufacturing capacity to combat Chinese competition (WSJ).
