RATES & FED

Treasuries are fairly steady Wednesday morning after rallying Tuesday on the cooler CPI print. Markets are now pricing roughly 32 basis points of Fed hikes this year, down from ~44 basis points pre-CPI, with the July 29th meeting seen as a hold and ~70% odds of a hike by the September 16th meeting. The cooler inflation read removes the near-term hike threat but doesn't give the Fed an all-clear, given tariff risks, energy pressure from the Middle East, and persistently above-target price levels.

MARKET OUTLOOK

Tuesday's CPI was unambiguously bullish and the big bank earnings were terrific — but three negative preannouncements in a single day (HCA, IBM, PNR) heading into the heart of Q2 season is a yellow flag that shouldn't be dismissed. Yes, each miss has its own story, but the same-day clustering across wildly disparate sectors is uncomfortable. The WSJ flags a potential equity supply tsunami — blockbuster stock sales threatening to overwhelm the bull market — which adds an additional headwind. Bulls can take comfort in the banks' upbeat macro read and ASML's monster beat-and-raise, but the path higher is narrowing and the Iran situation remains a live wildcard on energy prices.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US conducted another round of strikes on Iran overnight and Iran retaliated, but oil is holding steady — many observers believe this latest escalation may be nearing a point where Trump posts a "we agreed to resume negotiations" on social media (Fox).

- Trump told Fox News Tuesday night that US-Iran communication was ongoing as recently as an hour before his interview, while simultaneously warning Tehran that strikes will intensify unless Iran returns seriously to the table (Fox).

- Trump "has no clear idea" how to end the Iran war; aides scrambled to kill his absurd 20% Hormuz surtax plan, and the replacement idea — Gulf state "massive investments" in the US — is equally impractical (CNN). Politico flatly says there's "no end in sight."

- The actual war cost is likely $80–100B and climbing, not the ~$30B figure the White House cites (NBC News).

- Trump is still pressing Netanyahu to withdraw IDF forces from Lebanon — a signal the White House is actively working to placate Tehran on the margins (Axios).

- Oil faces pressure from multiple fronts: Hormuz, Yemen (Houthi-Saudi flare-up threatening Red Sea shipping), and Russia, which is enduring its worst energy crisis since the Soviet collapse due to relentless Ukrainian drone strikes.

COMPANY NEWS

PYPL is surging ~16% pre-market after Reuters reported that Stripe and Advent International submitted a $60.50/share takeout bid valuing PayPal at over $53 billion.

ASML delivered a blowout Q2: sales of €9.3B vs. the Street's €8.8B, gross margins of 54% vs. 52% expected, and a massive guidance raise to €43–45B revenue (from €36–40B) with GMs of 54–56%. The company flagged an accelerating capacity expansion of 30% this year and 30% next — reassuring for AI-linked semis demand, though the aggressive capacity ramp is exactly how cyclical booms eventually turn (Richemont press release / company report).

Richemont posted FQ1 sales growth of +20% on an FX-neutral basis, nearly double the Street's +11.1% estimate. Jewelry surged +24% vs. +13.1% expected; Asia Pacific +21%, Japan +36%, Americas +27% — all geographies beat (Richemont).

PNR (Pentair) preannounced Q2 sales down 17% Y/Y vs. consensus of +1%, with EPS of $1.12 vs. $1.47 expected (including ~$35M in IEEPA tariff refunds). Full-year guidance cut to sales -4% to -7% and EPS $4.60–4.80 vs. prior $5.30–5.40, citing aggressive pool industry inventory destocking. This was the third negative preannouncement of Tuesday alongside HCA and IBM.

NVDA CEO Jensen Huang confirmed Vera Rubin is on track and already in production (Bloomberg). Separately, H200 shipments to China have commenced but in very low volumes (Reuters).

UBER is in advanced talks to acquire Delivery Hero, with a deal potentially as soon as this week (Bloomberg).

CRWV (CoreWeave) is exploring financial hedges against memory chip price declines on its long-term purchase deals with Micron and SanDisk (Reuters).

LION (Lionsgate) is exploring a sale and has drawn takeover interest from Bollore Group (Reuters).

ORCL is leading the race over Amazon, Microsoft, and Google to provide Japan's government with air-gapped cloud services (FT).

BABA's Qwen AI is set to be integrated into Apple Intelligence in China (Reuters).

AMZN is building trade-compliant warehouses near key Chinese ports to court mainland sellers amid rising US competition from Shein and Temu (Nikkei).

INTC is using ASML's next-generation EUV machine to manufacture its Panther Lake laptop processors (Reuters).

LCID (Lucid) denied bankruptcy reports, saying it has sufficient liquidity into 2027, though it has engaged advisors to strengthen operations (FT).

DeepSeek is seeking fresh funding at a ~$70B valuation just weeks after closing a round at ~$50B, and could file IPO paperwork this year for a 2027 debut (FT, Bloomberg).
