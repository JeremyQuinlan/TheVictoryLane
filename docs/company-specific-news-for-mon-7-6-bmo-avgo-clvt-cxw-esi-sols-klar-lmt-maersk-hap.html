GEOPOLITICAL

- Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd announced they are shifting the AE15 Gemini service back through the Suez Canal, away from the Cape of Good Hope routing that had been in place since February 2025 — citing materially improved security conditions in the Middle East (Maersk press release, 7/6).

COMPANY NEWS

AVGO (Broadcom) extended its deep-rooted chip partnership with Apple through 2031, with Broadcom set to develop and supply custom ASIC silicon products across multiple generations of Apple hardware — a deal that cements Broadcom's role as a cornerstone supplier in Apple's silicon strategy.

CLVT (Clarivate) is divesting its Life Sciences & Healthcare segment to Altaris for $600MM, while reaffirming prior guidance — a clean portfolio trim that should sharpen the company's focus on its core IP and analytics businesses.

CXW (CoreCivic) closed the sale of two detention facilities for approximately $1.5B gross, with anticipated net proceeds of $1.1B — a meaningful balance sheet event for the private prison operator.

ESI/SOLS — Element Solutions (ESI) is being acquired by Solstice (SOLS) in a cash-and-stock deal valued at $14.5B. ESI holders receive $10/share in cash plus 0.5 SOLS shares, representing total consideration of $50.10 per share.

KLAR (Klarna) has filed for a U.S. banking charter, a move that would allow the BNPL giant to bring its domestic banking operations in-house. Klarna has held a European banking license since 2017 and currently serves U.S. customers through partner banks.

LMT (Lockheed Martin) is acquiring Ultra Maritime — a next-generation undersea warfare solutions provider — from Advent for $3.5B, bolstering its naval defense portfolio at a moment of heightened global submarine competition.

MSTR (Strategy) disclosed sales of 1,363 bitcoin during June 29–30 and an additional 2,225 bitcoin during July 1–5, and reported an $8.32B loss on digital assets for Q2 ending June 30 — of which $8.31B was unrealized.

MU (Micron) announced a long-term Strategic Customer Agreement with Ford for memory supply. Notably, Micron had already flagged a slate of SCAs on its recent earnings call, and that disclosure likely encompassed both the Ford deal and last week's GM agreement.

RAIL (FreightCar America) secured a new multi-year order for 1,900 railcars from a key customer, with deliveries scheduled through 2028 — a solid backlog addition for the manufacturer.

RCI (Rogers Communications) is buying the remaining 25% stake in Maple Leaf Sports & Entertainment from Kilmer Sports for C$4.35B, taking full ownership of one of Canada's premier sports and entertainment properties.

WULF (TeraWulf) signed a 20-year lease with Anthropic at its Justified Data campus in Hawesville, Kentucky, expected to generate approximately $19B in contracted revenue over the initial term. Separately, WULF is selling its 50.1% interest in the Abernathy Joint Venture to an investor group led by joint venture partner Fluidstack.
