MARKET OUTLOOK

Bank earnings are sending a clear signal: the U.S. economy is running hot, credit quality is holding, and capital markets activity is exceptional. The bull case is intact, but sustainability questions on trading revenues and rising expense guidance at JPM will keep the upside capped near-term. Dimon's tectonic plate analogy — geopolitical risk, sticky inflation, large fiscal deficits, elevated asset prices — is worth taking seriously even if the immediate data looks clean. Don't get complacent just because Q2 was strong.

COMPANY NEWS

BAC posted Q2 EPS of $1.21 vs. the Street's $1.13, on revenue of $31.5B vs. consensus $30.8B (Vital Knowledge). NII and NIM came in roughly inline while noninterest income beat. The efficiency ratio outperformed by roughly 100 basis points, and provisions of $1.36B came in below the expected $1.51B. Loans grew 8% and deposits 2%. The standout was the markets business: IB fees surged 50% to $2.138B, Equities trading rocketed 70% to $3.6B vs. the Street's $2.7B, and FICC rose 9% to $3.5B. Management flagged strong near-term pipelines and accelerating commercial borrowing. Bottom line: solid across the board, but elevated expectations and questions around Equities revenue sustainability limit the upside read.

JPM delivered Q2 EPS of $7.70 headline / $6.14 ex-items vs. consensus of $5.73, with the gap partly reflecting a $1.27/share Visa-related gain and a $0.29 gain on equity investments (Vital Knowledge). Total revenue rose 27% Y/Y headline (15% ex-items), loans up 10% Y/Y, deposits up 7%. Equities trading was the jaw-dropper: $6.02B vs. the Street's $3.978B, up 86%. FICC rose 6% to $6.1B, slightly below consensus $6.2B. NIM of 2.4% missed the Street's 2.45% forecast. Full-year NII guidance raised to $105.5B (from $104.5B), expenses guided higher to $107.5B from $105B. Jamie Dimon cited a "particularly favorable environment" but warned of tectonic risks beneath the surface — geopolitical tensions, sticky inflation, large fiscal deficits, and elevated asset prices. Bottom line: exceptional quarter, but the NIM miss, expense creep, and trading sustainability concerns will keep the stock honest.

WFC beat Q2 EPS handily at $2.00 vs. consensus $1.71, driven by a 13% jump in noninterest income (including a ~$250M JV acquisition gain), an efficiency ratio of 60% that came in several hundred basis points better than expected, and provisions of $914M vs. the Street's ~$1.2B (Vital Knowledge). Average loans rose 12% Y/Y, deposits up 10%. Management credited both broad macro strength and company-specific turnaround execution. Full-year NII and expense guidance were left unchanged. Bottom line: a strong print, though quality watchers will flag the ~$250M asset gain, a ~$0.04 tax benefit, and below-trend provisions as tailwinds that won't repeat every quarter.
