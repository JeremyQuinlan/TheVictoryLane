MARKET OUTLOOK

The violent rotation out of semis and into banks, healthcare, transports, and staples is the dominant theme — and it has legs, because positioning in chips was dangerously crowded heading into earnings season. TSM's mixed print (margin softness, rising capex, weak non-AI end markets) has given bears exactly the ammunition they needed to press the sector. The equal-weight S&P outperforming the cap-weighted index is a healthy sign for breadth, but don't confuse rotation with a broad rally — the market needs to see AI capex translate into revenue outside of hyperscalers before tech re-rates higher. Watch NFLX tonight as a cleaner read on consumer streaming demand.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Iran released an American citizen after 1.5 years in captivity; Trump called it a "gesture of goodwill" — this is more significant as a diplomatic signal than the Houthi noise (WSJ).

- Reuters reports Iran has told Houthis to close the Red Sea gateway if the U.S. strikes Iran's power network — a meaningful escalation threat worth monitoring (Reuters).

- JD Vance on Joe Rogan made clear the White House has no appetite for large-scale troop deployment or regime change in Iran; the off-ramp is a negotiated deal, not military victory (Fox News). Separately, WSJ notes that controlling the Strait of Hormuz would require far more troops and risk than currently contemplated (WSJ).

- The White House announced a 25% tariff on select Brazilian goods citing "unfair trade practices," though key categories — oil/gas, beef, coffee, oranges, aircraft parts — are exempt (NYT).

- South Korea's central bank hiked rates 25 basis points, its first increase in over three years, and signaled further tightening ahead (WSJ). Seoul also unveiled curbs on single-stock leveraged ETFs to reduce systemic risk (WSJ).

COMPANY NEWS

UNH (UnitedHealth) crushed Q2 estimates, posting EPS of $6.38 vs. the Street's $4.89, and raised full-year guidance to $19.75 from "greater than $18.25" — a full pass-through of the $1.50 beat, a more shareholder-friendly move than peer ELV did on Wednesday.

STT (State Street) delivered strong Q2 EPS of $3.65 vs. $3.33 expected, with revenue surging 17% to $4B and pre-tax margins expanding 470 basis points year-over-year on 645 basis points of operating leverage. Full-year fee revenue guidance raised to +12–13% from +7–9%.

GE (GE Aerospace) beat Q2 EPS at $2.02 vs. $1.86, with adjusted revenue spiking 24% to $12.6B vs. $11.8B expected. Margins were roughly inline at 21.7% (-130 basis points). Stock is under pressure despite the raise — rotation dynamics at work.

USB (US Bancorp) reported Q2 EPS of $1.35 (+21.6%) vs. $1.28 expected, with NII and noninterest income both ahead, efficiency ratio improving more than 200 basis points year-over-year, and provisions ~$60MM light. Full-year guidance raised.

CFG (Citizens Financial) beat Q2 at $1.30 vs. $1.25, driven by revenue upside on both NII and noninterest income, with guidance moving higher.

ABT (Abbott) posted Q2 EPS of $1.31 vs. $1.28 and nudged full-year EPS guidance to $5.45–$5.60 from $5.38–$5.58.

TSM (TSMC) guided to strong revenue but flagged H2 margin softness, rising capex, and weakness in non-AI consumer end markets — the trifecta that's crushing the broader chip complex today.

JBHT (JB Hunt) surged after Q2 EPS came in at $1.91 (+45%) vs. $1.74, on revenue of $3.49B vs. $3.25B expected.

UAL (United Airlines) beat Q2 EPS but elevated fuel costs are weighing on forward guidance.

MAN (Manpower) beat Q2 with EPS of $0.99 vs. $0.94 on revenue of $4.86B vs. $4.71B; Q3 guide roughly inline at $1.01 midpoint vs. $1.00 consensus.

LLY (Eli Lilly) announced a deal to acquire ATAI (AtaiBeckley) at $6.75/share cash plus a CVR worth up to $2.50.

UBER announced a bid to acquire Delivery Hero at €41.50/share, implying an equity value of $14.8B ($13.7B net of Uber's existing stake), with 7–9% EPS accretion expected by year three (Uber IR).

TSM peer ABB reported 12% organic revenue growth inline, with strong orders (+28% vs. +12% expected), but the headline was its ~$5.5B bid for Rotork — strategically sound but pricey (ABB IR).

Anthropic is scheduling investor meetings ahead of an expected IPO (CNBC). SpaceX's rocky public market debut is being watched as a bellwether for AI-linked IPO appetite, with lockup expirations beginning in early August (Reuters).
