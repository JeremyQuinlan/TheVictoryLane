RATES & FED

June CPI came in cooler than expected, sending 2-year yields tumbling 10 basis points while 30-year yields dipped just 2 basis points — a flattening move that signals relief on the short end but lingering long-run inflation concern. Markets now price roughly 30 basis points of rate hikes this year, down from ~45 basis points pre-CPI, with the July 29th meeting widely expected to be a hold. Fed Chair Warsh's inaugural House testimony struck a hawkish tone consistent with prior remarks, reiterating "no tolerance for persistently elevated inflation."

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

Tuesday was a session full of crosscurrents, and the net read is cautiously constructive but far from clean. The CPI beat is real, but energy is already reversing — Brent ripped back to $85 — and AI is proving genuinely inflationary, not just abstractly so. IBM's warning that corporate IT budgets are shifting hard toward data center hardware and away from software is either a company-specific disaster or the beginning of a painful sector rotation; the market is betting on the former, but the jury is still out. Bank earnings were exceptional across the board and validate the bull case on financials, though Y/Y trading and IB comps get harder from here. Until the Middle East resolves — and there's no resolution in sight — oil remains a sword hanging over the inflation narrative and the Fed's ability to pivot.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Trump sent Congress a fresh war notice on Iran (new 60-day clock started July 7), then scrapped his 20% ship surcharge plan — only to replace it with a vague demand that Gulf states make "massive investments" in the US for protection. Neither idea is workable, and the White House looks rudderless on Middle East strategy (Politico).

- Iran apparently rejected an MOU that was heavily skewed in its favor, deepening the stalemate. Reports indicate Trump is still pressing Netanyahu to withdraw IDF forces from Lebanon as part of a broader bid to placate Tehran (Axios).

- Yemen adds another pressure point — fighting between Houthis and Saudi Arabia risks disrupting Red Sea shipping — while Ukraine drone attacks are pushing Russia toward its worst energy crisis since the Soviet collapse, further tightening global supply (Bloomberg).

- China holds the oil price wild card: Beijing dramatically drew down inventories this spring, capping Brent's rise — but if it reverses and begins refilling stockpiles, prices could surge fast (NYT).

- NY Governor Hochul signed an executive order banning large data center construction in the state for up to a year (WSJ).

COMPANY NEWS

JPM, BAC, GS all posted strong Q2 beats. BAC earned $1.21 vs. the Street's $1.13, on revenue of $31.5B vs. $30.8B expected, with expense discipline and provisions of $1.36B well below the $1.51B consensus. GS was the blowout of the group: EPS of $20.98 vs. $14.49 expected, revenue +39% Y/Y to $20.3B, efficiency ratio of 57.4%. JPM similarly beat on noninterest income, expense controls, and lower provisions. WFC and C also posted EPS upside — Wells at $2.00 vs. $1.71, Citi at $3.15 vs. $2.74 — but both stocks fell, underscoring how elevated expectations had become.

IBM issued a damaging negative preannouncement: revenue guided to $17.2B (up just 1% Y/Y, well below the $17.9B consensus), gross margins down 70 basis points, EPS of $2.93 vs. $3.02 expected. The company cited customers shifting budgets toward data center hardware and away from software — raising the question of whether this is a broad AI-driven IT reallocation or IBM-specific rot.

HCA cut full-year guidance, citing a payer mix shift toward the uninsured (patients losing exchange coverage) and a drop in surgery volumes — inpatient down 2.3%, outpatient down 3.4%. The read-through weighed on hospitals and surgical supply names broadly.

UBER is in advanced talks to acquire Delivery Hero, with a deal potentially imminent (Bloomberg). AAPL is in discussions to use PrismML's AI compression technology to run powerful models on-device rather than in the cloud (CNBC). NVDA has begun H200 shipments to China in very low volumes (Reuters), while also tightening its Asia authorized-buyer list to curb chip trafficking (FT). BIIB shares fell after disappointing Alzheimer's tau trial data (Bloomberg). TREX issued an upside Q2 preannouncement and raised full-year guidance on strong consumer demand. LCID denied bankruptcy speculation, saying it has liquidity well into 2027. BRK/B's Buffett stated his goal is to divest all Berkshire shares within 8 years. DeepSeek is seeking fresh funding at a ~$70B valuation — up from ~$50B just weeks ago — and could file IPO paperwork as soon as this year for a 2027 debut (FT, Bloomberg).
