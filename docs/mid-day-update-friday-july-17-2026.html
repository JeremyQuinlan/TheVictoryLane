MARKET OUTLOOK

The tech-led selloff has a familiar, uncomfortable feel — two consecutive days of heavy rotation out of mega-cap and AI-adjacent names while the equal-weight S&P holds up, suggesting this is a valuation/sentiment reset in a specific trade rather than broad market deterioration. The Moonshot Kimi release is being reframed (from pure DeepSeek-style panic toward a more nuanced compute-intensive read), which explains the bounce in DELL, MU, and HPE off pre-market lows — but the OpenAI/Anthropic market share overhang is real and won't resolve quickly. Geopolitics is a genuine macro headwind now, not just background noise: Hormuz disruptions, Red Sea risk, and Black Sea pressure are collectively a supply-chain and energy-price problem. The coming week's earnings — particularly Google and ServiceNow Wednesday night — will be decisive for whether AI capex narratives hold or crack further. Corporate insider selling at near-record pace (Bloomberg) is not a signal to ignore.

COMPANY NEWS

TRV (Travelers) delivered a massive Q2 beat — EPS of $10.04 vs. the Street's $5.37 — driven by a headline combined ratio of 83.6%, more than 1,000 basis points better than expected, plus stronger investment income. The stock is spiking and pulling the broader insurance sector with it.

NFLX (Netflix) is tumbling after providing underwhelming Q3 guidance and announcing it will disclose less data on engagement — precisely the metric investors are growing most concerned about.

ISRG (Intuitive Surgical) beat Q2 EPS ($2.80 vs. $2.50 consensus), helped by IEEPA tariff refunds (+8 cents) and gross margins of 70% (200-plus basis points above plan), but full-year procedure growth guidance disappointed and the stock is selling off sharply.

FITB (Fifth Third) posted Q2 EPS of $1.02 vs. 98 cents expected, with the beat driven by cost controls (efficiency ratio of 57.1%, several hundred basis points better), lower provisions, and capital markets fees up 71% Y/Y. Full-year guidance reiterated with tightened ranges.

TFC (Truist) posted strong Q2 EPS of $1.23 vs. $1.07 expected on higher noninterest income and healthy credit, but NII/NIM missed slightly and full-year revenue guidance was trimmed to +3.5–4% from +4%.

AA (Alcoa) beat Q2 adjusted EPS ($2.12 vs. $2.03) with very strong free cash flow ($422MM vs. $80MM expected), but missed on sales ($3.96B vs. $4.01B) and EBITDA ($901MM vs. $919MM), and trimmed alumina production guidance.

META is hiring AWS senior executive Dave Brown, a clear signal it is moving forward with plans to become a hyperscaler and sell compute capacity externally (WSJ).

IBM's board and advisors are aware the stock's weakness following its profit warning leaves it vulnerable to an activist campaign (WSJ).

BRK/B (Berkshire) likely repurchased $5–11B of stock in Q2, up sharply from just $235MM in Q1 (Barron's).

PYPL (PayPal)'s board views the Advent/Stripe bid as inadequate and believes it faces regulatory and financing obstacles (Reuters).
