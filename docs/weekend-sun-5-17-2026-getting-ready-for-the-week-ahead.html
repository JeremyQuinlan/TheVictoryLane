The FOMC minutes are due Wednesday afternoon and are expected to be hawkish, though these reflect discussions from before the hot April data was released. Japanese investors could potentially sell US Treasuries to repatriate cash and capitalize on rising JGB yields (FT).

MARKET OUTLOOK

At around 7400 on the SPX, three bear forces take precedent over earnings strength: Iran uncertainty and fallout, elevated sovereign yields with accelerating inflation (both tied to Iran), and tech bubble risks including the massive backlog of tech IPOs hitting markets through year-end. While March earnings were robust and April looks equally strong (if CSCO and AMAT are any indication), these headwinds outweigh the bullish earnings momentum. An Iran deal at this point won't trigger material SPX gains and could spark a sell-the-news reaction since the market already anticipates an agreement, and reaching an accord won't erase what's already happened—supply chain disruptions won't be fully resolved for years. The so-called "de-equitization" trend that powered the bull market is ending as Mag7 companies pour cash into infrastructure instead of buybacks while IPO volumes surge (FT).
