RATES & FED

Treasuries are rallying modestly with yields flat to down 4 basis points across the curve. Markets are now pricing roughly 30 basis points of H2 Fed rate hikes, down from a recent peak of ~40 basis points — a shift driven by falling oil prices and Warsh's hawkish credibility helping anchor inflation expectations (FT).

MARKET OUTLOOK

The underlying market is healthier than the index-level numbers suggest — the equal-weight S&P is green while cap-weighted tech drags. The OpenAI IPO delay story is the real tell today: if AI capex is increasingly dependent on capital markets generosity, any issuance window closure threatens the data center buildout thesis directly. Pair that with Washington tightening the screws on frontier model releases and Chinese open-source eating share, and the AI infrastructure trade deserves a harder look. Away from tech, the détente between the US and Iran — signaled by Saudi Arabia resuming Ras Tanura oil loadings for the first time in four months (Reuters) — is a genuine structural shift keeping energy under pressure.

GEOPOLITICAL

- Saudi Arabia resumed oil loadings at its Ras Tanura terminal after a ~4-month war-related halt, a clear signal the US-Iran relationship is moving toward sustained détente (Reuters).

- Chinese AI models are rapidly gaining global token consumption share due to stronger capabilities and significantly lower costs, pressuring US frontier labs (NYT).

- Eurozone 12-month inflation expectations fell to +3.5% in May from +4% in April; 3- and 5-year expectations held at +2.9% and +2.4% (ECB).

- Washington is asserting itself in frontier AI model releases, asking OpenAI to stagger the GPT-5.6 launch to a select group of early users over national security concerns (The Information).
