MARKET OUTLOOK

Tuesday's rally was a tech-driven illusion — the equal-weight S&P actually dipped, with weakness spread across financials, healthcare, telecom, staples, and energy. The tech narrative is fracturing into three mutually exclusive buckets (pick-and-shovel, hyperscalers, legacy software/services), and the chips-vs-hyperscaler trade is looking dangerously stretched — one cautious capex comment during CQ2 earnings could spark a violent rotation. Memory price surges are compounding the problem, squeezing margins and demand across the supply chain, with Chinese handset OEMs already slashing shipment targets (Nikkei). Add tariff uncertainty back into the mix — the White House appears set to formally abandon USMCA — and the bull case requires everything to go right simultaneously. It won't.

COMPANY NEWS

CNXC (Concentrix) is the story of the day — shares tumbled more than 20% after FQ2 EPS/revenue came in essentially inline but full-year guidance was cut to EPS of $10.83–$11.18 (from $11.48–$12.07) with FX-neutral sales growth of +0.25–1.25% (from +1.5–3%). The stock trades at ~2x PE with a free cash flow yield north of 50%, and the market didn't care — zero patience for bad news from legacy software/services names.

AVAV (AeroVironment) spiked after FQ4 EPS of $1.84 and EBITDA of $140MM crushed estimates of $1.41/$126MM; sales of $641.6MM also beat the $556MM consensus, and book-to-bill hit an impressive 1.4x. Modest FY27 EBITDA guidance of $315MM vs. the Street's $346MM was the lone blemish.

MSTR (Strategy) dealt a psychological blow to the entire crypto complex after announcing it will fund share buybacks via bitcoin sales — BTC dropped ~2.5% and crypto-adjacent names COIN and HOOD followed suit (Barron's).

T, TMUS, VZ — all three major US telecoms hit fresh 52-week lows amid intensifying concerns that SPCX (SpaceX/Starlink) is positioning to become a serious competitor in consumer mobile.

APD (Air Products) announced it will not proceed with its Louisiana Clean Energy Complex project.

LCII (LCI Industries) will be acquired by PATK (Patrick Industries) in an all-stock deal valuing LCII at ~$116/share (~$2.8B equity), with holders receiving 1.244 PATK shares.

PM (Philip Morris) — the FDA will allow ZYN to be marketed as less harmful than cigarettes (Axios).

ENPH/SEDG rallied on a Reuters report that Washington is working to ban Chinese energy inverters (Reuters).

FDS (FactSet) announced a strategic partnership with Google Cloud to develop AI-powered solutions for the financial industry.

ABBV/MRK — a bipartisan House committee opened an investigation into whether both drugmakers participated in Chinese clinical trials that benefited Beijing's military capabilities (Reuters).

CNDT (Conduent) will sell its Tolling business to Quarterhill for $70MM cash plus a 7% equity stake in Quarterhill.

P (Everpure) rallied after Reuters reported activist Jana Partners has built a new stake in the company.
