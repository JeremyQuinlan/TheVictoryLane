MARKET OUTLOOK

Markets are caught in a tug-of-war: decent earnings from INTC and SAP provide a floor, but GOOGL and TSLA's underwhelming results this week served as a real gut-check for mega-cap AI optimism. The oil complex is the wildcard — Brent pulling back on ceasefire trial balloons is supportive today, but with Hormuz and the Red Sea both under threat, any supply disruption could reprice energy and yields violently. Next week's Fed decision, Q2 GDP, and PCE data all land in the same 48-hour window — that's a genuine minefield. The bull case rests entirely on Trump eventually pursuing a negotiated settlement with Iran; the bear case is that he can't, and energy markets know it.

---

GEOPOLITICAL

- Iran rejected a ceasefire proposal Trump transmitted via Iraq's Prime Minister (NYT). Trump is reportedly "frustrated" and entering "revenge mode," believing Tehran only responds to military force (WSJ). His rhetoric to Axios Thursday was notably heated, yet it remains unclear why intensified airstrikes would change conditions on the ground.

- The Houthis are threatening to open a second front, targeting Saudi ships in and around the Red Sea — compounding the energy market risk at a moment when both the Strait of Hormuz and Red Sea face disruption and global inventories are already deeply depleted.

- The Saudi nuclear deal appears to be in jeopardy after Trump added a new condition — Riyadh joining the Abraham Accords and normalizing with Israel — seemingly out of nowhere, illustrating how difficult it is to negotiate with this White House.

- The White House formally unveiled its new tariff regime: 10-12% import taxes under Section 301 applied to goods from 80+ countries. This was expected and largely holds rates consistent with the temporary 10% global tariff that expired today. Exemptions include oil/gas and USMCA-covered goods (Bloomberg, CNBC, NYT).

COMPANY NEWS

INTC (Intel) delivered a massive Q2 beat: EPS 42c vs. Street 21c, revenue $16.1B vs. Street $14.4B, with Client, Data Center, and Foundry all above plan. Gross margins came in at 41.8% vs. Street 39.2%. Q3 guide equally bullish at EPS 38c vs. Street 27c on revenue of ~$16.3B vs. Street $15.06B. CEO Lip-Bu Tan cited "unprecedented demand" from AI and flagged ~$20B in capex this year to capitalize.

SAP reported solid Q2 demand metrics — current cloud backlog +26% FXN (Street +23.8%) and cloud revenue +24% (Street +23.2%) — though margin pressure drove an earnings miss. Full-year op. profit outlook trimmed for acquisition dilution, but management sees revenue acceleration in 2027. This is the third better-than-feared legacy software print this week following NOW and ROP.

EW (Edwards Lifesciences) posted Q2 sales growth of +12.5% (Street +10.3%) with EPS of 78c vs. Street 74c. Full-year sales guide lifted to +10-11% from +9-11%.

GEHC (GE Healthcare) preannounced Q2 EPS above expectations with organic revenue growth of 3.5% (inline); full-year outlook reiterated. CFO announced plans to step down.

THC (Tenet) beat Q2 sales and EBITDA estimates, raised full-year guidance, and announced a $2B buyback increase — a welcome contrast to peer HCA's recent negative update.

DECK (Deckers) posted FQ1 EPS of 94c vs. Street 89c, driven by gross margin outperformance. Full-year EPS guide raised modestly, though entirely due to a lower tax rate assumption rather than operational upside.

SAM (Boston Beer) earned $4.96 in Q2 vs. Street $4.84, with depletions -6% vs. feared -9%. Full-year guidance maintained.

AMKR (Amkor) announced a multi-year Nvidia partnership for advanced semiconductor packaging and test technologies; Nvidia will provide a prepayment to support U.S. capacity expansion.

DLR (Digital Realty) beat Q2 FFO and raised full-year FFO guidance to $8.10-8.15 (FXN) from $7.95-8.05. Q1 bookings dropped to $307MM from $707MM, but management signed two hyperscale leases in July totaling $410MM.

IBM CEO expressed confidence in recapturing deals that slipped out of Q2, defending the mainframe as a critical long-term technology (WSJ).

ORCL (Oracle) landed a 10-year Pentagon software contract worth up to $7B — not a bad headline, but secondary to investor concerns about massive capex commitments and OpenAI's ability to meet its obligations.

MSFT (Microsoft) will replace OpenAI models with its own in PowerPoint and Bing (Bloomberg).

META faces higher borrowing costs on its El Paso data center project as AI-linked debt investors demand increased compensation (FT).

PSKY/WBD (Paramount/Warner Bros. Discovery) — a federal judge extended the merger closing restriction by 14 days to August 18 (Variety).

NEM (Newmont) reported Q2 EPS of $2.10 vs. Street $2.02 with better free cash flow, though EBITDA fell slightly short. Full-year production guidance reiterated with costs tracking "well below" targets.

BYD (Boyd Gaming) posted Q2 EBITDA of $350.5MM vs. Street $339MM; Las Vegas destination business remains soft while Midwest/South and Online segments outperformed.

Stripe is in talks to acquire AI model marketplace OpenRouter in a deal potentially worth $10B (WSJ). HSBC agreed to sell its Singapore life and health insurance unit to Allianz for $2.1B (WSJ). Wise shares fell after regulators rejected its U.S. banking license application (FT). SpaceX targeting Friday July 24 for the next Starship test flight after a one-day delay (Barron's).
