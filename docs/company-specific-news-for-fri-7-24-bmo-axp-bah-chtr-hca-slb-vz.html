COMPANY NEWS

AXP Q2 EPS came in at $4.53 vs. the Street's $4.41, with revenue roughly inline. Full-year revenue guidance was nudged higher to +10% (from +9-10%), though the EPS outlook of $17.30-$17.90 was reiterated despite the beat, as management plans to ramp investment spending in H2. The EPS upside was largely a credit story — provisions of $1.08B came in well below the $1.38B consensus. Total billed business (FXN) rose 9% in Q2, consistent with Q1, while US consumer billed business accelerated to +11%. New card adds held steady at 3M, and net card fee growth ticked down to +15% from +16% in Q1, though management guides Q3 to accelerate with Q4 in the high-teens. Solid print, but don't be surprised if the stock gets dinged on the lack of revenue upside and the unchanged EPS guide.

HCA reported official Q2 results and reaffirmed 2026 guidance, but the numbers are fully consistent with the July 14 preannouncement — no incremental information of note.

SLB delivered a clean beat across the board: EPS of 55c vs. 51c expected, revenue of $8.9B vs. $8.68B, and EBITDA of $1.89B vs. $1.78B. International markets drove sequential growth, led by offshore activity in Latin America, Europe & Africa, and Asia, offsetting ongoing Middle East disruptions. The Data Center Solutions business is on track to exceed $1B in annualized revenue run rate by year-end, with management targeting $2B+ as they exit 2027. Strength in both the core and the high-growth data center pivot makes this one of the cleaner prints of the week.

BAH posted FQ1 EBITDA of $334MM vs. the Street's $304MM, with the beat driven by margin expansion of 130 basis points. Book-to-bill came in at a healthy 1.5x, and full-year guidance was reiterated. National Security demand is described as accelerating; Civil remains a drag. Given the macro uncertainty around government spending, this result should be a relief — margins held, the backlog is building, and guidance is intact.

CHTR missed on EBITDA ($5.44B vs. $5.58B expected), with margins down 110 basis points year-over-year, and free cash flow of $969MM fell short of the $1.12B consensus. Revenue was inline at -1.7% to $13.5B. Sub trends were mixed: mobile net adds of +406K beat the +340K estimate, video losses of -21K were better than the feared -57K, but internet losses of -172K were worse than the -150K expected. Not a great report, though expectations were already in the basement — wireless momentum is real, but the internet sub erosion is the number that will dog the stock.

VZ was the standout of the morning — EPS of $1.30 vs. $1.27, EBITDA of $13.72B vs. $13.65B, and free cash flow of $6.4B crushed the $5.4B estimate. Postpaid phone net adds of +184K nearly doubled the 105K consensus, and broadband adds of +348K beat the +302K estimate. Revenue missed, but entirely due to a ~20% year-over-year drop in equipment revenue tied to lower upgrade volumes and a deliberate pullback on device subsidies — not a demand problem. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $4.99-$5.04 (from $4.95-$4.99), free cash flow growth now guided at +9-10% (from +7%), and the buyback is being expanded. The turnaround is working.
