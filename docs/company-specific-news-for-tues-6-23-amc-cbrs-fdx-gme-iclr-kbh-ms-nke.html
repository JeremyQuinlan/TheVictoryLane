COMPANY NEWS

CBRS (Cerebras) delivered FQ1 upside on operating income — posting an op. loss of just $3.5M versus the Street's $31.5M loss forecast — with core revenue up +92% Y/Y and +12% Q/Q to $191.3M (vs. consensus $181.4M). Q2 guidance of $194M in core sales tops the Street's $173M estimate, and full-year 2026 is guided to ~$860M vs. consensus $825M. That said, the beat was widely anticipated, the qualitative commentary was bullish but unsurprising, and the OpenAI/AWS deals cited in the press release were previously announced — so don't expect this to move the needle on AI sentiment in a meaningful way.

FDX (FedEx) posted FQ4 EPS of $6.31, with revenue coming in at $25B — nearly $1B above the $24.06B consensus. Operating margin of 8.4% came in roughly 25 basis points below expectations. Strength in U.S. domestic and international priority yields, plus transformation cost savings, drove the beat, partially offset by higher purchased transportation costs, wage rates, and the drag from global trade policy changes. Guidance is complicated by a fiscal year-end shift (from May 31 to December 31) and the FedEx Freight spinoff, but for C2026 the company sees 11% revenue growth, adjusted EPS of ~$17.50 at the midpoint, and $1B in opportunistic buybacks.

GME (GameStop) announced CEO Ryan Cohen is voluntarily forfeiting his previously awarded performance compensation, citing a desire to keep leadership "fully focused on GameStop's operating performance and its proposed eBay acquisition." The move appears designed to clear the air around a distraction that had drawn scrutiny ahead of the EBAY bid process.

ICLR (ICON) reported FQ1 results with revenue and EBITDA coming in modestly above expectations, but the standout number is the book-to-bill ratio at a very healthy 1.42x. Full-year 2026 guidance, originally issued May 27, was reiterated in full.

KBH (KB Home) posted a slight FQ2 EPS miss at $0.43 vs. the Street's $0.45, though revenue, gross margins, and SG&A were broadly in line. Updated FY guidance calls for 10,500–11,000 home deliveries (vs. prior range of 10,000–11,500) with gross margins of 16.1–16.5%, above the Street's ~15.8% forecast. Management flagged sequentially higher volumes and margins in each of the final two quarters. File this one under "could have been worse" given the headwinds facing housing.

MS (Morgan Stanley) saw a negative headline after the close — a private credit fund capping redemptions at 5% — but context matters here. The fund is small, redemption requests were only 7.2% of units outstanding, the 5% cap is standard disclosed terms across virtually every comparable vehicle, and the fund's own shareholder letter was relatively sanguine on the redemption outlook. The "fund caps withdrawals" headline genre is getting stale; it signals awareness of private credit stress, not a new escalation of it.

NKE (Nike) announced a CFO transition — David Denton, former Pfizer CFO, takes over from Matthew Friend in August — and pre-announced that FQ4 EPS (due Tuesday, June 30 AMC) will beat expectations due to a tariff refund not included in prior guidance. Excluding that item, results are "expected to be generally inline with previously provided guidance." The CFO change adds anxiety to an already nervous setup heading into the FQ4 print, where the Street will be scrutinizing F27 guidance closely — consensus currently models EPS of ~$1.84 in F27 vs. ~$1.50 in F26, a meaningful jump that needs to be earned.
