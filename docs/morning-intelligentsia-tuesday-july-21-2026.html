GEOPOLITICAL

- Trump is at a critical decision point on Iran: either a 10-day ceasefire gets done soon, or the US escalates dramatically alongside Israel. Sec. Rubio has kept the diplomatic door open, but Axios warns the window is closing fast (Axios).

- Israel believes Iran has relocated significant covert nuclear infrastructure — including centrifuges — to Pickaxe Mountain; Trump has said publicly he's prepared to bomb it (WSJ).

- Strait of Hormuz traffic has slumped materially since the US reimposed its blockade on Iran, adding a tangible supply-side squeeze to oil markets (CNBC). Bears also flag Houthi threats of a naval blockade around Saudi Arabia and Ukrainian drone strikes hammering Russian energy infrastructure as compounding supply risks.

- The White House is weighing options to block or heavily restrict American companies' use of open-source Chinese AI models — significant given how much market share those models have taken from OpenAI and Anthropic and how much they've helped compress token costs (Axios, WSJ). Separately, China is considering its own export restrictions on AI model weights and chips (FT).

- Trump announced 50% tariffs on select Canadian goods (wine, hockey sticks, cement) under Section 232, effective ~August 20. Energy, potash, autos, steel/aluminum are exempt — the optics are mostly symbolic, but it reinforces that US trade policy remains unpredictable (White House).

COMPANY NEWS

CALX (Calix) posted non-GAAP EPS of 47c vs. Street 40c in FQ2, but GMs came in at 54.8% vs. 55.6% expected due to surging memory costs. FQ3 guidance is a problem: GM midpoint of 52% vs. Street 55.6%, EPS 41c vs. 46c. The company is attempting to pass memory surcharges on to customers — a dynamic likely to resurface across tech earnings season.

CCK (Crown Holdings) beat Q2 EPS by 16% ($2.49 vs. Street $2.16) on global bev can volume growth of 5%, with double-digit gains in Asia. Full-year guidance raised to $8.30-8.50 from $7.90-8.30.

STLD (Steel Dynamics) delivered Q2 EBITDA of $921MM vs. Street $884MM and sales of $6.1B vs. $5.58B, citing strengthening steel fundamentals and extended backlogs.

WRB (WR Berkley) reported Q2 EPS of $1.27 vs. Street $1.08; combined ratio beat by ~150 basis points at the headline level, investment income of ~$419MM vs. ~$384MM expected.

ZION (Zions) headline Q2 EPS of $3.05 looks impressive until you strip out $1.31 of one-time gains — adjusted that's $1.74 vs. Street $1.63, with the remainder driven by lower provisions (low quality) while NII/NIM missed. Full-year guidance unchanged.

ORCL (Oracle) credit default swap costs hit near 18-year highs Monday; bonds and stock both weakened on mounting concern over balance sheet deterioration and AI capex commitments (Bloomberg).

IEP (Icahn Enterprises) selling Pep Boys to Mavis Tire Express for ~$700MM cash (WSJ).

CAH (Cardinal Health) acquiring AdaptHealth's Diabetes Health unit and Strive Medical in deals totaling ~$360MM cash.

WBD/PSKY — a federal judge halted the Warner/Paramount merger pending a state-level challenge; hearing set for August 3 (FT).

SPCX (SpaceX) will release its inaugural public earnings report after the close on August 4.

TSM (TSMC) set to raise chipmaking prices up to 10% in 2027, with advanced nodes potentially higher, to offset rising costs (Nikkei).

GOOGL (Google) is developing a new AI chip called "Frozen" that would embed Gemini model components directly into silicon for major efficiency gains; potential deployment as early as 2028 (The Information). Separately, Google search traffic is at record highs as AI serves results — but traffic to the broader web is declining sharply (NYT).
