MARKET OUTLOOK

The single biggest driver of US equities remains AI, and the hyperscaler-vs-pick-and-shovel rotation is the trade to watch — the crowded pick-and-shovel bet is vulnerable the moment any major cloud spender hints at capex moderation, which could trigger a violent unwind. Q2 earnings season will be the proving ground, with hyperscaler capex guidance the defining question. On the macro side, the US-Iran détente trajectory and falling oil prices are the most important disinflationary tailwinds markets have going for them right now. If Brent keeps drifting lower, central banks get cover — but Ukraine's drone campaign against Russian energy infrastructure is a real upside risk to oil that shouldn't be ignored.

GEOPOLITICAL

- A potential US-Iran meeting in Doha is the day's most watched geopolitical event; both sides appear reluctant to resume full hostilities, and the bull case for expanded oil supply (higher OPEC quotas, UAE/Iraq exits, removal of Iranian sanctions, Hormuz bypass infrastructure) remains intact. Bears point to continued kinetic clashes and discrepant rhetoric between the two sides as reasons the Middle East stays a risk factor (Reuters).

- Ukraine's aggressive drone strikes against Russian energy infrastructure pose an upside risk to global oil and gas prices that could complicate central bank efforts to cool inflation.

- The BOJ faces growing political pressure to slow its tightening pace, even as the yen slides to 40-year lows and Japan retains FX intervention rhetoric without acting (Reuters).

- Trade uncertainty is rising as the White House moves to restore pre-SCOTUS average tariff levels and the USMCA enters extended limbo.

- South Korea has tightened home-buying restrictions as the memory chip boom pushes property prices higher (Bloomberg).
