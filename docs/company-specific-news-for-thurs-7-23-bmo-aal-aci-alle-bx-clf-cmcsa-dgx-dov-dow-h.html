MARKET OUTLOOK

The Thursday BMO earnings wave is broadly constructive — defense, healthcare, and select industrials are putting up clean beat-and-raise prints, which should help sustain the broader rally. Consumer-facing names are the soft spot: AAL's fuel headwinds, ACI's grocery miss-and-slash, and TSCO's comp stumble all point to a consumer that's still spending but increasingly selectively. Software sentiment is stabilizing after back-to-back solid prints from NOW and ROP, though SAP's results will be the next real test. Don't get too comfortable on the staples trade — ACI's print is ugly enough to drag KR and WMT into the crossfire.

COMPANY NEWS

AAL (American Airlines) beat Q2 EPS handily (15c vs. Street 3c) as demand held firm, but the guide is the story — Q3 EPS seen at -30c vs. Street +31c, with full-year roughly breakeven vs. Street +61c, all owing to a ~$2.2B year-over-year spike in fuel expense.

ACI (Albertsons) badly missed FQ1 EPS (42c vs. Street 54c) with comps of -0.8% vs. Street -0.55% as unit trends softened and the consumer turned cautious. Full-year EPS guidance slashed to a $1.80 midpoint from $2.27. CFO Sharon McCollam also announced plans to retire later this year — not a great backdrop for a leadership transition.

HOG (Harley-Davidson) beat Q2 EPS (75c vs. Street 67c) with HDMC EBITDA margins up 110 basis points year-over-year on restructuring gains, global inventories down 17%, and NA retail up 3%. Full-year HDMC op. income guide raised to $10-50MM from -$40-+$10MM.

BX (Blackstone) delivered distributable EPS of $1.52 vs. Street $1.33, with inflows of $68.3B crushing the $54B estimate across all four major segments. Total AUM hit $1.34T. Performance revenue — up 68% to $793MM — did a lot of the work, which some will discount.

NDAQ (Nasdaq) posted strong Q2 EPS of $1.07 (+25% year-over-year) vs. Street 99c, with revenue up 15% to $1.5B vs. Street $1.46B.

DGX (Quest Diagnostics) beat on EPS ($3.12 vs. Street $2.83) and sales ($3.04B vs. Street $2.97B), raising full-year EPS guidance to $11.05-11.25 from $10.63-10.83. Organic revenue growth exceeded 10% across physician, hospital, and consumer channels.

TMO (Thermo Fisher) beat Q2 EPS ($6.03 vs. Street $5.72) and sales ($11.99B vs. Street $11.7B), with organic growth of 5% running roughly 200 basis points above consensus. Management says end markets "continue to strengthen."

WST (West Pharma) beat Q2 EPS ($2.37 vs. Street $2.08) and sales ($872.3MM vs. Street $841.5MM), raising full-year EPS to $8.85-9.05 from $8.40-8.75. Net sales rose 12.7% organically, driven by GLP-1 elastomers and Biologics HVP demand.

LMT (Lockheed Martin) blew out Q2 EPS ($7.94 vs. Street $7.13) and revenue ($20.1B vs. Street $19.3B); free cash flow of $2.9B was roughly double consensus, book-to-bill was ~3.2x, and full-year EPS raised to $29.95-30.65 from $29.35-30.25.

RTX (Raytheon) posted Q2 EPS of $1.89 (+21%) vs. Street $1.66, with organic sales +16% to $24.7B vs. Street $22.9B — all three units beat. Backlog grew to $289B from $271B in Q1; full-year EPS raised to $7.10-7.25 from $6.70-6.90.

HON (Honeywell) delivered Q2 EPS of $1.95 vs. Street $1.83 ex-Aerospace, with organic revenue +4% (~200 basis points above plan) and orders surging 16%. Full-year organic growth guide raised to +3-4% from +2-3%, EPS to $8.05-8.35 from $7.90-8.30.

CLF (Cleveland-Cliffs) came in roughly in line on EPS (-20c vs. Street -21c) but EBITDA outperformed ($286MM vs. Street $260MM). Management guided Q3 EBITDA to "more than double" sequentially, implying north of $572MM vs. Street $571MM, with Q4 up again from there. Free cash flow turned positive and debt reduction has begun.

DOW (Dow Inc.) beat Q2 EPS ($1.44 vs. Street $1.31); sales and EBITDA also modestly ahead. Cost savings target raised ~$200MM. Q3 EBITDA guide of $1.75B came in just under the Street's $1.86B. Macro read: packaging resilient, data centers robust, U.S. consumer holding, China soft, U.S. housing still weak.

CMCSA (Comcast) beat on pro forma revenue ($29.56B vs. Street $29.3B) and EBITDA ($8.92B vs. Street $8.84B), with free cash flow ~$1B above plan at $4.6B. Wireless net adds of ~450K topped the 384K estimate. Broadband and video sub losses were no worse than feared. Content & Experiences revenue surged 22.9% to $10.72B. The bar was low — this probably clears it, but the structural sub-shedding trend isn't going away.

ROP (Roper Technologies) beat Q2 EPS/revenue ($5.38/$2.11B vs. Street $5.29/$2.095B) and repurchased $1.2B in stock (~14% of market cap annualized). Full-year guide raised. Second clean beat-and-raise from a software name after NOW — sentiment is stabilizing, with SAP the next test.

TMUS (T-Mobile) beat on EBITDA ($9.5B vs. Street $9.4B) and free cash flow ($4.8B vs. Street $4.6B), but revenue missed slightly ($22.8B vs. Street $22.9B) and postpaid net account adds of 277K fell 13% year-over-year. Guide mostly maintained. Not a bad print, but investors were likely looking for more.
