MARKET OUTLOOK

Markets are kicking off Q3 on a soft note — no single catalyst, just a mild accumulation of caution: weak Asia PMIs, Nike's downbeat demand commentary, and tariff uncertainty creeping back as the White House prepares to formally abandon USMCA. Thursday's jobs print (Street at +115K vs. +172K prior) is the real near-term test. Brent's continued slide is a genuine tailwind for inflation and yields, and the cooler Eurozone CPI removes ECB pressure — both constructive. Until sustained revenue momentum shows up in bellwether consumer names, and until Warsh gives the market something clearer than "task force," the path of least resistance is sideways-to-lower.

COMPANY NEWS

NKE (Nike) posted FQ4 EPS of 72c, but that included a 52c IEEPA tariff refund — strip that out and it's 20c vs. the Street's 12c. Sales fell 4% on an FX-neutral basis; China was -17% vs. the feared -21.75%. Management flagged worsening demand linearity through the quarter and trimmed the revenue outlook to down low-to-mid-single digits (from low-single digits prior), though EPS is still expected to hold roughly flat on better margins. Sportswear and Jordan Streetwear (~50% of sales combined) remain particularly soft; running is the bright spot. An analyst day is set for November 16–17.

STZ (Constellation Brands) beat FQ1 EPS at $3.43 comparable vs. the Street's $3.18, with sales of $2.43B vs. $2.388B expected. Full-year EPS guidance was reiterated. Beer depletions fell only 0.3% vs. the feared -0.6%; beer operating margins of 39% were ~85 basis points ahead of plan. The reiterate-despite-the-beat is modestly underwhelming given a still-difficult macro backdrop.

PRGS (Progress Software) delivered solid FQ2 upside — EPS $1.62 vs. Street $1.49 — on revenue of $253M (+6% FX-neutral) vs. $243M expected, with operating margins of 40% roughly 50 basis points ahead of plan. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $6.09–$6.21 from $5.91–$6.03. ARR growth remains muted.

AA (Alcoa) is acquiring South32's bauxite, alumina, and aluminum assets for up to $4.85B ($3.1B cash + ~$1B stock upfront, plus a $750M CVR). Expected to be immediately EPS and FCF accretive.

FDX (FedEx) is near a deal to sell its third-party logistics business to CMA CGM for $1.4B in cash (FT).

FMC (FMC Corp) is receiving a ~$400M strategic minority investment from Tessenderlo Group at $13.30/share vs. Tuesday's close of $11.50.

ALGN (Align Technology) faces an EU antitrust probe into its Invisalign sales practices (WSJ).

BE (Bloom Energy) and Brookfield expanded their strategic partnership from $5B to $25B, citing surging demand from hyperscalers and AI infrastructure developers.

MSFT (Microsoft) is preparing to cut thousands of jobs — fewer than 2.5% of its workforce — as it continues to control costs (Business Insider).

PM (Philip Morris): FDA will allow ZYN to be marketed as less harmful than cigarettes (Axios).

CRCL/COIN (Circle/Coinbase) slumped Tuesday after Visa, Mastercard, and Stripe announced a competing stablecoin called Open USD (Barron's).

GETY/SSTK (Getty Images/Shutterstock): Getty is walking away from the merger and will retain an advisor for strategic financing alternatives.
