MARKETS

SPX gained +1.08% while Nasdaq climbed +1.54%. The Dow jumped 645 points (+1.31%) and R2K spiked 70 points (+2.56%). Brent crude sank ~5.70% to ~$105. DXY fell 25 basis points. Gold jumped +1.45%, silver rose +3.15%, and Bitcoin ticked up ~0.85% to ~$77.6K.

RATES & FED

Treasuries saw big gains with yields falling 7-11 basis points across the curve, helped largely by the oil price drop. Markets still place >50% odds on a Fed hike by year-end despite the dovish undertones from recent minutes suggesting rate cuts remain on the table if Middle East tensions ease.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The Iran situation presents a classic lose-lose scenario — either Trump escalates militarily (exacerbating supply chain strains) or strikes a deal cementing Iran's Hormuz control. Even with some tanker traffic getting through and the UAE's bypass pipeline 50% complete, inflationary pressures will persist for months. The AI boom appears to be contributing to inflation rather than creating productivity-driven disinflation, while extremely heavy IPO volume (SpaceX, OpenAI) will create additional headwinds. Strong earnings across retailers suggest consumer resilience, but elevated expectations make rallies harder to sustain.

COMPANY NEWS

ADI reported strong Q2 EPS of $3.09 vs. Street $2.92, driven by sales of $3.62B vs. $3.51B expected (though industrial revenue missed slightly). Q3 guidance strong at $3.30 EPS vs. $3.03 Street and $3.9B sales vs. $3.62B expected, though stock fell on sell-the-news reaction.

TGT delivered impressive Q1 EPS upside at $1.71 vs. Street $1.43, with revenue up 6.7% to $24.9B vs. $24.1B expected and operating margins rising ~80 basis points to 4.5%. Full-year guidance raised to sales up ~4% vs. prior ~2% forecast.

TJX posted strong Q1 EPS of $1.19 vs. Street $1.01, with sales of $14.32B vs. $14B expected and aggregate comps +6% vs. Street +4%. Pre-tax margins jumped 170 basis points to 12%. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $5.08-5.15 vs. prior $4.93-5.02, though Q2 guide fell short.

LOW reported Q1 EPS of $3.03 vs. Street $2.96 with comps +0.6% (close to HD's performance) and reiterated full-year guidance despite challenging housing market conditions.

NVDA faces new China restrictions on its RTX 5090D V2 gaming chip designed for export compliance (FT). Company has bankrolled the AI boom with ~$90B in fundraising and deals, often funding companies that buy Nvidia chips (FT).

INTU plans to slash ~17% of workforce as latest tech firm reducing headcount due to AI-linked efficiencies (Reuters).
