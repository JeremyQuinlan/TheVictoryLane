MARKET OUTLOOK

The earnings picture reveals a market grappling with mixed signals — memory stocks like WDC and SNDK are delivering blowout results that justify the extreme bullish sentiment in that space, while software names like TEAM and TWLO are showing surprising strength. Apple's healthy June quarter guidance suggests the mega-cap foundation remains solid, even as consumer discretionary names like RBLX hit significant headwinds from regulatory changes. The AI infrastructure theme continues driving outperformance, though margin pressures from Middle East conflicts are starting to surface across multiple sectors.

GEOPOLITICAL

Middle East conflict escalation is creating both headwinds and opportunities across sectors. FND cited elevated gas prices and declining consumer sentiment from geopolitical tensions, while BIO called out conflict escalation impacting academic end markets. However, EMN expects the Middle East disruption to create net earnings upside in their industry, and MHK is successfully raising prices to offset conflict-related cost pressures.

COMPANY NEWS

AAPL delivered solid FQ2 results with EPS of $2.01 vs. Street $1.96 and revenue up 22% to $111.2B vs. $109.6B expected. The June quarter guidance was particularly strong, calling for 14-17% sales growth vs. Street's +9% forecast. Tim Cook warned that memory cost pressures could intensify beyond the June quarter, though they're managing well for now. The company expanded capital returns with $100B in new buyback authorization.

RBLX posted a disastrous quarter with DAUs falling to 132MM vs. Street 143.8MM and hours engaged dropping to 31B vs. 33.6B expected. Management blamed new age restrictions and the Russia ban, slashing full-year bookings guidance to +8-12% growth from prior +22-26%. The stock suffered major after-hours declines despite management's insistence the safety measures benefit long-term prospects.

WDC reported blowout FQ3 results with EPS of $2.72 vs. Street $2.37, driven by 45% revenue growth to $3.34B and gross margins of 50.5% vs. consensus 47.5%. FQ4 guidance also exceeded expectations with 40% revenue growth vs. Street's 33%. While fundamentally impressive, expectations for memory names remain extremely elevated.

SNDK delivered monstrous Q3 upside with EPS of $23.41 vs. Street $14.51, powered by 251% revenue growth to $5.95B and data center sales surging 645%. Gross margins hit 78.4%, more than 1000 basis points above consensus. FQ4 guidance calls for ~$31.50 EPS vs. Street ~$24.

RDDT showed strong Q1 momentum with EBITDA of $265MM vs. Street $221MM, driven by 69% revenue growth to $663MM as ARPU significantly exceeded expectations. EBITDA margins reached ~40%, more than 300 basis points ahead of estimates.
