MARKET OUTLOOK

The bull case rests on the assumption that US-Iran escalation remains controlled and ultimately leads to some form of détente — but that's a fragile foundation with Brent flirting with $90 and Houthis threatening a naval blockade around Saudi Arabia. The bear case is more compelling right now: oil is a tax, UK fiscal credibility is cracking, open-source Chinese AI models are quietly eroding the revenue base of the capex-mania's core beneficiaries (OpenAI, Anthropic), and the White House is pushing tariffs back toward pre-SCOTUS levels by summer's end. The SPX opened strong and couldn't hold it — that's a telling tape. Equal-weight was underwater before 10am and never recovered, which suggests the weakness is broad, not just headline noise.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US-Iran conflict is "on a knife's edge" with neither side able to afford full escalation but no off-ramp in sight; Sec. of State Rubio says diplomacy remains on the table, and Pakistan/Qatar are reportedly floating a 10-day ceasefire proposal, though nothing appears imminent (NYT).

- Oil volumes through the Strait of Hormuz are running at roughly two-thirds of pre-war levels, per Energy Secretary Wright (Politico). US gas prices have crossed $4/gallon (Reuters).

- Iran's currency hit record lows versus the dollar as economic strain deepens (FT). Iran rejected an MOU heavily skewed in its favor, signaling a higher pain threshold than markets assumed.

- Houthis declared a naval blockade around Saudi Arabia, raising risks for both Saudi oil production and Red Sea shipping.

- Ukraine drone strikes continue hammering Russian energy infrastructure, adding further upward pressure on global oil supply.

- China shipped ~20% fewer rare-earth magnets to the US versus pre-trade-war levels despite the nominal trade truce (Bloomberg). Xi is still planning a state visit to the US, and the White House is not pursuing a probe into alleged Chinese election interference (CNN, Politico).

- China's "national team" funds made sizeable equity purchases to support markets after last week's slump (FT). Chinese Q2 GDP missed the government's target range, prompting discussion of stimulus including accelerated bond issuance (FT).
