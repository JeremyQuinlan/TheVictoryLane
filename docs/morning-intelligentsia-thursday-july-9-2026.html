MARKET OUTLOOK

The tentative bid in equities this morning is Iran-driven relief, and it's fragile — Washington signaled the Wednesday strikes were the last round "for now," but Tehran's internal divisions make any negotiated settlement genuinely hard to execute. The week of July 13 is where the real test comes: bank earnings, CPI, PPI, ASML, TSMC, and Warsh's congressional testimony will collectively set the tape's direction for the back half of July. Bulls should note that the AI capex cycle remains firmly intact (Applied Materials sees "tremendous visibility for the next eight quarters"), while bears will point to softening consumer data and an oil price risk that hasn't fully cleared. Stay nimble — the geopolitical premium isn't priced out yet.

GEOPOLITICAL

- The US conducted a second round of strikes on Iran Wednesday night but signaled it was the final round for now; Trump claimed Tehran "called a little while ago…they want to make a deal" (NYT, Axios). However, Iran's president and foreign minister were physically attacked by hardline faction supporters, underscoring how fractured and difficult-to-negotiate-with the leadership remains (NYT).

- Control over the Strait of Hormuz has become Iran's single biggest strategic priority — more important than the nuclear program — and Tehran has signaled it is willing to fight for dominance of that waterway (Reuters).

- Ukraine has turned the tide against Russia; Trump agreed to allow Kyiv to manufacture Patriot interceptors and endorsed Ukrainian strikes deep into Russian territory (WSJ). Russia is suffering its worst domestic energy crisis since the Soviet collapse and suspended diesel exports Wednesday (FT).

- Washington is preparing a list of Spanish products that could be embargoed, a move that risks a broader rupture with the EU (Politico).

COMPANY NEWS

LEVI (Levi Strauss) posted a solid FQ2 beat: EPS of 28c vs. Street 24c, revenue of $1.562B (+8% headline, +6% organic) vs. Street $1.519B, with operating margins up 70 basis points year-over-year to 9%. DTC (+8% organic), Asia (+12%), and Beyond Yoga (+16%) led; Europe (-1%) and wholesale (+3%) lagged. Full-year guidance raised to organic sales +5.5–6% with EPS $1.46–1.52. Dividend also increased. The guidance hike reflects H1 strength, not H2 optimism — a distinction the market will care about.

COST (Costco) reported June comps of +7% ex-FX and fuel for the total company (vs. +8% in May), with the US at +7.6% (vs. +8.7% in May) — a slight miss versus consensus. A separate WSJ profile highlights Costco's above-industry wages as a deliberate low-turnover strategy.

AZZ reported strong FQ1 results: EPS $1.85 vs. Street $1.69, revenue $449.5MM vs. Street $434.5MM. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $6.75–7.15 from $6.50–7.00.

VG (Venture Global) posted a modest sequential dip in LNG volumes (466.4 TBtu vs. 480.8 in Q1) but a sharp jump in the fixed liquefaction fee ($6.45 vs. $3.82 in Q1).

AMAT (Applied Materials) CEO said chip customers are preparing for years of expansion with "tremendous visibility for the next eight quarters" — a bullish signal for the AI capex cycle (Nikkei).

AZN (AstraZeneca) shares are plunging in London after its Wainua heart disease drug failed to meet its primary trial objective (WSJ).

WBD/PSKY (Warner Bros./Paramount) — US states could sue to block the merger as soon as next week (Reuters); separately, the combined entity will carry nearly $80B in debt (~6.5x EBITDA) with limited remaining cost cuts (WSJ).

NVDA (Nvidia) — China plans to allow select large AI firms (Alibaba, DeepSeek, ByteDance) to purchase fewer than 200K H200 chips for training purposes only, as Beijing wants domestic silicon used for inference (The Information).

GOOGL (Google/Waymo) will go fully autonomous in San Diego, Las Vegas, Tampa, and Denver (Engadget).

BE (Bloom Energy) slumped Wednesday after Hunterbrook published a negative report questioning scandium supply, underlying demand, and the gap between RPOs and backlog.

BRBR (BellRing) appointed Michael Axelrod as President and CEO, effective July 29.

MNST (Monster) announced a 2-for-1 stock split for holders of record July 24; trading on a split-adjusted basis begins August 11.

PLD (Prologis) is pushing Segro's board to engage on its $16.9B takeover bid (WSJ).

CF (CF Industries) raised its quarterly dividend 20% to 60 cents, implying a yield of ~2.05%.

SK Hynix's US ADR offering is more than 7x oversubscribed ahead of Friday's trading debut (Bloomberg, Reuters).
