COMPANY NEWS

AVGO (Broadcom) extended its chip collaboration with Apple through 2031, covering multiple generations of custom ASIC silicon products across Apple's product lineup — a clean long-term revenue anchor for Broadcom.

MSFT (Microsoft) is cutting 4,800 jobs (2.1% of headcount). Management was explicit that the roles are not being replaced by AI directly, but acknowledged AI is reshaping task execution across the firm (The Information).

NVDA (Nvidia) faces a potentially significant setback: the next-gen Kyber NVL144 AI rack system has been delayed to 2028 due to manufacturing difficulties, with the NVL576 also likely slipping. AMD and Google's TPU are the obvious beneficiaries; Asian PCB stocks sold off on the news (CNBC/SemiAnalysis, Bloomberg).

MU (Micron) formalized a long-term Strategic Customer Agreement memory supply deal with Ford — likely already embedded in MU's prior earnings disclosures alongside last week's GM SCA announcement.

META (Meta) is on two fronts: its superintelligence chief says the upcoming "Watermelon" model is on par with OpenAI's GPT-5.5 (Business Insider), and SemiAnalysis argues Meta's pivot toward selling excess compute capacity to third parties would be highly accretive — with 2027 capex described as "shockingly high."

LMT (Lockheed Martin) agreed to acquire Ultra Maritime, a next-gen undersea warfare solutions firm, from Advent for $3.5 billion.

ESI (Element Solutions) will be acquired by SOLS (Solstice) in a cash-and-stock deal valued at $14.5 billion — $10/share cash plus 0.5 SOLS shares, totaling $50.10 per share.

WULF (TeraWulf) signed a 20-year lease with Anthropic at its Hawesville, Kentucky data campus expected to generate ~$19 billion in contracted revenue, and will sell its 50.1% stake in the Abernathy Joint Venture to a Fluidstack-led group.

CXW (CoreCivic) completed the sale of two detention facilities for ~$1.5 billion, anticipating net proceeds of ~$1.1 billion.

KLAR (Klarna) filed for a US banking license, aiming to bring its domestic banking operations in-house after operating as a licensed European bank since 2017.

GFL (GFL Environmental) is weighing a take-private after receiving buyout firm interest (Bloomberg).

ARM CEO says CPU demand is "off the charts" driven by agentic AI — both unit volumes and core counts per chip are rising, a doubly bullish setup for the company (Tae Kim/Substack).
