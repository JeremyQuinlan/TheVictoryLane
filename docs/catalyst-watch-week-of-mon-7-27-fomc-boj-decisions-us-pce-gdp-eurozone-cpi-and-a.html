RATES & FED

The Fed decision lands Wednesday 7/29 at 2pmET, with markets fully pricing in no change — the next live meeting for a hike is September 16. The BOJ also decides Thursday night 7/30, with the market similarly expecting a hold and eyeing either the 9/18 or 10/30 meetings for the next move.

---

MARKET OUTLOOK

This is as loaded a week as you'll see all year — FOMC, BOJ, US PCE, GDP, Eurozone CPI, and a wall of marquee earnings all hitting simultaneously. The market's defensive rotation into Apple as an "AI shelter" tells you something about where conviction is right now. Valuations in semi equipment and data center infrastructure are priced for perfection, which means any stumble in names like KLAC, VRT, or TER will be punished asymmetrically. Energy majors look like a quiet spot of stability given the assumption oil holds above pre-war levels and data center power deals add an emerging growth kicker.

---

GEOPOLITICAL

- Middle East unrest is flagged as a near-term headwind for both luxury demand (particularly LVMH's regional exposure) and cruise bookings at RCL and NCLH, where geopolitical uncertainty compounds the fuel cost pressure (Vital Catalyst Watch, 7/24).

- Chinese EV and component competition continues to weigh on legacy auto OEMs including Ford, with the macro backdrop of stressed consumers, elevated rates, and tariffs adding further drag (Vital Catalyst Watch, 7/24).

COMPANY NEWS

AAPL reports Thursday after close. Street models sales +15.8% to $108.9B (iPhone +20.4% to $53.7B, Services +14.3% to $31.3B), GMs of 48.07%, and EPS +20.3% to $1.89. The nervousness is on margins — spiking memory costs are the culprit, and price hikes are the proposed remedy, though demand destruction risk is real.

AMZN reports Thursday after close. Street looks for revenue +17.3% to $196.8B with AWS FXN growth of 31%, op. income of $23.6B, capex ~$48.6B, and free cash flow near breakeven. Bulls are focused on AWS acceleration and the Trainium chip story; the lack of a proprietary frontier AI model is a relative knock.

MSFT reports Wednesday after close. Street models total revenue of $87.72B, Azure FXN growth ~40%, EPS of $4.24, and capex ~$42.1B. Sentiment has cooled on OpenAI diversification risk and legacy software displacement fears.

META and ARM also report Wednesday after close — no Street estimates provided in this note, but both are top-tier catalysts for AI sentiment.

BA reports Tuesday before open. Street models sales +6% to $24B, EPS of -$0.28, and free cash flow of -$380MM. The narrative is cautiously constructive — supply chain fixes are driving delivery improvements and prompting FAA to lift manufacturing restrictions.

V reports Tuesday after close. Street looks for revenue +12% to $11.38B (Value-Added Services +24.7% to $3.49B) and EPS +8.4% to $3.23. Stablecoin disruption fears have faded, consumer spending holds.

MA reports Thursday before open. Street models revenue +11.5% to $9.068B and EPS of $4.77 — same constructive read as Visa.

VRT reports Wednesday before open. Street looks for sales +28.5% to $3.39B, op. margins up ~300 basis points Y/Y to 21.6%, and EPS +51% to $1.43. Data center capex boom is the engine.

SBUX reports Wednesday after close. Street models comps +5.75% (North America +6%), op. margins +220 basis points Y/Y to 12.33%, and EPS of $0.65. Turnaround confidence is building since the strong FQ2 report in April.

CMG reports Wednesday after close. Comp growth expected at just 1.3% with restaurant margins down ~240 basis points Y/Y and EPS of $0.32, down ~3.5% Y/Y. Rich valuation leaves little room for error.

UPS reports Tuesday before open. Street models EPS of $1.66 and revenue +2.8% to $21.78B, with package volume down mid-single digits offset by average revenue per package up high-single digits. The turnaround thesis is intact but the valuation has caught up.

CVX and XOM both report Friday before open. Street models CVX EPS of $5.54 with free cash flow ~$14.1B; XOM EPS of $3.58 with free cash flow ~$14B. Data center energy deals are the emerging upside optionality.

ETN reports Friday before open. Street looks for total sales +15.6% to $8.12B and EPS of $3.08, with Electrical Americas +14.1% and Electrical Global +27.6%.
