MARKET OUTLOOK

The broader market narrative remains largely unchanged despite a mixed bag of earnings results. While individual company performances show resilience in pockets (particularly among higher-end focused firms like TOL), the underlying macro headwinds from geopolitical tensions and supply chain disruptions continue to create cost pressures across industries. The tech sector's mixed signals—with solid execution from infrastructure plays like KEYS but ongoing AI positioning battles—suggest markets are still sorting through the post-earnings landscape.

COMPANY NEWS

TOL delivered impressive Q2 results with EPS of $2.72 versus Street expectations of $2.59, driven by higher revenue from both unit closings and pricing, plus adjusted gross margins of 26.2% that beat guidance by roughly 70 basis points. The company raised full-year guidance across delivery units, pricing, and margins, benefiting from its focus on wealthier customers who aren't as rate-sensitive amid the challenging housing macro.

CAVA posted solid Q1 upside with EPS of 20c versus Street 17c, fueled by revenue growth of 32.2% to $434.4MM (vs. Street $418MM) and comparable store sales of +9.7% (vs. Street +6%). The company raised full-year guidance, now seeing comps of +5.5% versus prior +4% with EBITDA of $186MM versus prior $180MM.

RRGB surprised with strong Q1 EPS of 13c versus Street break-even expectations, as comparable sales declined only -0.6% versus feared -3.1% while restaurant margins expanded 50 basis points to 14.8%. Full-year 2026 guidance was reiterated.

KEYS delivered robust Q2 EPS upside of $2.87 versus Street $2.32 with a very strong book-to-bill ratio of nearly 1.2x, raising full-year guidance to sales of $1.74B with EPS of $2.46 versus prior outlook of $1.7B and $2.30 respectively.

ADI confirmed plans to acquire privately held AI power management chip firm Empower Semi for $1.5B in cash, following recent media speculation about the deal.

RBLX announced its first-ever stock buyback program worth $3B (9.4% of market cap), with management planning to execute over $1B in the next 12 months following recent share weakness after age verification measures impacted user metrics.

GOOGL made multiple announcements at its I/O conference keynote, though none dramatically shifted the stock narrative as the company continues battling perception that Gemini lags ChatGPT and Claude despite its AI infrastructure advantages.

CE announced price increases for engineered materials products in response to global supply chain disruptions and recent market developments.

JHX reported Q4 EBITDA upside of $381MM versus Street $371MM despite challenging weather and housing affordability pressures, though F27 EBITDA guidance of $1.475B came in slightly light versus Street $1.49B.

VIAV announced plans to raise $500MM through common share sales to repay its $450MM Term Loan B, taking advantage of recent stock price strength.
